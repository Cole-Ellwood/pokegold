## Error Classes Found

- Branch-action remains the central error. Turns 9 and 12 both show that the
  correct branch-beating action can be a support/setup move, not just attack or
  counter-switch.
- Support role recognition improved only partially. I identified Zapdos's
  active threat, but not quickly enough that it could be used as a Whirlwind
  Spikes converter.
- Support-mirror sequencing was weak. Turn 6 showed Toxic before Spikes as the
  route-changing support move in the Cloyster mirror.
- Receiver cycles need better action pricing. Screech, Rock Slide, Fire Blast,
  Earthquake, and Machamp all took turns being the right branch action as
  Snorlax, Exeggutor, Golem, and Zapdos cycled through.

## Reusable Lesson

The hidden-role question helped, but it was too narrow. The branch-beating
action is sometimes not the obvious attack, switch, phaze, or spinblock; it can
be Toxic, Screech, or a setup/support move that changes the receiver's future
board. Before choosing, ask: "What action makes the named receiver worse next
turn?"
