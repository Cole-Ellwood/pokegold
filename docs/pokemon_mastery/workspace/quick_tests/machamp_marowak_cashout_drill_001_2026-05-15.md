## Scenario 1 - Preserve Machamp

Public state: our Machamp is +2 Attack / +2 Defense / -2 Speed at full HP
after Cursing. Opposing Marowak is at 21%, +2 Attack, and can move before
Machamp. Our Snorlax is no longer needed for the endgame; Cloyster is healthy
enough to enter after a sack and Ice Beam. Opponent still has a sleeping
Tyranitar in back.

Frozen answer: switch Snorlax as the controlled sack, then bring Cloyster to
Ice Beam. Machamp's Rock Slide probably wins if it hits and Marowak does not
crit, but Machamp is the cleaner Tyranitar owner and does not need to take the
miss/crit tax when a stable sack -> revenge line exists.

Grade: pass.

## Scenario 2 - Resisted Explosion As Free Entry

Public state: our Cloyster has finished its immediate support/revenge job and
faces a sleeping Tyranitar at full HP. Our own Tyranitar is healthy and has
Earthquake. Machamp is preserved but not required if Tyranitar is put in range.
No revealed Ghost is available.

Frozen answer: Explosion. The resisted damage does not need to KO; it creates
the free entry and puts opposing Tyranitar into own-Tyranitar Earthquake range.
Switching Machamp is acceptable only if Explosion is blocked or the chip no
longer creates the exact follow-up.

Grade: pass.

## Scenario 3 - Exact Removal Beats Overguard

Public state: our boosted Snorlax is +2 Defense and can survive Cloyster's
Explosion. Cloyster is low enough that Double-Edge removes it if it stays; the
opponent has no revealed Ghost. Snorlax still owns the current route if it
stays boosted.

Frozen answer: attack with Snorlax, not automatic Rock-type guard. A guard is
only top if Explosion removes the route or the active hit does not convert.
Here the boosted active survives the cash-out branch and keeps the pressure.

Grade: pass.

## Scenario 4 - Faster Self-KO Action Skip

Public state: opposing Snorlax is faster than our heavily Cursed sleeping
Snorlax and uses Self-Destruct. Our Snorlax survives the hit. We expected to
wake and act afterward.

Frozen answer: do not count the slower wake/action this turn. In GSC, when the
faster self-KO user moves first and the target survives, the slower target does
not move that turn; wake, Sleep Talk, Rest, phaze, or damage must be priced for
the next turn instead.

Grade: pass.
