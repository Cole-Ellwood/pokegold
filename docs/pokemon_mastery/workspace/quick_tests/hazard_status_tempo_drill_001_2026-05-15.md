## Scenario 1 - Preserve Spinner Status Budget

Public state: our Golem faces Skarmory with Spikes on our side. Skarmory has
revealed Toxic. Our Zapdos is already poisoned and can enter without taking
new status. Golem still owns later Rapid Spin and Roar jobs.

Frozen answer: Zapdos. Rapid Spin is not free if it poisons the spinner and
the hazard reset is not immediately decisive. Use the already-statused or
immune absorber, then bring Golem in when Spin converts without donating its
future job.

Grade: pass.

## Scenario 2 - Sleeping Wall Invites Spinner

Public state: Nidoking has put Snorlax to sleep. Spikes are on both sides, and
Starmie has not yet revealed but is the likely spinner branch. Nidoking's
Earthquake strongly punishes Starmie on entry.

Frozen answer: Earthquake. Do not switch to Golem just because Snorlax is
asleep. First price the opponent's reset branch; if Starmie enters, active
Earthquake converts more than a delayed Spin setup.

Grade: pass.

## Scenario 3 - Cloyster Reopens Spikes On Rest

Public state: Skarmory is low and likely to Rest. Our Cloyster is chipped but
can enter and set Spikes. Snorlax can safely chip Skarmory but does not change
the hazard route.

Frozen answer: Cloyster. A safe Snorlax attack is acceptable only if Cloyster
cannot re-enter or the opponent can immediately deny Spikes. The passive Rest
turn is the window to reset the hazard route.

Grade: pass.

## Scenario 4 - Spin When Already Safe

Public state: Golem is already active against a sleeping Snorlax or a statused
opponent that cannot remove it before it acts. Spikes are on our side and the
opponent's spinner is not entering for free this turn.

Frozen answer: Rapid Spin. Once the spinner is already in safely and the
active threat cannot punish or status it first, reset denial converts
immediately.

Grade: pass.
