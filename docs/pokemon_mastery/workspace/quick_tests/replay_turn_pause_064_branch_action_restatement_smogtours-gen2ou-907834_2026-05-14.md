## Error Classes Found

- Branch-action remains unstable under role pressure. Turns 4 and 9 both show
  correct branch naming without the correct handoff action.
- Hazard-loop timing needs a finer gate. Turn 7 shows that blocking Spin too
  early can miss the opponent's reciprocal Spikes turn; turn 8 shows the
  spinblock becomes better after that support job lands.
- Cash-out and preservation are still too swingy. Turn 11 was an over-cash on
  low Zapdos; turn 13 was the opposite overcorrection, switching a sleeping
  RestTalk piece when staying still covered the counter-pivot.
- Sleep is confirmed as a route-state change, not a script. Sleeping pieces are
  often switched out to preserve Sleep Clause value, but RestTalk and board
  coverage can make staying correct.

## Reusable Lesson

Before moving, state the branch and the branch-covering action separately. If
the branch is "Earthquake punishes Gengar," the action cannot be "Gengar
pressure." If the branch is "Forretress may Spin after setting Spikes," the
timing question is whether the current turn is the Spin turn or the reciprocal
Spikes turn. If the branch is "RestTalk Zapdos still covers Ground pivots," the
sleep rule does not force a switch.
