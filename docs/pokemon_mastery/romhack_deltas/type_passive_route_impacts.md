## Core Rule

Do not stop at the type chart. A romhack-facing claim has four layers:

```text
chart result
-> passive-adjusted result
-> final damage or status outcome
-> strategic move label
```

A move can be correct at one layer and wrong at another. Example: a status move
may be legal by chart and still lose value into a Dark defender's first-status
shield. A damaging move may have the expected chart result and still fail an
exact KO because a Dragon, Water, Bug, Ground, Rock, Ice, or Psychic defender
modified the outcome.

## Highest-Impact Move-Choice Checks

### Dark Status Shield

Source behavior:

- Full Dark defender negates the first eligible incoming status attempt.
- Half Dark defender has a 50% chance to negate the first eligible incoming
  status attempt.
- The shield is consumed per active Pokemon after it successfully applies.

Strategic impact:

- Sleep, Toxic, Thunder Wave, Leech Seed, Encore, Disable, Mean Look, phazing,
  and stat-drop plans must price the shield before calling status reliable.
- Against Dark bosses or Dark hybrids, the first status attempt may be a probe,
  not a control plan.
- If the route needs immediate control, prefer damage, pivoting, phazing by a
  non-status route, or a plan that survives the shield branch.

Boss reminders:

- Karen's Dark pieces and Houndoom-style routes.
- Tyranitar, Umbreon, Murkrow, Houndoom, Sneasel, and Poison/Dark hybrids.

### Dragon Passives

Source behavior:

- Dragon attackers convert type-chart no-damage results into reduced damage for
  damaging, non-fixed-damage moves.
- Dragon defenders reduce non-super-effective, non-fixed-damage hits:
  half Dragon by 2/3 and full Dragon by 1/2.
- Outrage is still Dragon type, but for Dragon users it uses the physical
  damage category when current Attack is greater than current Special Attack.

Strategic impact:

- Do not call a pivot safe only because the base chart says the incoming move
  would do no damage. If the attacker is Dragon, check Dragon's Majesty.
- Do not assume neutral-looking chip will push Dragon defenders into range.
  Imperial Scales may turn a 2HKO or revenge range into a miss.
- Dragon Dance users need a category check for Outrage. The move's stat source
  can differ from normal Dragon-type expectations.

Boss reminders:

- Clair, Lance, Red's Charizard/Venusaur sun routes if Dragon answers are being
  preserved, Blue's Gyarados, Steelix, Kingdra, Dragonite, Dragonair, Ampharos,
  Yanma, and other Dragon hybrids.

### Poison Contact Retaliation

Source behavior:

- On a contact damaging hit into a Poison defender, the defender may poison the
  attacker: 10% for half Poison, 20% for full Poison.
- Substitute blocks this contact-retaliation path.
- The source also checks ordinary status constraints such as existing status,
  Poison/Steel user type, poison-prevention item, and Safeguard.

Strategic impact:

- Contact cleanup into Poison bosses is not free when the attacker must remain
  healthy for a later route.
- A low-risk contact move can become a high-risk plan if poison breaks the
  next answer map.
- Substitute or non-contact pressure can rise when the route depends on the
  attacker staying status-free.

Boss reminders:

- Bugsy Ariados, Koga/Janine Poison cores, Qwilfish, Muk, Crobat, Venusaur,
  Weezing, Tentacruel, and Poison hybrids.

### Psychic Mind Shield

Source behavior:

- Half Psychic defender has a 6/256 chance to set incoming damage to 0.
- Full Psychic defender has a 13/256 chance to set incoming damage to 0.
- The hit still proceeds through hit-flow semantics; do not describe this as a
  chart immunity.

Strategic impact:

- Exact-KO plans into Psychic defenders need a catastrophe branch for the
  zero-damage proc if losing that turn is irreversible.
- The proc chance is small, so do not overplay it when the alternative loses
  the main route. It matters most for final-turn KOs, one-use Explosion/trade
  lines, and routes where the attacker dies if the damage becomes 0.

Boss reminders:

- Sabrina, Will, Morty Ghost/Psychic lines, Red Espeon, Starmie/Slowbro/
  Slowking, Xatu, Hypno, Alakazam, Mr. Mime, Jynx, and Psychic hybrids.

## Medium-Impact Clock And Threshold Checks

### Grass Regrowth

- Grass users heal between turns if not statused and not full HP:
  maxHP/64 for half Grass, maxHP/32 for full Grass.
- Status disables the healing.
- Chip plans against Grass anchors need to ask whether poison, paralysis, burn,
  or sleep changes the clock more than raw damage.

### Fire Low-HP Damage Boost

- Fire attackers below one-third HP gain a damage boost:
  11/10 for half Fire, 6/5 for full Fire.
- Do not assume a low-HP Fire boss is harmless. Putting it under the threshold
  can make the next hit more dangerous.

### Ghost Damage Into Statused Defenders

- Ghost attackers do more damage to statused defenders:
  21/20 for half Ghost, 11/10 for full Ghost.
- Statusing your own pivot or letting it stay statused can make Morty/Karen
  Ghost routes more dangerous.

### Electric Speed, Fighting Status Tuning, Flying Accuracy

- Electric type slightly raises Speed: 41/40 half, 21/20 full.
- Fighting type softens paralysis speed loss, full-paralysis chance, and burn
  attack loss.
- Flying type raises final accuracy: 26/25 half, 27/25 full.

These usually matter at thresholds: speed ties, revenge range, paralysis plans,
burn plans, and inaccurate sleep or setup-denial moves.

For the broader public-info speed model, including Choice Scarf, Quick Claw,
DVs, Stat Exp, and boss-AI legality, see `speed_order_public_model.md`.

### Smaller Defensive Damage Reductions

- Ground defender on super-effective hit: 19/20 half, 9/10 full.
- Rock defender on critical hit: 19/20 half, 9/10 full.
- Bug defender versus physical-category hit: 19/20 half, 9/10 full.
- Water defender versus special-category hit: 39/40 half, 19/20 full.
- Ice defender above half HP: 39/40 half, 19/20 full.
- Steel user recoil mitigation: recoil is halved for half Steel and removed
  for full Steel.
- Normal STAB is slightly stronger than vanilla STAB when the user has Normal
  type contribution.

These are often too small to change broad advice, but they absolutely matter
near KO, survival, recoil, or revenge thresholds.

## Before Advising A Romhack Turn

Ask:

1. Which side is using the move, and what are the user's type contributions:
   half or full?
2. Which side is receiving the move, and what are the defender's type
   contributions?
3. Is the claim about chart result, passive-adjusted result, final damage, or
   strategic move label?
4. Does the move make contact, use a fixed-damage effect, use Outrage, apply
   status, create recoil, or rely on exact KO/survival?
5. Does a status plan run into Dark shield, Poison retaliation, Grass regrowth,
   Fighting status tuning, or Ghost-vs-statused damage?
6. If the passive creates a bad-luck branch, is that branch catastrophic or
   just acceptable variance?

## Policy Translation

- Promote the passive to move-deciding when it changes KO/survival range,
  status reliability, speed order, recoil math, safe-entry status, or the next
  route's irreplaceable piece.
- Mention the passive but do not overrule the main route when the branch is
  low-impact and the alternative gives up more.
- Never call a type matchup word sufficient evidence for a romhack move label.
  The label must survive the passive layer and the actual route context.
