# Type Passive Fixture Priorities

Status: planning artifact. These fixture priorities are source-grounded from
trainer rosters and type-passive source, but the listed battle-state fixtures
have not been run in emulator/debugger traces yet.

Purpose: decide which type-passive interactions should be validated first
before treating boss-sim results or turn advice as reliable.

Source basis:

- Passive behavior:
  `docs/pokemon_mastery/romhack_deltas/type_passive_route_impacts.md`
- Trainer rosters: `data/trainers/parties.asm`
- Passive contributions: `audit/type_passives.csv`, generated by
  `python tools/audit/dump_type_passives.py`

## Priority Rule

Validate passives first when all three are true:

```text
1. The passive appears on multiple boss rosters or a late-game boss.
2. The passive can flip a move label, not only a damage footnote.
3. The passive affects common advice words: safe pivot, status control,
   exact KO, revenge range, setup denial, or sacrifice trade.
```

## P0 Fixtures

These should be validated before relying heavily on boss-sim win rate.

### Dark Status Shield

Why P0:

- It can flip "click sleep/status now" into "status is only a probe."
- It affects immediate-control plans, not just damage ranges.
- It appears on important bosses: Karen, Koga, Will, Blue, and any Tyranitar,
  Umbreon, Murkrow, Houndoom, Sneasel, or Poison/Dark hybrid line.

Fixture families:

```text
dark_shield_full_umbreon_status:
  defender: Umbreon
  move classes: sleep, Toxic, Thunder Wave, Leech Seed, Encore, phaze/status
  expected question: does full Dark always negate the first eligible attempt,
  consume shield, and allow the second eligible attempt if other conditions
  permit?

dark_shield_half_houndoom_status:
  defender: Houndoom or Tyranitar
  expected question: does half Dark produce the 50% branch and consume only
  when the shield applies?

dark_shield_ineligible_move:
  defender: any Dark
  move classes: damaging move, already-blocked status, target already statused
  expected question: does the shield remain unspent when the status would not
  matter anyway?
```

Advice this protects:

- Karen: do not assume Hypnosis/Toxic/Confuse Ray style control is reliable
  into Dark pieces before pricing the shield.
- Will: Houndoom can make status-first answers unreliable.
- Blue: Arcanine is half Dark/Fire, so control plans into it need the shield
  branch priced.

### Dragon Passives And Outrage Category

Why P0:

- Dragon passives affect both "safe pivot" and exact damage labels.
- Lance and Clair are full route exams around Dragon and Outrage sequencing.
- Blue Gyarados and Steelix, Red routes, Ampharos, Kingdra, Dragonite, and
  Dragonair make this common in late-game advice.

Fixture families:

```text
dragons_majesty_no_damage_to_reduced_damage:
  attacker: Dragon user with damaging non-fixed-damage move
  target: chart no-damage target
  expected question: does no-damage become reduced damage, and are fixed-damage
  effects excluded?

imperial_scales_near_ko:
  defender: Dragonair / Kingdra / Dragonite / Steelix / Gyarados
  move class: non-super-effective damaging hit near KO range
  expected question: does the reduction flip 2HKO, revenge range, or sacrifice
  legality?

outrage_category_dragon_user:
  attacker: Dragon user with Attack greater than Special Attack, then with a
  tie or lower Attack
  expected question: does Outrage use physical only in the first case, and do
  Counter/Mirror Coat and item/category passives follow that category?
```

Advice this protects:

- Clair/Lance: do not call an anti-Dragon pivot safe or an Outrage answer
  durable without passive and category checks.
- Blue: Gyarados and Arcanine/Dragon coverage can make route handoffs depend
  on exact Dragon interactions.

### Poison Contact Retaliation

Why P0:

- It can flip contact cleanup from safe to route-breaking poison risk.
- It appears early and late: Bugsy Ariados, Koga, Janine, Venusaur, Tentacruel,
  Crobat, Muk, Qwilfish, Weezing, and Poison hybrids.
- It affects preservation of the user's key attacker, not only the defender's
  survival.

Fixture families:

```text
poison_retaliation_contact:
  defender: Muk or Ariados
  attacker: status-free non-Poison / non-Steel contact user
  expected question: does contact damaging hit trigger the 10% or 20% branch?

poison_retaliation_substitute_block:
  defender: Poison behind Substitute
  expected question: does contact into Substitute skip retaliation?

poison_retaliation_status_constraints:
  attacker: already statused, Poison, Steel, Safeguarded, or poison-preventing
  item holder
  expected question: are ordinary poison constraints respected?
```

Advice this protects:

- Bugsy/Koga/Janine: contact-first plans should name whether poison ruins the
  later Scyther/Muk/Qwilfish answer map.
- Red: Venusaur contact branches matter if the contact user must also handle
  sun or Snorlax routes later.

### Psychic Mind Shield

Why P0:

- It can invalidate exact-KO, Explosion, and final-turn lines.
- It appears on Sabrina, Will, Red Espeon, Starmie/Slowbro/Slowking, Morty
  Ghost/Psychic lines, Xatu, Hypno, Alakazam, Jynx, and Mr. Mime.
- The proc is low probability, but the loss branch can be irreversible.

Fixture families:

```text
mind_shield_zero_damage_hit_flow:
  defender: Alakazam or Espeon
  move class: damaging move with secondary/contact/recoil implications
  expected question: when damage becomes 0, what hit-flow effects still occur?

mind_shield_explosion_trade:
  defender: Psychic type
  move class: Explosion / Selfdestruct
  expected question: can the one-use trade fail to remove the target, and what
  happens to the user?

mind_shield_final_ko_branch:
  defender: low-HP Psychic type
  expected question: does the zero-damage branch require a catastrophe note in
  endgame advice?
```

Advice this protects:

- Sabrina/Will/Red: exact KO or one-use trade recommendations into Psychic
  pieces need the zero-damage branch classified as acceptable variance or
  unacceptable if avoidable.

## P1 Fixtures

These can flip advice in specific route families and should follow the P0 set.

## P1 Implementation Order

After the P0 fixtures, run these in this order. The order is based on how often
the passive can change the move class in boss advice, not on how large the
numeric modifier looks in isolation.

```text
P1A-001 fire_low_hp_boost_threshold
P1A-002 electric_speed_turn_order
P1B-001 fighting_status_tuning
P1B-002 grass_regrowth_timing
P1C-001 ghost_vs_statused_damage
```

### P1A-001 Fire Low-HP Boost Threshold

Why first after P0:

- Fire appears on late and final route pieces: Blaine's Ninetales, Rapidash,
  Arcanine, and Magmar; Will and Karen Houndoom; Blue Arcanine; Red Charizard.
- It can invert a common boss-advice shortcut: "chip the Fire attacker low,
  then pivot safely." In this hack, crossing below one-third HP can make the
  next Fire hit stronger.
- It stacks conceptually with sun, Charcoal, Life Orb/recoil, priority range,
  and "safe Water answer" assumptions.

Fixture:

```text
fire_low_hp_boost_threshold:
  attacker: full-Fire and half-Fire users in separate subcases
  HP states: just above one-third, exactly at boundary if representable, just
    below one-third
  move class: Fire damaging move into a neutral target
  assertions: damage boost applies only below threshold; half/full multipliers
    match source; no boost applies above threshold; final damage rounding is
    captured
```

Executable status:

```text
full-Fire below-one-third smoke:
  status: debugger-verified
  scenario: tools/damage_debugger/clobber_smoke.py::special_fire_low_hp
  oracle: tools/damage_debugger/oracle.py::special_fire_low_hp
  result: Cyndaquil Ember vs neutral Pidgey is 15 damage, while normal-HP
    Cyndaquil Ember remains 13 in special_no_items.
  verified by:
    python -m tools.damage_debugger.oracle
    python -m tools.damage_debugger.clobber_smoke

remaining subcases:
  - half-Fire attacker multiplier.
  - just-above and exact-boundary HP cases.
  - boss-like weather/item/recoil stacks.
```

Advice this protects:

- Blaine/Blue/Red/Karen/Will: do not call a low-HP Fire attacker harmless or
  route-complete until the next Fire hit is re-scored under passive, weather,
  item, and recoil state.

### P1A-002 Electric Speed Turn Order

Why second after P0:

- Electric appears in Lt. Surge's whole boss identity, Misty Lanturn, Red
  Pikachu, Lance Ampharos, Zapdos/Raikou-style route examples, and multiple
  player answers.
- Speed order changes move class directly: attack now, switch, revenge, use
  status, accept priority range, or abandon a cleaner.
- It interacts with paralysis, Choice Scarf, Agility, Dragon Dance, Quick Claw,
  and priority. A source-only speed note is not enough for turn-order proof.

Fixture:

```text
electric_speed_turn_order:
  actors: half-Electric and full-Electric users around a known Speed boundary
  controls: same base Speed without Electric contribution, then with Electric
  contribution
  modifiers: clean state first, then paralysis / Agility / Choice Scarf as
    separate boundary subcases
  assertions: effective Speed bump changes or does not change order exactly as
    source predicts; tie behavior remains a tie only when values still match
```

Advice this protects:

- Lt. Surge/Misty/Red/Lance: do not promise a revenge kill, sleep/status move,
  or safe pivot unless the Electric passive and all speed modifiers have been
  included.

### P1B-001 Fighting Status Tuning

Why next:

- Fighting-heavy bosses Chuck and Bruno specifically test whether status is
  enough to contain attackers.
- This passive can make paralysis and burn less decisive, which changes whether
  the right line is status, damage, phazing/Haze, or preservation.
- It is less universal than Fire/Electric, but it directly affects the user's
  common "slow or weaken the Fighting boss" advice.

Fixture:

```text
fighting_status_tuning:
  defender/user: full-Fighting and half-Fighting users
  status states: paralysis and burn
  assertions: full-paralysis chance, paralysis Speed penalty, and burn Attack
    penalty match local source; turn-order and damage implications are
    captured in at least one boss-like threshold
```

Advice this protects:

- Chuck/Bruno/Lt. Surge Raichu/Electabuzz/Feraligatr/Poliwrath: do not assume
  paralysis or burn has vanilla containment value against Fighting-type route
  pieces.

### P1B-002 Grass Regrowth Timing

Why after Fighting:

- Grass route pieces appear in Erika, Red Venusaur, player Meganium routes, and
  mixed Grass/Poison bosses.
- The healing is small, but long fights are exactly where small end-of-turn
  clocks change KO math, Rest timing, and whether status is worth spending.
- Timing matters more than the prose fact: the fixture must show when the heal
  occurs relative to Leftovers, weather, poison/burn, Leech Seed, and faint
  checks before advice leans on it.

Fixture:

```text
grass_regrowth_timing:
  defender: full-Grass and half-Grass users
  states: damaged/no status, damaged/statused, full HP, with and without
    Leftovers or weather-recovery context where applicable
  assertions: regrowth amount, status suppression, text/timing, and
    end-of-turn ordering are captured
```

Advice this protects:

- Erika/Red and player Grass answers: do not call chip progress real until
  regrowth, Synthesis-family recovery, status, weather, and KO thresholds are
  in the ledger.

### P1C-001 Ghost Damage Into Statused Defenders

Why last in P1:

- It matters for Morty and Karen Gengar lines, but the modifier is usually
  smaller than the route swings above.
- It still deserves a fixture because it can punish a common defensive habit:
  using a poisoned, paralyzed, or burned pivot as the "safe Ghost answer."

Fixture:

```text
ghost_vs_statused_damage:
  attacker: full-Ghost and half-Ghost users
  defender states: no status and each major status where legal
  assertions: damage boost applies only when the defender is statused; half/full
    multipliers and rounding match source
```

Advice this protects:

- Morty/Karen and any Ghost route: do not preserve a statused pivot as the
  Ghost answer without checking whether the passive moves it into KO or
  Destiny Bond / Curse / Pain Split trouble.

### Fire Low-HP Damage Boost

Bosses: Blaine, Red Charizard, Blue Arcanine, Karen Houndoom, Will Houndoom.

Validate exact threshold behavior around one-third HP and whether putting the
Fire attacker under the threshold changes the next-hit survival plan.

### Grass Regrowth

Bosses: Erika, Red Venusaur, Meganium/Bayleef lines, Jumpluff, Tangela,
Celebi-style hybrids.

Validate end-of-turn timing, status disabling, and whether tiny healing crosses
real KO/recovery thresholds in long games.

### Fighting Status Tuning

Bosses: Chuck, Bruno, Lt. Surge's Raichu/Electabuzz, Heracross, Poliwrath,
Feraligatr.

Validate paralysis speed penalty, full-paralysis chance, and burn attack
penalty changes. This matters for speed-control plans and burn-based
containment.

### Electric Speed Passive

Bosses: Lt. Surge, Misty Lanturn, Red Pikachu, Electrode/Raichu/Electabuzz,
Ampharos.

Validate the speed boost at relevant speed-tie and revenge-kill thresholds.

### Ghost Damage Into Statused Defenders

Bosses: Morty, Karen Gengar, Misdreavus, Gengar/Haunter lines.

Validate whether statusing or leaving status on the intended Ghost pivot changes
survival after Shadow Ball or other Ghost damage.

## P1 Boss-Sim Gate

Before counting boss-sim results for the following route families, require the
matching P1 fixture status to be at least `debugger-verified`:

```text
Fire route / sun route:
  P1A-001 fire_low_hp_boost_threshold

Electric route / speed-control route:
  P1A-002 electric_speed_turn_order

Fighting status-containment route:
  P1B-001 fighting_status_tuning

Grass recovery / chip-conversion route:
  P1B-002 grass_regrowth_timing

Ghost pivot preservation route:
  P1C-001 ghost_vs_statused_damage
```

Until a fixture passes, label advice as:

```text
source-verified but not runtime-fixture-verified
```

This does not ban advice. It prevents a later 50-battle boss-sim win rate from
counting as proof when the fight was decided by an unverified passive.

## P2 Fixtures

These are real but usually threshold-only:

- Ground defender reduction on a super-effective hit.
- Rock defender reduction on a critical hit.
- Bug defender reduction against physical-category hits.
- Water defender reduction against special-category hits.
- Ice defender above-half-HP reduction.
- Steel recoil mitigation.
- Normal STAB enhancement.

Validate these when a boss route sheet or damage-debugger trace shows an exact
KO/survival/recoil threshold depends on them. Do not let them dominate advice
when the route is decided by hazards, status, setup, recovery, or sacrifice
sequencing instead.

## How To Use This In Reviews

When a boss turn depends on a passive, record:

```text
Passive:
Boss and Pokemon:
Route it affects:
Move label before passive check:
Move label after passive check:
Source evidence:
Fixture status: not run / source-only / debugger-verified / emulator-verified
```

If the passive does not change the move label, mention it only when it explains
a damage range or hidden branch. The goal is better advice, not passive trivia.
