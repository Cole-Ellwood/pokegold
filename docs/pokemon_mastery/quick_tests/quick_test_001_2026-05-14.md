## Pre-Oracle Answers

### A1 - P11

Recommendation: make conservative progress.

Reason: an asleep RestTalk user is not a blank turn in GSC; Sleep Talk can still
advance or stabilize, including Rest interactions. Do not treat this as a free
setup turn unless the setup still wins through the Talker acting.

Ranking: conservative progress > force a different exchange > free setup.

Answer-changing information: the target lacks Sleep Talk, Sleep Talk PP is gone,
or the setup move creates an immediate forced win even through the worst Sleep
Talk branch.

### A2 - P12

Recommendation: Body Slam/Thunder paralysis line.

Reason: if the expected defensive answer is the blocker to a later route, speed
control can matter more than immediate damage. The para line is strongest when a
specific later breaker benefits from Skarmory or the answer being slowed.

Ranking: para line > strongest damage > hard double to sweeper.

Answer-changing information: the answer is already chipped into damage
thresholds, Heal Bell will erase paralysis immediately, or the hard double is
nearly forced by public switching incentives.

### A3 - P13

Recommendation: use a move that also pressures Snorlax.

Reason: blind paralysis into an obvious Snorlax switch is low value because
Snorlax can Rest-cycle and often wants to absorb generic status. The move should
still make progress if Snorlax appears.

Ranking: pressure Snorlax > double to Lax punisher > blind paralysis.

Answer-changing information: Snorlax lacks Rest, is already on a timer, or the
double is supported by repeated public route evidence.

### A4 - P14

Recommendation: Toxic on one and Roar on the other.

Reason: two Toxic slots aimed at the same switch-ins are redundant; diversify
the punishment so one slot starts the timer and the other converts forced
switches or hazard pressure.

Ranking: Toxic plus Roar > coverage on one > Toxic on both.

Answer-changing information: the team has no other way to status both targets,
or local team structure makes the switch-ins meaningfully different.

### A5 - P15

Recommendation: protect the cleric and do not Bell automatically.

Reason: Heal Bell is a finite route resource. If live sleep pressure can still
hit the cleric or force worse status allocation, curing immediately may spend
the reset before the important status has landed.

Ranking: protect cleric > accept current status placement > Bell now > sleep
with another mon.

Answer-changing information: current status is already breaking the route,
sleep pressure is gone, or Bell now creates an immediate winning tempo window.

### A6 - P16

Recommendation: hold Bell.

Reason: if the sleeping Pokemon is low-value into the remaining team, curing it
does not necessarily improve a route. Save Bell for a later status pile or a
route-defining piece.

Ranking: hold Bell > Bell later after more status > Bell immediately.

Answer-changing information: the sleeping mon has one specific route job left,
or holding Bell lets the opponent convert immediately.

### A7 - P17

Recommendation: Explosion.

Reason: if Cloyster has drawn Starmie and the offense needs Starmie removed,
the trade can be route conversion rather than generic damage. Surfing keeps the
remover alive; Pursuit support may be too slow if Starmie still controls Spin.

Ranking: Explosion > double to Pursuit > keep Surfing.

Answer-changing information: Cloyster is still the only keep-Spikes body, the
Explosion does not remove Starmie from the relevant HP band, or Pursuit support
is already guaranteed to trap without spending Cloyster.

### A8 - P18

Recommendation: sleep-then-punish.

Reason: the value of Sleep Powder pressure is that it shapes the switch. If the
obvious response is a Sleep Talk Electric, raw sleep may be low value, but the
forced absorber can be punished by Explosion or repositioning once it appears.

Ranking: sleep-then-punish > Explosion > Psychic > raw Sleep Powder.

Answer-changing information: the Electric is already in and is the exact
route-critical target, the opponent lacks a Talker, or landing sleep itself
immediately opens the endgame.

### A9 - P19

Recommendation: chip first.

Reason: Explosion should remove the route blocker, not merely damage it. If the
Raikou KO range is unclear, first create the damage or status threshold that
makes the later boom decisive.

Ranking: chip first > paralyze first > pivot > boom now.

Answer-changing information: Raikou is already in guaranteed Explosion range,
Gengar will not get another chance, or the game is lost unless the immediate
trade is attempted.

### A10 - P20

Recommendation: Explosion.

Reason: when a DrumLax or Growth sweeper is one turn from taking over, the
one-time trade can be the emergency route denial. A shaky wall or bluff line is
too passive if the next setup turn ends the game.

Ranking: Explosion > shaky wall > bluff and attack.

Answer-changing information: Explosion is blocked by a public Ghost/Protect
branch, the wall is actually stable, or the sweeper is not yet at a decisive
conversion point.
