# Policy Card: Branch Action After Naming

Status: active boundary card.

Use when: the opponent is likely to switch, reveal coverage, use a support
piece, or punish the current active matchup.

Trigger:

- You can name a likely receiver, absorber, hard answer, or support branch.
- The tempting move only improves the visible active matchup.
- A revealed move or set detail changes which action beats the branch.
- The active KO or active trade looks obvious, but the opponent can deny it by
  moving a receiver or sack into the line.

Default:

After naming the branch, name the action that beats that branch. Choose
counter-switch, coverage, phaze, Reflect, Toxic, Screech, setup, status,
Explosion, or sacrifice only if that action beats the named receiver or punish.

Substitute/setup extension: if the expected branch is an immunity or hard
receiver, rank Substitute or setup beside coverage and counter-handoff. It can
be the positive-selection action when it keeps the active route while exposing
or punishing the receiver.

Utility-screen/drop extension: if Reflect, Light Screen, Screech, Dynamic
Punch, Toxic, or a similar utility move changes the named receiver map, rank it
as a branch-punish candidate before default damage or switch. The support move
is top only when it creates a concrete follow-up: phaze, Pursuit, Rock Slide,
Explosion, Spin after Ghost coverage, or a safer handoff. If the utility move is
only possible-only, keep the fallback line explicit.

Revealed-package extension: when a move reveal shows a coherent package, re-solve
the whole route before continuing the old plan. Substitute into a RestTalk
target may be passive scouting by itself, but if Nightmare, confusion, Leech
Seed, Growth, Baton Pass, or phazing support is revealed or strongly implied,
rank the package follow-up above generic damage unless a revealed reset, phaze,
faster breaker, or low-cost switch invalidates it.

Revealed-coverage blocker extension: when a support piece reveals coverage that
hits the named blocker or spinblocker, re-rank that coverage before a safe
handoff. The support piece may have become the converter for this board.

Revealed coverage over STAB extension: once Snorlax, Steelix, or another
attacker reveals coverage that hits the named Ghost, Rock, Steel, or spinner
branch, rank that revealed coverage above generic STAB into the immunity or
resist. Do not demote the revealed answer just because the species's default
damage button is stronger into the active target.

Pursuit-punish extension: when a Ghost or Psychic route piece has just blocked
Spin or forced progress, name the Pursuit user that can enter. If coverage,
Dynamic Punch, status, Explosion, or a lower-value switch beats that Pursuit
branch, rank it before a generic retreat.

Pass/Substitute package extension: when Baton Pass, Growth, Substitute,
coverage, sleep, or phaze appears in the same route, name the package before
choosing. Dry Baton Pass to a status threat, Substitute after absorbing the
faster hit, coverage into a low phazer, or a counter-handoff to the receiver can
all beat a generic safe switch if they win the next board.

Growth/recovery package extension: when Espeon, Vaporeon, Jolteon, or another
booster shows Growth, Morning Sun, Baton Pass, or coverage in the same route,
rank the package-denial action before generic Pursuit, chip, or retreat. The
top action must either remove the booster, phaze it, status the package, hit
the revealed coverage branch, or hand off to the owner that survives the next
boosted turn.

Dry-pass reveal extension: if a fast Electric or support piece has shown dry
Baton Pass as a handoff, its next appearance must reopen Agility, Growth,
Substitute, receiver, and receiver-denial lines. Do not collapse Baton Pass into
a generic switch method once the package is public.

Compound Baton Pass extension: when Agility, Acid Armor, Growth, Swords Dance,
Belly Drum, Roar, or Substitute appears with Baton Pass, keep an explicit boost
and receiver ledger until the boosts are phazed, the receiver faints, or the
chain is otherwise reset. Choose the action that beats the receiver board:
phaze, screen, paralysis, damage to put the receiver in revenge range, or a
counter-handoff. Do not continue a sleep/status script after the pass route is
public unless that status hits the receiver or enables the phaze/revenge turn.

Passed-Fighting receiver extension: when Agility-pass or speed support reaches
Machamp or another Fighting breaker, price Cross Chop, Rock Slide, coverage,
setup, and the high-crit branch before deciding Snorlax can stay. A Ghost
handoff is not automatic if it gives the receiver a free setup or coverage
turn; active damage is correct when it puts the receiver into a named revenge
or phaze range. Staying is wrong when the Fighting hit can remove the route
piece before that threshold is achieved.

Revealed-lure coverage extension: when a Pokemon reveals off-standard coverage
into its normal answer, immediately re-rank future turns around that reveal.
Before the reveal, keep the lure in strong-prior or possible-only tiers; after
the reveal, do not demote it because the species's default role points
elsewhere.

Revealed-lure second-turn extension: after a lure move such as Thunder, Fire
Blast, Hidden Power, or coverage into a support answer becomes public, the next
turn is often about the absorber that blanks the lure. Name that absorber class
and the counter-handoff owner before repeating the active lure. Keep the active
move top only if it removes the target now or the absorber branch is weaker.

Counter-handoff extension: when the current active is likely to leave, name the
next board and our owner for that board. If that owner enters safely and
creates immediate progress, the top action is usually the handoff rather than
damage into the Pokemon that is about to leave.

Early-cycle handoff extension: in opening GSC loops after Spikes, Toxic, Rest,
or an obvious forced switch, identify whether the next board belongs to
Snorlax, Forretress/Cloyster, an Electric, a Ghost, Skarmory, or a phazer. The
top action must either move that owner in, hit that owner, or explain why the
active pressure still converts through the expected handoff.

Repeated-cycle extension: once the same defensive cycle repeats twice, treat
the next node as a revealed or strong-prior branch until public evidence breaks
it. Choose the move or handoff that wins that node, not the move that merely
would have been safe on the previous node. In spectator-public work, score a
hidden exact teammate by class, but keep a fallback to a revealed owner.

Ground-breaker receiver triangle extension: when Marowak, Rhydon, Steelix, or
a similar Ground/physical breaker enters on a paralyzed Electric or forced
Normal-resist loop, name the receiver before clicking STAB. Skarmory and
Forretress ask for Fire coverage or a handoff if Fire is only possible-only;
Starmie, Suicune, Vaporeon, and other Waters ask for Earthquake, setup, or a
preservation switch; Ghosts ask for Earthquake, Rock Slide, status, or a
Pursuit/handoff. Do not let Earthquake stay top into a named Skarmory branch.

Status-absorber extension: when the named branch is a sleep or status absorber,
do not keep the status move on top by habit. Re-rank cash-out, coverage,
counter-switch, setup, or Substitute into that absorber before choosing the
original status move.

Status-into-switch extension: if the visible active is already poisoned,
immune, or likely to leave, status can still be the branch-punish when the
named switch target can be statused and that status changes the route more
than coverage into the current active. Choose the status only after naming the
incoming target and fallback if it stays.

Status-tolerant receiver extension: when the opposing support move is Toxic,
Thunder Wave, or sleep, include status-tolerant receivers such as Snorlax,
Ground-types, already statused pieces, or RestTalk users alongside type
immunities. The correct handoff is the one that preserves or creates the next
converter, not always the one that blocks Electric damage.

Setup-into-receiver extension: when an active physical converter forces a
Starmie, Zapdos, Snorlax, Ghost, or phazer receiver, rank Curse/setup beside
coverage and immediate damage. Setup is the branch-punish only if it changes
the receiver board before the receiver can recover, status, phaze, or KO.

CurseLax coverage-receiver extension: after Snorlax reveals Curse, solve the
receiver map before choosing another setup turn. Compare coverage into the
named Ghost, Cloyster, Skarmory/phazer, Rock/Steel, or sleeping RestTalk branch
against another Curse, Rest, and handoff. Revealed or side-known coverage can
anchor the top line; strong-prior coverage must name the fallback if absent;
possible-only coverage stays a branch unless the answer is explicitly a
high-risk read.

Boosted-timer conversion extension: after a Snorlax-like converter has already
banked boosts and poison, Toxic, recoil, Perish, or phaze pressure starts the
clock, re-rank attack, Rest, more setup, and handoff by the next two turns.
Strong-prior Rest is not automatically top. If revealed or standard STAB now
chunks the receiver, forces a Rest/phaze/explosion decision, or preserves the
boosted route before the timer steals it, rank the cash-out attack before
generic preservation.

CurseLax Rest-clock extension: when CurseLax faces revealed RestTalk Electric
or special pressure, compare Double-Edge, Rest, another Curse, and handoff
against the next Thunder/Sleep Talk/wake turn. Curse is top only if the extra
boost changes the post-Rest or receiver board; Rest is top when the next hit
would remove the boosted route; attack is top when it forces the irreversible
trade before the clock.

Sleep-clause absorber extension: when the opponent already has a sleeping
RestTalk or otherwise useful sleeping piece, price the switch that brings it
back in to blank further sleep attempts and preserve sleep-clause value. Choose
status only if it still improves through that absorber.

RestTalk receiver re-entry extension: after Rest or Sleep Talk is public, a
sleeping Electric, Snorlax, bulky Water, or other RestTalk user can be the
named receiver even while asleep. Price the switch, Sleep Talk roll, and wake
clock as revealed route facts, not as possible-only hidden-team anchors.

Cash-out immunity guard: before Explosion, self-sacrifice, or an all-in trade,
name revealed immunities and the legal hidden immunity class that would blank
the trade in no-Team-Preview play. If the trade spends an irreplaceable piece
and loses to a plausible immunity branch, prefer pressure, setup, phaze,
coverage, or a handoff unless the line is explicitly marked as a high-risk
read.

Handoff boundary: before switching, compare active pressure, coverage/status/
phaze/setup into the expected branch, and the handoff. If the current move
already affects the next board after switch cost, Spikes damage, recoil, and
information loss are priced, it can beat the handoff.

Opposite boundary:

Do not overread every possible switch. If the active punish is still the best
branch, or if the receiver takes route-changing damage from the active move,
keep pressure instead of making a speculative double.

Exceptions:

- Spectator-public hidden bench may make the exact counter-switch unknowable;
  score by class when the route action is correct.
- Setup is correct when the expected receiver cannot reset the route or when
  the boost changes the next board more than meeting the receiver now.
- If a set reveal changes the role, re-solve instead of importing the standard
  species job.
- If the active move already improves through the expected receiver, do not
  hand off just to look predictive.
- If the package spends the user's own HP or tempo and the opponent can break
  it without losing route equity, treat the package as self-tax rather than
  automatic progress.

Worst branch:

You correctly say "they switch to the answer," then choose a move that only
beats the Pokemon currently on screen and hand the opponent the board.

Local status:

claims need local evidence when decision-relevant.

Drill:

Give four positions where the same visible active matchup has different correct
actions: immediate active punish, counter-switch to receiver, coverage into
receiver, setup because the receiver cannot reset the route, and package
follow-up because the set reveal changes the route.
