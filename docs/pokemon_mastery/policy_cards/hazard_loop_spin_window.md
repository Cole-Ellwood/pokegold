# Policy Card: Hazard Loop Spin Window

Status: active boundary card.

Use when: Spikes, Rapid Spin, spinblocking, or post-support handoff determines
whether hazard progress is real.

Trigger:

- Spikes can be set, retained, removed, or converted.
- Rapid Spin or a spinner entry path is live.
- The current move looks like generic progress but may create a Spin window.

Default:

Treat Spikes as a route subgame. Ask whether the line sets, retains, removes,
punishes Spin, or converts the entry map. Hazard progress is not real if the
opponent can remove it without giving up a route-relevant cost.

Hazard job ordering: before choosing Rapid Spin, ask whether the hazard piece
can set missing Spikes on the opponent's side right now. If one side is
unspiked and the setter is already active, setting the missing Spikes may beat
clearing owned-side Spikes.

Tempo coverage before layer: if the support piece can immediately remove,
slow, or force out the active threat while surviving, coverage such as Surf,
Icy Wind, Hidden Power, or Toxic can beat the first Spikes layer. The layer is
top when it converts before the support piece is forced out or killed.

Cloyster mirror branch extension: before Cloyster clicks Rapid Spin into
Skarmory, opposing Cloyster, or another passive hazard-cycle piece, name the
opponent's next owner. If opposing Cloyster enters, Surf, Toxic, Icy Wind, or
Explosion can convert more than Spin because they damage the setter/spinner
that keeps the loop alive. If the owner is a flyer, Electric, Ghost, or phazer,
rank the counter-handoff, coverage, status, or Spin by which action beats that
owner, not by the field job alone.

Spin side-ownership gate: before recommending Rapid Spin, explicitly name
whose side has Spikes. If the spinner's own side is clean, Spin is not hazard
progress and should fall below pressure, status, Recover, coverage, or handoff
unless the move's chip effect itself matters.

Setup-threat boundary: if a boosted or immediately boosting Pokemon is already
converting, status, phazing, or cash-out may outrank both setting missing
Spikes and clearing owned-side Spikes.

Revealed phaze-loop commitment: once Whirlwind or Roar is public and Spikes is
already converting, keep the phaze loop ranked until the opponent shows a
public branch that removes hazards, forces the phazer out, wins a Rest race, or
opens a better converter. Do not drop the loop for generic chip or a safe switch
without naming what breaks the loop.

Post-Spin reset check: after a successful Rapid Spin, immediately ask whether
the opposing setter is active, alive, and free to reset Spikes. Clearing hazards
is not stable progress if the setter can restore them before paying a real
route cost.

Post-Spin Recover check: after Starmie or another recovering spinner clears
Spikes and survives the shown hit, ask what stops the Recover loop. Repeating
weak damage is not conversion if the spinner can heal through it; rank Electric
pressure, Toxic/status, Pursuit/trapping, Explosion/cash-out, or a double-switch
to the support receiver before repeating the same attack.

SubSpin shield extension: when Starmie or another spinner reveals Substitute
before Rapid Spin, treat Substitute as a status shield and spin enabler, not
just HP loss. Against a faster SubSpin Starmie, Toxic, sleep, and Explosion may
hit the barrier instead of the real spinner, and an immediate Spikes reset can
hand over a protected Spin cycle. Rank pressure handoff, breaking the
Substitute, or named spinner cash-out before repeating support.

SubStarmie pressure extension: Substitute Starmie may be a Surf pressure route
even when Spin is not immediate. If it is behind a Substitute against Thunder,
price Thunder accuracy, shield break, Surf chip, Recover, and Rest-forcing
before assuming the Electric answer already owns the board.

Spinblock coverage extension: when Gengar or another spinblocker enters the
hazard loop and the setter has revealed coverage that damages it meaningfully,
price active coverage before pivoting or trying Spin. The spinblocker is not
stable if the current board can force it low or out.

Rock spinner package extension: Golem can be a Normal-resistant phazer,
Explosion threat, and Rapid Spin user at the same time. When it enters after
hazards are up, price Spin, Roar, Explosion, and Earthquake together before
calling it only a Ground or boom branch.

Tentacruel spinner package extension: Tentacruel can reveal Rapid Spin without
being a generic setup Water. After Spin is public, rank Surf, Protect scouting,
switch, and Spin as one package. Protect can buy Leftovers and reveal the
spinblock response before the next Spin or handoff.

Spinblock-to-Pursuit chain: after a Ghost blocks Spin, immediately ask whether
Tyranitar, Umbreon, or another Pursuit user can enter. If the spinblocker has
revealed coverage or Dynamic Punch into that Pursuit user, rank the punish; if
not, preserve or sack with the Pursuit tax explicit.

Forretress compression extension: Forretress is primarily a Spikes piece, but
it also compresses Normal resistance, Rapid Spin, and defensive Explosion.
When it enters a Snorlax or Zapdos route, compare first-layer Spikes against
the special handoff, the Normal-resist job, and the emergency trade. Do not
replace Forretress with a generic Ghost or Skarmory branch without naming what
Forretress uniquely converts.

Forretress move-tier extension: after Forretress reveals some of Spikes, Toxic,
Rapid Spin, Hidden Power, Giga Drain, Rest, or Explosion, keep the remaining
slots tiered as revealed, strong-prior, or possible-only. Explosion is common
and can be the emergency route-denial move, but Hidden Power/coverage chip,
Spin, Spikes, Toxic into a switch, or a handoff may be the converting action.
Do not make an unrevealed Explosion the main line unless the boosted threat,
target, post-trade owner, and fallback if Explosion is absent are explicit.

Steelix phaze/setup split: with Spikes down and Roar revealed, Steelix can
convert by repeatedly phazing expected counters. Keep Roar top while the
switch loop is stable. If the opponent's loop-breaker is a spinner, Skarmory,
Forretress, or another piece that lets Steelix set up or forces a different
resource trade, rank Curse, coverage, or handoff before autopiloting Roar.
Curse can be correct before Roar when Steelix has just forced Raikou,
Tyranitar, or another target that is likely to leave and the receiver cannot
immediately remove Steelix. After the receiver reveals coverage that hits
Steelix, price the damage clock before choosing another boost; Roar or handoff
beats Curse if the coverage will stop the phaze from resolving.

Opposite boundary:

Do not overprotect Spikes when the route has shifted. If the opponent must spin
through a losing trade, or if direct cash-out opens the converter now, take the
conversion.

Exceptions:

- A spinner without an entry path is not an immediate Spin threat.
- A spinblocker without a safe entry is not real coverage.
- If the spinner is already status-controlled and the setter still has a
  future entry/job, hand off to pressure before resetting or spending by habit.
- If the setter is already active after Spin, resetting Spikes may be the best
  immediate job even while status-controlled.
- If the setter is already active before Spin and the opponent's side is
  unspiked, setting Spikes may be the best immediate job before any clear.
- If a setup threat is already live, stop the route first; hazard work is only
  top if the threat cannot convert through it.
- If a support piece has just done its job, identify the counter-pivot before
  assuming the hazard loop is stable.

Worst branch:

You call setting Spikes progress, give the opponent a free spinner sequence,
and lose both hazard pressure and tempo.

Local status:

Vanilla GSC has one Spikes layer. In this romhack, Spikes layer count and the
successful Rapid Spin hazard-clear command clearing all layers are
`runtime_verified` in the local mechanics index. Exact text, PP, full switch-in
damage timing, Rapid Spin failure boundaries, and same-turn interactions remain
`unknown` unless the current answer cites a fixture or trace.
