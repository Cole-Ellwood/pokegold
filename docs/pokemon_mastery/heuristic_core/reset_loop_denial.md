# Heuristic: Reset Loop Denial

Status: live tiny card.

Use when: Spikes, Rapid Spin, Rest, Recover, Heal Bell, phazing, Sleep Talk,
Substitute, or Baton Pass may reset the route.

Rule: progress counts only if the opponent cannot reset it for free.

Fast tiebreaker: if Spikes, Rest, phaze, status, or the correct-owner handoff
already created pressure, chip/status/coverage is top only when it forces the
next choice before the route piece becomes scarce. Otherwise rank Rest,
phaze-on-sleep, staying with the absorber, or the lower-cost handoff first.

Ask:
- Who resets: spinner, setter, Recover user, RestTalker, phazer, passer?
- What denies the loop: pressure, Toxic, Pursuit, phaze, cash-out, handoff?
- With Spikes down, does Roar or Whirlwind convert better than direct damage?
- After a Normal resist absorbs pressure, does phazing now deny the Water,
  spinner, or special counter-owner better than switching away?
- Does the current move convert before the reset happens?
- Before Rapid Spin, who denies it: Gengar/Misdreavus, pressure owner,
  Explosion, or a switch that makes Spin lose tempo?
- Before using a sleep/passive turn to bring in your spinner, does their
  spinner or status user enter and get punished harder by the active move?
- After sleep or status lands, re-solve hazard tempo before default Spin or
  damage: who resets Spikes, who spins, and what preserves the finite job?
- Does Spin convert now, or does it spend the spinner's status/HP budget when
  a statused or immune absorber preserves the reset piece?
- When Starmie is the spinner and Spikes are already up, does Rapid Spin cover
  the switch branch better than exact damage into the active spiker?
- If a doomed Spiker can place Spikes before dying, does the hazard create more
  route pressure than preserving a piece with no remaining entry path?
- Before phazing, does the phazer survive the hit or Self-Destruct that moves
  before negative-priority Roar/Whirlwind?
- After a boosted target Rests, does phaze, pressure, or force-out convert the
  sleep window before another boost repeats unstable progress?
- If a low target is likely to Rest before negative-priority phaze, Roar or
  Whirlwind still converts after Rest when the phazer survives the turn.
- In a setup-versus-Rest race, compare the two-turn clock: boost now then
  attack next, or attack now then let Rest reset. Chip counts only if it
  changes the next forced choice.
- Against RestTalk, mark Rest-called-by-Sleep-Talk as a fresh Rest clock. On
  the wake-and-act turn, re-sleep, status, phaze, cash-out, or KO can outrank
  generic damage that lets the reset loop continue.
- Against RestTalk plus a boosted passer, price the attacking Sleep Talk rolls
  as well as Rest. If the roll removes the passer before damage converts, pass
  to a receiver or phaze instead of feeding the loop.
- In a Toxic-versus-Recover loop, does poison already outpace recovery so
  setup, preservation, or handoff converts while the target is forced to heal?
- If Toxic is the clock, can the target switch out and reset or downgrade the
  ramp before it is forced to Recover?
- After poisoning a Recover user, who immediately forces Recover or punishes
  the switch: damage owner, phazer, Pursuit, setup, or handoff?
- After poison, Spikes, or phaze pressure already forced the branch, does Rest,
  Protect, or a lower-cost handoff preserve the route piece better than chip?
- If residual poison will KO the faster active target after its action, do not
  rely on the slower Pokemon's chosen support move happening afterward.
- In GSC, if a faster Explosion or Self-Destruct user moves first and faints,
  a slower surviving target does not act that turn. Do not bank wake, Rest,
  Sleep Talk, phaze, support, or damage after the self-KO.

Top move denies or exploits the reset loop, not the move that repeats unstable
progress.

Do not treat cleared hazards, chip, or a sleeping target as stable by default.
Do not attack a Rest-forced target just because damage is safe when phaze,
Pursuit, Toxic, setup, or a handoff converts the sleep window now.
Do not attack into Recover just because damage is available when the poison
clock already denies the reset and setup improves the next board.
Do not chase one more chip when the public clock has already forced Rest or a
switch and the current RestTalker, spinblocker, spinner, or phazer must remain
usable. In those loops, Rest, Protect, or the exact handoff can be the
converter.
Do not count bad poison as stored progress by itself. In GSC, switching can
reset or downgrade the ramping clock, so Toxic only owns the route when the
target is held active, forced to Recover, or punished on the switch.
