# Heuristic: Spend Or Save Piece

Status: live tiny card.

Use when: a low support piece, Explosion user, sleeper, spinner, setter, or
sack candidate may be preserved or spent.

Rule: name the remaining job before spending or saving.

Ask:
- Is the job still live: hazards, Spin, phaze, sleep absorb, pivot, check?
- Is the job complete or now irrelevant?
- What converter opens if this piece attacks, sacks, explodes, or hands off?
- Does a reversible move or handoff cover the same active target and named
  branch while keeping this irreversible converter available for the next
  forced choice?
- Before Explosion or Self-Destruct, what board exists afterward: target
  removed or chipped, target action denied, free-entry owner, exact range
  created, future job lost, and preserving line available?
- If hazards kill this piece on entry, does the controlled sack cancel their
  action and create clean entry for the named owner?
- Against a low revealed sleeper or self-KO candidate, which lower-value
  Normal resist, Ghost, or sack preserves the route piece?
- If revealed Pursuit/trap damage punishes escape, what HP and job remains?
- If a low defensive converter attacks, does it KO or change the next forced
  choice before the return hit removes its job?
- Before preserving a pressured piece, does its current attack, phaze, or
  setup force Rest, deny a reset, or win the next branch this turn?
- If the piece is a RestTalker, spinblocker, spinner, or phazer in a Spikes
  loop, does extra chip actually change the next forced choice before its job
  is spent?
- If setup spends this piece's HP/tempo, does it survive revealed or
  strong-prior coverage that targets its role?
- Does exact removal actually happen before self-KO after priority, speed,
  paralysis, and move order are priced?
- Before raw Explosion or Self-Destruct from a threatened piece, does it move
  first or survive the revealed/strong-prior hit? If not, boom is a read.
- In a mapped endgame or explosion-stack route, does preserving the lowest-
  future-job exploder serve a named job, or only generic insurance?
- If a needed converter attacks through miss/crit exposure, does controlled
  sack -> stable revenge remove the same threat while preserving the converter?
- If a boost can be passed, does the current boosted Pokemon die to a revealed
  or strong-prior immediate roll before the damage route converts?
- If a support piece is dying anyway, can it finish its job first with Spikes,
  status, phaze, Spin, or boom before the hit lands?
- Will residual damage end the active target before this slower support piece
  can spend its move?

Preserve if the job still owns a future route. Spend if the target, absorber,
post-trade owner, and lost job are all named.
For Growth/Baton Pass, the boost owner is a route piece. Pass when the receiver
survives the public or strong-prior roll and keeps the boost live; attack only
when the attack converts before the roll can remove the passer.

Cash-out is not a reward for naming Explosion. It becomes top only after the
support job is delivered, irrelevant, or impossible to deliver in time.
Explosion can convert without a KO when chip plus the free entry creates a
named exact-range owner; still name the lost job, immunity/resist branches, and
post-trade owner before spending.
If ordinary damage, setup, status, phaze, or a handoff covers both the active
target and the named branch while preserving Explosion, Self-Destruct, Baton
Pass, or another irreversible converter, rank the reversible line first. Spend
now only when delay lets the target reset, escape, remove the user, or
invalidate the route.
Resisted or non-KO self-KO can still convert when chip plus the GSC action skip
plus free entry creates a named owner or exact range that the preserving line
cannot create before reset, phaze, escape, or removal. If the post-boom board is
not better than the best preserving line, keep the converter.
In mapped endgames, do not preserve an Explosion user as vague insurance. If
the named blocker and post-trade owner are known, trade down when it simplifies
the remaining board.
The defensive mirror is mandatory: when their low Snorlax/Cloyster/Exeggutor
can cash out after delivering sleep, hazards, or status, preserve the valuable
RestTalker, special wall, or win piece unless its current move converts before
Self-Destruct/Explosion.
Exact removal beats fear. If the active move KOs the low blocker before it can
phaze, reset, or trade, do not switch to a guard just because self-KO is legal.
But exact removal is not exact if status or speed lets the self-KO move first.
Likewise, your own self-KO cash-out is not live if a revealed faster attack
removes the user before it can move; preserve or hand off unless the miss/switch
read and fallback are named.
After chip into a low support or self-KO user, re-run preserve / coverage /
cash-out / defensive-sack immediately; last turn's correct Surf or attack may
have been the bluff before the trade.

Do not protect a piece with no entry path or cash out into an unpriced immunity.
Do not click obvious coverage from a low route piece if Recover, handoff, or
preservation is what keeps the check alive.
Do not spend a low RestTalker, spinblocker, spinner, or phazer on chip after
poison, Spikes, or Rest pressure has already created the route. Preserve with
Rest, Protect, or handoff unless the attack changes the next forced choice.
Do not preserve by reflex when the current piece can convert before the
handoff and still leaves a named owner afterward.
Do not call Pursuit/trap escape preservation unless the named job survives.
Do not spend a needed converter into miss/crit exposure when a lower-value sack
plus stable revenge produces the same removal and keeps the converter for the
next route.
Do not overguard a support piece whose key job is still unfinished if it can
complete that job before dying. After the job is complete, re-run the cash-out
guard normally.
Do not count a slow support spend if poison or other residual will remove the
target before the support move executes.
Do not set up with a route piece into strong-prior coverage that removes it
unless the line is explicitly a high-risk read with a named payoff.
