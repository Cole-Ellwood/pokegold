# GSC Canon: Phazing Priority

Use when Roar or Whirlwind can reset boosts, deny Rest loops, stop Baton Pass,
or decide a Curse mirror.

## Route Rules

- In GSC, Roar and Whirlwind have priority -1. Do not import later-generation
  -6 phazing priority.
- When both sides use a negative-priority phazing move, ordinary priority and
  speed ordering still matter inside that bracket. The slower phazer can move
  after the faster phazer and win the exchange.
- Curse can therefore create a phazing timing route: becoming slower may
  improve the phaze mirror while also changing damage and Rest clocks.
- Phazing is not progress by itself. It converts when it removes a setup route,
  preserves Spikes damage, forces a bad entry, or buys a specific Rest/PP
  window.
- If the opponent can attack for decisive damage instead of boosting, do not
  overprice the phaze line.

## Move-Choice Checks

1. What exact setup or pass route does phazing deny this turn?
2. Is the phazer faster or slower after current boosts and Curse drops?
3. Does phazing preserve a route piece, or does it donate a free entry?
4. Is attacking, Resting, or switching better because the opponent is not
   forced to continue setup?
