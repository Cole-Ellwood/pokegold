# GSC Canon: Lead RPS

Use when the first turns depend on opening role pressure, sleep risk, early
Spikes, item/status scouting, or preserving the correct opener.

## Route Rules

- Lead choice creates the first route package: immediate pressure, Spikes,
  sleep threat, item scouting, or defensive ownership.
- A useful shortcut from Smogon lead analysis is the common RPS shape:
  Electrics pressure Cloyster, Cloyster pressures Snorlax with Spikes and
  Explosion threat, and Snorlax pressures Electrics with bulk and immediate
  damage.
- Secondary leads such as Exeggutor, Nidoking, Jynx, Forretress, Smeargle,
  and Tyranitar bend that triangle through sleep, Spikes, Thief, Explosion,
  matchup fishing, or anti-lead pressure.
- Do not overfit the lead. No Team Preview means the first turn should create
  a route while preserving flexibility for the hidden five.
- Lead sleep is especially branch-heavy: identify the absorber, the Sleep
  Clause implication, and whether the sleep move actually improves the next
  two turns.

## Move-Choice Checks

1. Who owns turn 1 and by what resource: damage, sleep, Spikes, Thief, or
   forced switch?
2. What hidden role is being preserved by not trading the lead immediately?
3. Which branch punishes the common receiver or absorber?
4. Is this an opening route or just a safe-looking script?
