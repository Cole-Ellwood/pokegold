# GSC Canon: Pursuit And Spinblock Pressure

Use when Pursuit, Ghosts, Starmie, Cloyster, Forretress, Tyranitar, Houndoom,
Gengar, or Misdreavus decide whether hazards and support pieces survive.

## Route Rules

- Pursuit punishes switching. Its value is highest when trapping or weakening a
  role piece changes the hazard, spin, Explosion, sleep, or endgame route.
- Pursuit on a Ghost can open Rapid Spin routes. Pursuit on Starmie can keep
  Spikes up by removing or weakening the spinner.
- Do not click Pursuit only because the target might switch. Name the branch:
  what leaves, what stays, and which branch the move actually punishes.
- Ghosts are not interchangeable. Gengar tends to offer immediate offense and
  Explosion pressure; Misdreavus tends to offer bulkier spinblock and Perish
  routes. Use revealed behavior first, not set memory.
- Tyranitar and Houndoom Pursuit pressure can be route-defining, but do not
  anchor a recommendation on an unrevealed trapper unless it is a labeled
  high-risk read with a fallback.

## Move-Choice Checks

1. If the target switches, does Pursuit change the route or just add damage?
2. If the target stays, is the Pursuit turn still acceptable?
3. Which hazard or spinblock piece becomes free after the trap?
4. Is the trapper revealed, strong-prior, or possible-only?
