# GSC Canon: Sleep And Status

Use when a move choice depends on sleep, Rest, Sleep Talk, Toxic, paralysis,
burn, or freeze timing.

## Route Rules

- Sleep is a route resource, not just a status. Name the absorber and the
  next-board owner before clicking the sleep move or before letting a Pokemon
  take sleep.
- Sleep Clause means a second sleep attempt usually cannot be your main route
  if one opposing Pokemon is already sleeping from your sleep move.
- Rest sleep is self-inflicted and has different strategic meaning from
  opponent-induced sleep. In Rest races, compare boost-now/attack-next against
  attack-now/reset-next.
- Sleep Talk can keep a sleeping Pokemon active, but it does not make the
  sleeping piece free. Price the bad rolls and the opponent's reset or setup
  turn.
- Toxic pressure is clock pressure only if the opponent cannot reset it by
  switching or by using Rest before the route converts.
- Paralysis matters most when it changes ownership: speed control, full-para
  risk, and enabling a slower breaker or phazer.
- Burn cuts physical damage and adds a clock, but do not treat it as a hard
  answer to special attackers or setup packages it does not actually stop.
- Freeze is high-impact but not a plan unless the line is already good without
  relying on it.

## Move-Choice Checks

1. Is the target the right absorber, or are you sleeping the wrong role?
2. Does the sleep/status click convert this cycle, or does it give a free
   switch, Rest, Spin, phaze, or setup?
3. If the sleeper can Sleep Talk, which roll is actually required?
4. If the line depends on Toxic, what happens after the target switches?
