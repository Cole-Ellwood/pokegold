# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-891171

Replay: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-891171

Tournament context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-2025-circuit-championship-semifinals.3774299/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for general GSC
study, not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 47 turns.

Players: Glfgno7 defeated THE PDC SHOW.

## Team Shapes

Glfgno7 revealed:

- Nidoking: Earthquake, Lovely Kiss.
- Cloyster: Toxic, Spikes.
- Machamp: Curse, Cross Chop.
- Snorlax: Curse, Body Slam, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Tyranitar: Crunch, Roar.
- Zapdos: unrevealed moves in this log.

THE PDC SHOW revealed:

- Raikou: Hidden Power.
- Snorlax: Curse, Double-Edge, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Skarmory: Toxic, Hidden Power.
- Alakazam: Toxic.
- Golem: Earthquake, Rapid Spin, Roar.
- Cloyster: Spikes, Toxic, Surf, Explosion.

## Phase Map

Opening, turns 1-5:

- Nidoking immediately chunks Raikou with Earthquake, then misses Lovely Kiss
  into the Snorlax switch.
- Glfgno7 does not continue a failed sleep script. Cloyster enters on Curse,
  poisons Snorlax, and sets Spikes before dying.
- The crit Double-Edge into Cloyster is costly, but Cloyster still leaves a
  concrete route: poisoned Snorlax plus one layer of Spikes.

Machamp pressure and first answer collapse, turns 6-15:

- Machamp uses Curse as Skarmory enters.
- Skarmory lands Toxic, but Machamp still removes it with a critical Cross Chop
  on turn 9.
- Golem removes Spikes on turn 12, then Roars Snorlax out, but the spin route
  costs it too much position. Tyranitar eventually KOs Golem on turn 15.
- By this point PDC has removed the hazard, but has also lost Skarmory and
  Golem, two important physical-route controls.

PDC hazard counterplay, turns 16-25:

- PDC's Cloyster sets Spikes on turn 16.
- Snorlax and Nidoking force damage onto PDC's poisoned Snorlax, but PDC gets
  Rest on turn 19 and later uses Cloyster Toxic plus Explosion to put Glfgno7's
  Snorlax under pressure.
- This is a good route-trade sequence from PDC: their Cloyster turns from
  hazard setter into status plus Explosion pressure once Spikes are up.

RestTalk anchor fight, turns 26-38:

- The Snorlax mirror becomes a RestTalk branch-pricing drill.
- Sleep Talk calling Rest at full HP fails, but later damaged Rest calls heal
  and reset the sleeping Snorlax.
- Glfgno7's Snorlax repeatedly damages PDC's RestTalk Snorlax, then pivots to
  Tyranitar on turn 37 as PDC's Snorlax Rests again.
- Tyranitar Roars on turn 38, forcing Alakazam in and changing the route from
  a pure Snorlax mirror into a mixed cleanup problem.

Expiring converter endgame, turns 39-47:

- Tyranitar poisons itself by taking Toxic from Alakazam, but Crunch puts
  Alakazam low.
- The key preserved piece is Machamp. It has been poisoned since turn 8 and is
  repeatedly taxed by Spikes, but it still threatens Raikou, Alakazam, and
  Snorlax.
- On turns 41-47, Glfgno7 schedules Machamp entries while it still has enough
  HP: it KOs Raikou, catches Alakazam on a switch, and finally removes
  RestTalk Snorlax.
- The final Snorlax KO is a critical hit, so the exact no-crit endgame is not
  proven by the log. The strategic point is that Machamp's poisoned timer was
  cashed in while the matchups were still available.

## Critical Turns

Turn 2:

- Lovely Kiss misses. The important behavior is the re-score: Nidoking does not
  keep trying to force a sleep plan into a now-active Curse Snorlax. Cloyster
  enters and creates a different clock with Toxic plus Spikes.

Turns 4-5:

- Cloyster dies, but not before poisoning Snorlax and setting Spikes. This is
  an acceptable route trade because Snorlax is a central anchor and the hazard
  creates switch pressure while PDC still has several grounded Pokemon.

Turn 9:

- Machamp removes Skarmory. The crit matters, but the decision pressure was
  already real: a boosted Machamp forced Skarmory to answer it, and Skarmory's
  Toxic did not end Machamp's route immediately.

Turn 12:

- Golem spins away Glfgno7's Spikes. This does not fully erase the opening
  route because Golem then becomes the target of Snorlax/Tyranitar pressure and
  dies soon after. Hazard removal can still be expensive.

Turn 25:

- Cloyster Explodes into Snorlax. This is PDC's best route-trade moment:
  Cloyster has already set Spikes and poisoned Snorlax, so it spends itself to
  prevent Glfgno7's Snorlax from freely taking over.

Turn 37:

- Glfgno7 pivots Snorlax out as PDC's Snorlax Rests. This is a strong
  plan-revision turn. Continuing the mirror is no longer the only plan; Roar
  Tyranitar can turn the sleeping Snorlax state into a forced Alakazam entry.

Turns 41-47:

- Machamp is not healthy, but it is still live. The correct question is not
  "is Machamp poisoned?" but "can Machamp still convert before poison and
  Spikes expire it?" Glfgno7 uses the remaining HP to force the endgame.

## Practical Lessons

- A statused attacker can still be the win condition. Poison changes the time
  budget; it does not automatically make the Pokemon expendable.
- Schedule expiring converters. If a poisoned or hazard-taxed piece is the only
  route into the opponent's remaining threats, preserve enough HP for the exact
  entries that matter and cash it before the clock runs out.
- Hazard removal has an opportunity cost. Golem successfully spun, but the spin
  did not win the position once Golem became exposed and Skarmory was gone.
- RestTalk mirrors are not static. Sleep Talk can attack, boost, fail at full
  HP with Rest, or heal when damaged; each call changes whether to continue,
  phaze, pivot, or hand off to another converter.
- Do not review the final crit as the whole game. The final Cross Chop crit
  mattered, but the preceding route work preserved Machamp and removed or
  weakened the pieces it needed to beat.

## Cookbook Update

Added `Recipe: Expiring Converter Scheduling`:

```text
When a poisoned, burned, trapped, hazard-taxed, or low-HP Pokemon is still the
best converter into the opponent's remaining route, do not treat it as either
fully healthy or already dead. Count its remaining usable entries, preserve the
ones that matter, and cash it before its clock expires.
```
