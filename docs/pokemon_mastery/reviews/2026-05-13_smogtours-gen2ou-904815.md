## Phase Review

Opening contact creates the first route map, turns 1-10:

- Exeggutor misses Sleep Powder into Heracross on turn 1, then steals Raikou's
  Leftovers with Thief on turn 5 and sleeps Raikou on turn 6.
- Cloyster switches into Curse Tyranitar and KOs it with Surf on turns 9-10.
- The important opening fact is not a preview read. It is fast route discovery:
  Raikou is asleep and itemless, Tyranitar is removed, and Cloyster has already
  proved it can punish slow setup.

Whirlwind is real while the target pool exists, turns 15-42:

- Both Cloyster set Spikes on turn 15.
- Zapdos uses Whirlwind on turns 17, 19, 31, 35, 39, and 42. These turns drag
  Cloyster, Snorlax, Exeggutor, and Heracross through Spikes or force them into
  worse HP / status timing.
- Steelix Explodes on Cloyster on turn 25, and Snorlax trades with opposing
  Snorlax on turn 28.
- Cloyster later Explodes on sleeping Heracross on turn 38. M Dragon has
  converted hazards and phazing into material, but each trade also reduces the
  number of future phaze targets.

The target pool collapses, turns 45-58:

- Zapdos KOs Exeggutor on turn 45. RUBYBLOOD now has only Raikou left.
- Vaporeon enters on turn 54 and nearly converts the damage race after Growth:
  Surf takes Raikou to 52% on turn 56 and then to 6% on turn 57.
- Raikou's Thunder hits on turn 58, removing Vaporeon. The endgame becomes
  Zapdos against last-Pokemon Raikou.

Whirlwind becomes no progress at last-mon, turns 59-82:

- Raikou repeatedly uses Rest and Sleep Talk while Zapdos attacks with
  Thunderbolt / Hidden Power or attempts Thunder Wave.
- Zapdos uses Whirlwind on turns 69, 70, 73, 74, 79, and 80. No drag follows
  because Raikou has no bench target left.
- Thunder Wave also has timing trouble: attempts into sleeping Raikou do not
  create the needed status, while paralysis on awake Raikou is later cleared by
  Rest.
- Raikou finally wakes, lands Thunder, and KOs Zapdos on turn 82.

## Critical Turns

Turn 15:

- Both sides set Spikes. This makes the early Whirlwind turns valuable because
  the dragged targets have a cost.

Turns 17 and 19:

- Zapdos uses Whirlwind successfully while RUBYBLOOD still has a bench. The
  same move is strong here because it both denies Raikou's sleep stabilization
  and taxes the next target with Spikes.

Turns 25, 28, and 38:

- Explosion and Self-Destruct trades remove Cloyster, Snorlax, and Heracross.
  These trades improve the immediate material map but also shrink the future
  Whirlwind target pool.

Turn 45:

- Exeggutor faints, leaving RUBYBLOOD with only Raikou. From this point,
  Zapdos must win through damage, status timing, PP, or preserving Vaporeon;
  Whirlwind no longer has a legal target.

Turns 69-80:

- Repeated Whirlwind attempts do not move Raikou. This is not a prediction
  failure; it is a mechanics / target-pool failure.

## Practical Lessons

Phazing has a target-pool gate:

- Before recommending Roar or Whirlwind, confirm that the target has a living
  non-active Pokemon to drag. If not, the move is no longer route control.

The same move can change class:

- Whirlwind was strong with Spikes and a bench. After the bench disappeared,
  it became a low-effect PP spend. Re-score control moves after every KO,
  trade, or forced switch.

Hazard pressure consumes its own future:

- Phazing plus Spikes wants targets to keep entering. If your trades remove
  those targets, the plan must hand off to damage, status, or PP before the
  final one-on-one begins.

Status must beat the reset map:

- Thunder Wave was useful only if it landed on an awake Raikou and mattered
  before Rest cleared it. Status into a Rest loop is not progress unless it
  changes the next forced action.

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab uses local force-switch code, so Roar / Whirlwind advice must
  check local mechanics, not modern phazing assumptions.
- Local source confirms the target-pool gate: `BattleCommand_ForceSwitch`
  calls `FindAliveEnemyMons` for enemy targets and
  `CheckPlayerHasMonToSwitchTo` for player targets; both route to failure when
  no non-active living target exists.
- The same local command also checks Gen 2 move order through
  `wEnemyGoesFirst`, so a phazing move can fail either because it acts too
  early or because there is no legal target to drag.
- Player-side boss advice may know the boss roster and remaining boss material
  after the fight reveals it. Boss AI must not infer a hidden player bench from
  unrevealed team knowledge; it can only use public battle state, revealed
  species, observed switches/faints, and source-allowed memory.

## New Notebook Lesson

Roar and Whirlwind need both timing and a target pool. If the move cannot act
last or the target has no living bench, stop treating phazing as route control
and hand off to damage, status, PP, recovery, or a trade.
