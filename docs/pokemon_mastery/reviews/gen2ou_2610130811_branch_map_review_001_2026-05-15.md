## Branch Map

Turns 1-2:

into paralyzed Gengar. Active target and receiver punish aligned.

Turn 3:
After Psychic left Gengar low, Tyranitar was the actual counter-owner because
it blanked Psychic and threatened Exeggutor. Giga Drain was the exact punish.
Before Tyranitar was public, this was a high-risk hidden exact read; the
reusable rule is to list Dark/Rock/Pursuit owner as a branch, not to assume
Tyranitar exists.

damage, but paralysis changed the later pass and speed map while Giga did not
remove Tyranitar before Crunch chunked Exeggutor.

Turn 5:
After Tyranitar was paralyzed, Exeggutor's low HP did not make it useless.
Giga Drain hit both the active Tyranitar route and the Alakazam switch while
recovering enough HP to keep Exeggutor relevant.

Turns 6-8:
Blissey first absorbed Alakazam's Psychic, preserving Umbreon as the growth/
pass converter. After Reflect, Umbreon entered and Growth was correct because
Alakazam was playing support/recovery instead of immediate damage.

Turns 9-10:
Encore changed the pass route. Once Umbreon was locked into Growth, the next
question was not "how many boosts can I greed," but whether a physical/phaze
counter-owner could enter before Baton Pass was legal again. Breaking Encore
with a pivot was sharper than staying.

Turns 11-12:
Donphan invited a Water/Ground answer, but the opponent used low Gengar as the
counter-pivot. Exeggutor answered that board. Once Gengar was active and low,
Psychic beat the active target, but Stun Spore beat the Alakazam branch and
kept speed control; status was the multi-branch punish.

## Reusable Rules

- Hidden exact counter-owners from no Team Preview are branches, not anchors.
  Promote them only when they are public, strong-prior by revealed structure,
  or explicitly marked as a read with fallback.
- A low support attacker can still be the right branch punish when its coverage
  hits both active target and likely switch while restoring HP.
- Against support Alakazam, price Encore after Reflect/Recover appears; if
  Encore locks a pass route, compare staying for more boosts against breaking
  the lock before a phazer or physical counter-owner enters.
- If active KO and switch status both look good, prefer the move that beats
  multiple named branches when the active target is already low/statused.

## Scoring Adjustment

Do not treat turn 3 as proof that hidden Tyranitar should have anchored the
pre-reveal recommendation. It is a branch-calibration miss, not a hidden-info
license. Future artifacts should separate exact top, acceptable class match,
and "hidden exact branch only known after reveal."
