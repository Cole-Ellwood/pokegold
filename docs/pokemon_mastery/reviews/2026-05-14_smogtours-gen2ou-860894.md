# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-860894

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-860894

Source thread context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-2025-circuit-championship-finals.3774299/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Use this for trap escape maps,
phazing, Spikes asymmetry, Reflect timing, and post-trap route checks, not as
romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 47 turns, ending by forfeit.

Players: melancholy0 defeated underlying.

## Revealed Pieces

underlying:

- Tyranitar with Earthquake, Rock Slide, Thunderbolt, and Dynamic Punch.
- Gengar with Destiny Bond, Mean Look, Perish Song, and Ice Punch.
- Snorlax with Earthquake, Double-Edge, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Clamp, and Explosion.
- Alakazam, shown only as a sacrificed switch-in.
- Zapdos, shown late but without useful revealed moves.

melancholy0:

- Raikou with Reflect, Thunderbolt, and Rest.
- Misdreavus with Toxic and Thunder.
- Skarmory with Toxic, Drill Peck, and Whirlwind.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Rapid Spin, Toxic, Surf, and Leftovers.
- Snorlax with Curse, Double-Edge, Rest, and Flamethrower.
- Umbreon with Charm and Pursuit pressure.

## Phase Map

Reflect and status make Tyranitar finite, turns 1-9:

- Raikou opens with Reflect as Tyranitar uses Earthquake. The damage is real,
  but Reflect keeps Raikou from becoming immediate dead weight.
- Misdreavus then draws Tyranitar's Rock Slide, pivots through Gengar/Snorlax,
  and lands Toxic on Snorlax.
- Skarmory's Ground immunity and Cloyster's Spikes turn convert that early
  status into a longer map: underlying has to fight while repeatedly paying
  entry costs and poison/recovery timing.

Hazard asymmetry is established, turns 10-13:

- melancholy0's Cloyster sets Spikes on turn 10. underlying's Cloyster sets a
  counter-layer on turn 11, but is poisoned.
- On turn 13, melancholy0's Cloyster Rapid Spins away the counter-layer before
  underlying's Cloyster Explodes.
- The Explosion leaves Cloyster at 11%, but the key map is asymmetric: Spikes
  remain on underlying's side, and melancholy0 keeps a low Cloyster that can
  still be useful later.

PerishTrap removes one converter, turns 19-26:

- melancholy0's Snorlax reaches repeated Curse turns and KOs Alakazam on turn
  19.
- underlying's Gengar enters, uses Destiny Bond to make direct removal
  awkward, then traps Snorlax with Mean Look and starts Perish Song.
- Snorlax damages Gengar with Flamethrower, but cannot leave; underlying
  switches to Tyranitar as the count reaches zero, and Snorlax faints on turn
  26.
- This is a successful trap, but it is not the end of the route. The remaining
  map still contains Skarmory's Whirlwind, Raikou's Reflect/Rest, Umbreon
  support, Cloyster, and Spikes on underlying's side.

Second trap fails the escape map, turns 27-30:

- Gengar returns into Skarmory, uses Mean Look, and starts Perish Song.
- Skarmory's Drill Peck puts Gengar low, then Whirlwind on turn 29 ejects it
  before the trap route can convert.
- This is the central lesson: a trapping move hitting does not prove the trap
  route. The escape branch still existed and resolved.

Reset and Spikes close the game, turns 31-47:

- Raikou gets Reflect again on turn 31, trades down Tyranitar, and Rests on
  turn 34.
- Skarmory repeatedly denies Snorlax and Gengar with Toxic and Whirlwind, while
  Spikes keep shrinking every attempted reset.
- Tyranitar later pressures Skarmory with Thunderbolt, but Umbreon cuts its
  Attack with Charm and Pursuit pressure catches the Gengar pivot sequence.
- The low Cloyster from turn 13 still matters. It pivots into Gengar, absorbs
  Ice Punch at low HP, then Surfs the Spikes-taxed Tyranitar for the final KO
  on turn 47.

## Critical Turns

Turn 13:

- Cloyster spins before taking Explosion.
- This is a familiar hazard-retention lesson: damage into the spinner after
  Spin is not the same as denying Spin. Here it creates a durable Spikes
  asymmetry that shapes the whole game.

Turns 21-26:

- Gengar successfully uses Destiny Bond, Mean Look, and Perish Song to remove a
  boosted Snorlax.
- The play is valuable, but the advisor should immediately ask what the trap
  opened and which blockers remain. It removed one converter; it did not solve
  Skarmory, Raikou, or the Spikes map.

Turn 29:

- Skarmory Whirlwinds Gengar after Mean Look and Perish Song.
- The second trap route fails because the escape branch was legal and live.
  A trap plan must check phazing before treating the first trap's success as a
  reusable script.

Turns 31-35:

- Raikou uses Reflect, trades with Tyranitar, and Rests.
- The reset is not passive because Spikes and Skarmory keep underlying from
  repeatedly choosing the same attacks without paying entry and poison costs.

Turns 45-47:

- Low Cloyster switches into Gengar, survives the low-pressure branch, then
  catches Tyranitar with Surf after Spikes.
- This is not a full spacer-sack line because Cloyster survives. It is still
  low-material utility: a nearly spent piece can be the exact pivot that closes
  the last route.

## Practical Lessons

Trap success is not a reusable script:

```text
target trapped:
target removed:
route opened:
escape branch that failed or remained:
next blocker after the trap:
```

- Gengar removing Snorlax was real progress.
- Repeating the same Mean Look / Perish Song idea into Skarmory failed because
  Whirlwind was a live escape.
- After a trap succeeds, rebuild the route map before trying the next trap.

Hazard asymmetry changes every support judgment:

- underlying removed a large chunk of Cloyster with Explosion, but the counter
  Spikes were already gone.
- melancholy0's side kept the only layer that mattered. That made Tyranitar,
  Gengar, Alakazam, and Snorlax entries all more expensive.
- A support trade should be scored by the retained board, not by the visible HP
  swing alone.

Low-material pieces still need a job check:

- Cloyster at 11-20% was not preserved as a healthy wall. It was preserved
  because it still had enough HP and typing to take a narrow Gengar line and
  close with Surf afterward.
- Before dismissing a low piece, ask whether it can still spin, phaze, pivot,
  absorb a hit, force recoil, scout, or cash out.

Reflect is a route bridge, not just damage reduction:

- Raikou's Reflect kept Tyranitar from turning early Earthquake damage into a
  clean trade and later helped the team reset around Snorlax/Tyranitar
  pressure.
- Screens should be judged by what they let the team do during the reduced
  damage turns: Rest, pivot, force Spikes entries, or expose the next attacker.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab advice that recommends Mean Look, Spider Web, Perish Song, or
  another trap route must name the escape branch: phazing, Haze, Baton Pass,
  switch timing, KO, Protect/Endure, Rest, cure, item, or local type/passive
  behavior.
- A boss or player can repeat a trap line only after a new public fact makes
  the old escape branch worse: phazer removed, phazer low, no target pool,
  Speed/order changed, trapper healthy enough, or hazards make the dragged
  target unfavorable.
- Local Spikes are three-layer and Rapid Spin clears all local layers, so the
  turn-13 hazard asymmetry lesson is even sharper in Gym Leader Lab.
- Boss AI may use its own team plan and public state to judge trap/escape
  branches, but must not use hidden player reserves to prove that no phazer,
  Haze user, Ghost, spinner, or trap answer exists.

## New Notebook Lesson

No new policy entry needed. This replay reinforces `STP-044`: non-damage
support needs a receiver and escape map. A successful first trap does not make
the second trap correct unless the new target's escape branch is priced.
