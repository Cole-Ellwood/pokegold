# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-890958

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-890958

Source thread context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-2025-circuit-championship-finals.3774299/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Use this for route timing,
reset-hub leakage, Explosion targeting, phazing, and endgame spacer lessons,
not as romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 68 turns.

Players: KeshBa45 defeated Siatam.

## Revealed Pieces

Siatam:

- Snorlax with Curse and Body Slam.
- Quagsire with Belly Drum, Earthquake, and Rest.
- Zapdos with Thunder.
- Miltank with Heal Bell, Growl, Milk Drink, and Body Slam.
- Starmie with Thunder Wave, Rapid Spin, Recover, and Surf.
- Rhydon with Earthquake.

KeshBa45:

- Exeggutor with Stun Spore and Explosion.
- Golem with Roar and Earthquake.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Toxic, Surf, and Explosion.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Rest, Sleep Talk, and Earthquake.
- Zapdos with Thunderbolt, Thunder Wave, and Whirlwind.
- Marowak with Earthquake and Swords Dance.

## Phase Map

Opening status and phaze skeleton, turns 1-13:

- Exeggutor paralyzes Snorlax on turn 1. Siatam still starts Curse pressure,
  but the route is no longer a clean speed-neutral anchor route.
- KeshBa45 repeatedly uses Golem's Roar to deny Curselax conversion on turns 4,
  23, 33, 40, 45, and 47.
- Cloyster gets Spikes on turn 10 and poisons Starmie on turn 11. Starmie later
  becomes the reset hub, but it is already being asked to spin, heal, status,
  and absorb hazard timing.
- KeshBa45's Snorlax trades aggressively with Zapdos on turns 7 and 13. The
  turn-13 critical hit matters, but the strategic point is that Zapdos no
  longer checks the later phaze and Marowak map.

Reset hub keeps the middle game alive, turns 14-42:

- Miltank repeatedly resets status with Heal Bell, lowers Attack with Growl,
  and heals with Milk Drink. Starmie spins on turns 25 and 34, then Recovers
  and pressures Cloyster.
- Those resets are real stabilization. They are not a win condition yet,
  because KeshBa45 still has leaks through Cloyster Explosion, Exeggutor
  Explosion, Golem/Zapdos phazing, and Marowak.
- The middle game is a useful anti-autopilot example: reset loops should be
  judged by whether all leaks are closed, not by whether the active threat is
  temporarily contained.

Cloyster cashes out against the reset hub, turns 42-43:

- Cloyster sets Spikes on turn 42. Starmie immediately Rapid Spins on turn 43,
  so the layer does not remain.
- Cloyster Explodes into Starmie on that same turn, removing the spinner and
  recovery hub even though the hazard layer is gone.
- The trade is still coherent because the target is the reset function, not
  because Spikes were retained. This differs from a pure hazard-retention
  line.

Delayed Explosion creates breaker entry, turns 49-56:

- Exeggutor is preserved until Snorlax is active and route-relevant.
- On turn 55, Exeggutor Explodes into Snorlax, leaving it at 8%. Marowak enters
  and KOs Snorlax on turn 56.
- The Explosion is not just large damage. It removes the immediate body in
  front of Marowak and creates clean breaker entry before Miltank can fully
  reset the position.

Endgame spacer, turns 57-68:

- Miltank uses Body Slam, Milk Drink, and Growl to slow Marowak, but Marowak
  still forces enough damage that Miltank and Rhydon fall before Quagsire
  removes it.
- Quagsire creates a dangerous last route with Belly Drum on turn 63 and Rest
  on turn 64.
- KeshBa45's paralyzed Golem is low, but it still has one job left. On turn 67
  it switches into Quagsire's wake-up Earthquake and faints, giving Snorlax a
  fresh final entry.
- On turn 68, Snorlax survives +6 Earthquake at 4%, KOs Quagsire with
  Double-Edge, and faints to recoil. KeshBa45 wins because the spacer made the
  final material map exact.

## Critical Turns

Turn 25:

- Starmie Rapid Spins into Exeggutor and removes Spikes, but Exeggutor lands
  Stun Spore.
- The spin succeeds, yet Starmie's reset role is degraded. Status on the reset
  hub is a route tax even when the immediate support move works.

Turn 43:

- Starmie Rapid Spins and removes Spikes before Cloyster Explodes.
- Do not misread this as hazard retention. KeshBa45 loses the layer but removes
  the spinner/recovery hub, changing the later reset map.

Turn 55:

- Exeggutor Explodes into Snorlax, then Marowak enters.
- This is delayed one-time-resource timing: preserve Explosion until the target
  is the blocker or body that gives the breaker clean entry.

Turn 67:

- Golem dies to Quagsire Earthquake, but that death is not empty. It absorbs the
  boosted wake-up hit and brings Snorlax in from a clean state for the final
  forced trade.

## Practical Lessons

Reset hubs win only after leaks are closed:

```text
What reset hub is stabilizing the board:
What leaks still break the loop:
Which leak can be removed now:
What converter wins after the leak is gone:
```

- Miltank and Starmie repeatedly reset status, hazards, HP, and Attack stages.
- That was strong enough to keep Siatam alive, but not enough to end the game
  while Cloyster, Exeggutor, phazing, and Marowak were still live.
- Do not call a reset loop winning until the remaining one-time trades and
  breakers have been named and controlled.

Explosion can target a reset function instead of a hazard layer:

- Turn 43 removes Starmie after Rapid Spin succeeds.
- If the review only asks whether Spikes stayed up, it misses the point. The
  reset hub is gone, so later Snorlax/Marowak routes no longer have to fight
  Starmie's spin, Recover, Surf, or Thunder Wave.

Delayed Explosion should be tied to the receiver:

- Exeggutor does not boom at the first contact point.
- The turn-55 Explosion matters because Marowak enters immediately afterward
  into the weakened Snorlax map.
- Before approving Explosion, name both the target removed and the Pokemon that
  converts after the trade.

A low piece can still be endgame material:

- Golem looked nearly spent: low HP, paralyzed, and repeatedly used for Roar
  earlier.
- Turn 67 shows the remaining job. It absorbs the boosted wake-up Earthquake
  and creates a clean Snorlax entry, removing the full-paralysis and entry-risk
  branch from the final turn.
- A spacer sack is not "save scoreline" play. It is correct only if the final
  material after the sack is better than hard-switching or preserving it.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab boss fights should treat low-HP pieces as possible spacers,
  not automatically as dead material. The question is whether they can absorb a
  wake turn, priority hit, boosted hit, Focus Band branch, Quick Claw branch,
  phazing target, or contact/passive trigger before the converter enters.
- A boss or advisor can use public state to plan spacer sacks, but boss AI must
  not use hidden player reserves to prove that the sacrifice has no downside.
- Exact transfer needs local evidence for Explosion damage, Focus Band,
  Protect/Endure priority, Quick Claw, sleep/wake timing, phazing target pools,
  type passives, Spikes damage, and local Speed order.
- When advising a boss fight, write the final material after the sack. If the
  incoming piece still cannot force the win, the sack is cosmetic.

## New Notebook Lesson

Spacer sacks are endgame material. A low-value piece can be worth more by
fainting on the exact turn that gives a converter clean entry or removes a bad
variance branch than by being preserved as dead material.
