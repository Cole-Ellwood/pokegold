Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-740650

Source discovery context:

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Use this for support ordering,

Length: 42 turns.

Players: Conflict defeated thankyou.

## Phase Map

Opening tempo after the lead crit, turns 1-6:

- Both players lead Snorlax without Team Preview information.
- Conflict's Snorlax crits Double-Edge on turn 1 while thankyou Curses.
- thankyou must Rest on turn 2. Conflict uses the sleep window to bring
  Forretress in safely.
- Forretress sets Spikes on turn 3, then Steelix enters and starts pressuring
  with Curse, Earthquake, and later Roar.
- The key opening lesson is not "crit wins." It is that a forced Rest creates
  a support window, and Conflict immediately converts that window into Spikes
  plus Steelix positioning.

Hazard tax and first removal, turns 7-23:

- thankyou is repeatedly taxed by Spikes while trying to answer Steelix,
  Zapdos, Exeggutor, and Snorlax.
- Golem enters on turn 22 into Snorlax Double-Edge and reveals Rapid Spin on
  turn 23, removing the layer.
- This is a good spin turn because Golem survives the entry plus attack branch
  and clears before Snorlax's Earthquake damage matters.

Counter-layer at high cost, turns 24-27:

- thankyou brings Cloyster into Snorlax and sets Spikes on turn 25.
- Conflict's Snorlax reveals Thunder and KOs Cloyster immediately.
- The layer does matter later, but Cloyster's death means thankyou has spent
  the setter and cannot keep fighting the hazard war.

Support compression, turns 27-29:

- Conflict brings Forretress into sleeping Suicune on turn 27.
- On turn 28, Suicune Sleep Talks Surf and chunks Forretress, but Forretress
  still sets Spikes.
- On turn 29, Suicune Sleep Talks Ice Beam and Forretress Explodes, leaving
  Suicune at 23% asleep.
- This is the central lesson: once a support mon has a safe enough support
  window, it can deliver the support first and then cash out before preservation
  becomes fake value.

Endgame conversion, turns 30-42:

- Zapdos wakes and pressures Raikou, paralyzing it on turn 30.
- Golem is too low and dies to Zapdos Hidden Power on turn 31 after taking
  Spikes.
- Conflict then chains one-time trades: Snorlax Self-Destructs into sleeping
  Snorlax on turn 34, and Gengar Explodes into paralyzed Raikou on turn 35.
- Zapdos is left to fight a low, sleeping Suicune and Skarmory. It eventually
  breaks Suicune with Thunder crit on turn 41 and KOs Skarmory on turn 42.

## Critical Turns

Turn 2:

- thankyou Rests after the lead Snorlax crit.
- Conflict switches to Forretress, correctly treating Rest as a support-entry
  window rather than a passive reset.

Turn 23:

- Golem Rapid Spins into Snorlax and removes Spikes.
- Snorlax's Earthquake hurts Golem afterward, but the spin already happened and
  Golem survives. Damage after removal is not the same as preventing removal.

Turn 25:

- Cloyster sets Spikes and dies to ThunderLax.
- This is a costly support turn. The layer must be judged by whether the
  remaining team can convert it, because Cloyster will not keep supporting.

Turns 28-29:

- Forretress sets Spikes, then Explodes.
- The order matters. Explosion first would damage Suicune but leave no layer;
  preserving Forretress would ignore that Surf/Ice Beam pressure is making its
  future support value disappear.

Turns 34-35:

- Conflict spends Snorlax and then Gengar with Self-Destruct / Explosion.
- These are not independent "big damage" clicks. They make sense because the
  earlier Spikes plus paralysis plus low Suicune leave Zapdos as the converter.

## Practical Lessons

Forced recovery or sleep is a support-entry window:

```text
What support can land before the target wakes/resets:
Who receives that support:
Can the support user live one more turn after delivering it:
If not, what cash-out preserves the route:
```

Order support before cash-out when the support changes the endgame:

- Forretress's turn-28 Spikes creates the later entry tax.
- Turn-29 Explosion then converts Forretress's remaining HP into Suicune
  damage.
- The sequence is stronger than either action alone because the layer makes
  later switches worse while the trade puts Suicune into Zapdos range.

Do not over-preserve a support mon after its last job is known:

- Forretress was being damaged too quickly to promise future layers or spins.
- Once it had set Spikes, Explosion was a coherent way to prevent the support
  piece from becoming dead material.

Damage after Rapid Spin must be separated from denying Rapid Spin:

- Turn 23 mirrors the hazard-retention lesson from other reviews. Snorlax's
  Earthquake punished Golem but did not stop the layer from being removed.
- A spinner punish needs an endpoint beyond "we hit the spinner."

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab has three Spikes layers, so the value of "support first,
  cash-out second" can be higher, but so is the punishment for letting Rapid
  Spin erase the stack.
- A boss or player support mon should not be preserved by role label alone. If
  it can deliver its last real support job and then trade into the route's
  blocker, that may be better than switching out.
- Exact transfer needs local evidence for Explosion/Self-Destruct damage,
  Focus Band, Protect/Endure priority, Rapid Spin clearing, Sleep Talk timing,
  Rest turns, type passives, and boss AI public-info incentives.
- Boss AI may know its own Forretress-like support plan and remaining team, but
  it must not choose the cash-out because of hidden player reserve knowledge.

## New Notebook Lesson

Support compression has an order. If a low-future-value support piece can still
land the support that lets the converter win, deliver the support first; then
cash out only if the trade improves the named converter's route.
