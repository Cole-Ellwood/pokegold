## Revealed Structure

A Real Jester:

- Umbreon with Mean Look / Growl / Toxic.
- Cloyster with Spikes / Surf / Toxic.
- Snorlax with Curse / Double-Edge / Earthquake / Rest.
- Zapdos with Thunder / Hidden Power.
- Golem with Rapid Spin.
- Machamp with Curse / Rock Slide / Cross Chop.

Fakes:

- Forretress with Explosion.
- Steelix with Roar / Earthquake / Curse / Explosion.
- Zapdos with Thunder / Rest / Sleep Talk / Hidden Power.
- Cloyster with Spikes / Surf / Explosion.
- Vaporeon with Surf / Growth / Acid Armor / Rest.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge / Earthquake.

## Phase Review

Umbreon tests the escape map, turns 1-10:

- Umbreon repeatedly uses Mean Look and Growl into Steelix.
- The trap does not fully convert because Steelix can Roar Umbreon out on
  turns 2, 6, and 10.
- The Growl turns still matter. They keep Steelix from converting immediate
  Earthquake pressure and create space for Cloyster to set Spikes on turn 3.
- Turn 8 also proves an important negative: Steelix is immune to Umbreon's
  Toxic, so the Steelix plan cannot rely on poison.

Snorlax gets the first conversion window, turns 12-17:

- Fakes's Zapdos misses three Thunders while A Real Jester's Snorlax uses Curse
  twice and then damages Zapdos heavily.
- Snorlax then hits Forretress with Earthquake as it enters through Spikes.
- Forretress Explodes on turn 16, cutting Snorlax to 48% but not removing it.
- Snorlax Rests on turn 17 as Steelix Curses, preserving the anchor for the
  later endgame.

Cloyster trades hazard and material pressure, turns 18-22:

- Cloyster enters Steelix, then poisons Fakes's Cloyster while Fakes sets
  Spikes.
- A Real Jester's Cloyster keeps attacking instead of treating Toxic as enough.
- Fakes's Cloyster Explodes on turn 22, removing A Real Jester's Cloyster.
  Both sides have converted support pieces into different resources: Fakes has
  Spikes on the opposing side, while A Real Jester still has Spikes on Fakes's
  side and poison on Vaporeon later becomes reachable.

Umbreon relabels Vaporeon, turns 23-32:

- Umbreon re-enters and uses Growl as Vaporeon comes in, then Mean Look and
  Toxic land on Vaporeon.
- Growl is mostly irrelevant to Vaporeon's special damage, but the key support
  result is Toxic plus trapping pressure: Vaporeon is now a finite route unless
  it Rests.
- A Real Jester then pivots Zapdos into Surf pressure. The support turn is
  judged by the receiver map, not by Umbreon's damage.

Spin and support collapse, turns 33-43:

- Golem enters Snorlax and removes A Real Jester's Spikes weakness with Rapid
  Spin on turn 35, but then falls to a critical Earthquake.
- Fakes's Snorlax later critical KOs Umbreon on turn 39. This removes the
  ongoing debuff/trap support, but the earlier Toxic on Vaporeon remains in the
  route ledger.
- Machamp uses Curse and pressures Zapdos, then Steelix Explodes on turn 43,
  leaving Machamp at 26%. This trade damages the route but does not stop
  Snorlax from inheriting later.

Poisoned Vaporeon fails to hold the endgame, turns 44-58:

- Vaporeon uses Growth on turn 45, then later Growth, Acid Armor, and Rest on
  turns 51-53.
- The earlier Toxic forces the Rest cycle after Spikes and poison have already
  dragged Vaporeon down.
- Snorlax wakes on turn 53, Curses, and uses Double-Edge through the Rest
  turns. Vaporeon is KOed on turn 56 despite Acid Armor.
- Snorlax then removes sleeping Zapdos and the opposing Snorlax to end the
  game.

## Critical Turns

Turns 1-2:

- Mean Look into Growl does not become a full trap because Steelix Roars. This
  is the first escape-map lesson: a support plan is not real until phazing,
  switching, status immunity, and damage branches are priced.

Turn 3:

- Cloyster sets Spikes because Growl/Roar created the opening. The support
  sequence has already paid off if the layer later changes switch costs.

Turn 8:

- Toxic fails into Steelix immunity. After this, the Steelix control plan must
  use Growl, phazing pressure, damage, or another route; poison is off the map.

Turns 12-16:

- Snorlax converts the Zapdos miss window into Curse and damage, then survives
  Forretress Explosion. This is a high-variance section, but the useful review
  point is that support and hazards created a receiver that could use the
  opening.

Turn 30-31:

- Umbreon traps and poisons Vaporeon. That does not win immediately, but it
  turns a Growth/Acid Armor/Rest Pokemon into a timed route rather than a clean
  endgame anchor.

Turn 35:

- Golem spins away A Real Jester's Spikes weakness before fainting. Even though
  the crit removes it, the spin still changes Snorlax's later entry math.

Turns 51-56:

- Vaporeon boosts and Rests, but poison and prior chip force the timing. Snorlax
  wakes and converts the sleep turns. This is the delayed payoff from the
  earlier Umbreon support, not a standalone late-game discovery.

## Practical Lessons

Support contact must name the receiver:

- Growl, Mean Look, Toxic, Spikes, and Rapid Spin all look low-damage. They are
  progress only if they create a specific receiver state: Snorlax can Rest and
  re-enter, Vaporeon is poisoned into a Rest clock, or Steelix is too softened
  to punish.

Trap plans need an escape map:

- Mean Look did not invalidate Steelix because Roar kept resetting the trap.
  Strong advice prices phazing, immunity, switch timing, and the support user's
  HP before calling the trap complete.

Negative evidence matters:

- Toxic failing into Steelix immunity should immediately remove poison from the
  Steelix route plan. Repeating a rejected support line is not persistence.

Debuff turns can be real even when they do not deal damage:

- Growl did not KO anything, but it reduced physical pressure long enough to
  set Spikes, preserve Umbreon, and later keep Steelix from getting a clean
  damage snowball.

Delayed support payoff is still payoff:

- Vaporeon was poisoned on turn 31 and lost on turn 56. The correct review
  ledger connects those turns instead of treating the endgame as a fresh
  Snorlax-only exchange.

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab bosses and player teams often use low-damage support: Growl,
  Charm, Screech, Sand Attack, Toxic, Mean Look, Spider Web, Haze, phazing,
  screens, Rapid Spin, and Protect.
- Do not dismiss a support move because it lacks damage. Ask which receiver,
  clock, or later route it changes.
- Do not overrate support either. If the boss can Roar, Haze, switch, cure,
  Rest, ignore the status through immunity, or KO the support user before the
  receiver acts, the support route may be fake.
- Local advice must verify the romhack's trapping, phazing, stat-stage,
  immunity, status, Rest, Rapid Spin, and AI public-information behavior before
  transferring exact GSC claims.

## New Notebook Lesson

Non-damage support is route material only when it changes a receiver's future
turn. Name the receiver, the escape branch, and the payoff turn. If phazing,
immunity, cure, or a faster KO erases the support before the receiver acts,
abandon the support script and rebuild.
