## Phase Review

underlying wins the first hazard/status exchange, turns 1-12:

- Raikou leads into Snorlax and immediately pivots Forretress into
  Double-Edge.
- Forretress sets Spikes on turn 2 and poisons Snorlax on turn 3.
- Skarmory poisons Raikou on turn 6, but Spikes plus Toxic force Snorlax to
  Rest on turn 12.
- The opening is not a Team Preview plan. It is a public route map built from
  first contact: Forretress establishes Spikes, Snorlax is already on a Rest
  cycle, and Raikou's poison creates a separate clock that later requires Rest.

Vaporeon becomes the main converter, turns 17-24:

- Raikou Rests on turn 17 while Tyranitar starts Sandstorm.
- Vaporeon enters on turn 18 and Rests through the pressure on turn 19.
- Sleep Talk calls Growth on turn 20 and Surf on turn 21, which chunks Zapdos
  from full to 39%.
- Cloyster returns on turn 22, fails Toxic once on turn 23, lands it on turn
  24, and then dies to Surf.
- The important point is that Vaporeon's setup was not a one-turn sweep
  script. It accepted poison and Sleep Talk variance because the payoff was
  removing Cloyster and breaking Zapdos's safe-entry plan.

The converter overperforms, then runs out, turns 25-33:

- Vaporeon pressures sleeping Snorlax with Surf on turns 25-26. Sleep Talk Rest
  resets Snorlax on turn 26, so the route is not over.
- Zapdos re-enters on turn 27, hits Vaporeon hard, and then dies to Sleep Talk
  Surf on turn 28.
- Snorlax survives the next sequence through Sleep Talk Rest and Double-Edge.
  Vaporeon Rests again on turn 31, but repeated Double-Edge finally KOs it on
  turn 33.
- This is not a failed Vaporeon route. By the time it faints, it has removed
  Cloyster and Zapdos, forced multiple Snorlax resets, and exposed the
  remaining blockers: Skarmory, Quagsire, Tyranitar, and Snorlax.

underlying rebuilds immediately instead of chasing the dead converter, turns
34-42:

- Golem enters after Vaporeon faints and uses Rapid Spin on turn 34, clearing
  the Spikes on underlying's side before the final material has to keep
  switching.
- On turn 35, Golem Roars Skarmory into sleeping Snorlax through Spikes.
- On turn 36, Golem Explodes into Skarmory, leaving it at 1%.
- Exeggutor finishes Skarmory on turn 38 and helps pressure Snorlax.
- Forretress, now low, switches into sleeping Snorlax on turn 40, misses Toxic
  on Quagsire on turn 41, then Explodes on turn 42 to remove Quagsire anyway.
- The sequence shows clean route handoff: Vaporeon is gone, so the next job is
  hazard removal, phaze/trade pressure, and blocker elimination for Snorlax.

Final Snorlax cashes the opened route, turns 43-47:

- Snorlax lands Lovely Kiss on Tyranitar while Tyranitar chooses Sandstorm on
  turn 43.
- Snorlax Curses once, then KOs Tyranitar with Earthquake on turns 45-46.
- melancholy0's Snorlax re-enters at 46% after Spikes and Sandstorm. Both
  Snorlax use Double-Edge on turn 47, and underlying's Snorlax wins the trade.
- The final win was not a solo Vaporeon sweep. Vaporeon opened the route;
  Golem and Forretress removed the post-Vaporeon blockers; Snorlax converted.

## Critical Turns

Turn 2:

- Forretress sets Spikes early enough that every later Snorlax, Quagsire,
  Cloyster, Tyranitar, and Skarmory entry has a cost. This pressure makes the
  later one-time trades decisive instead of merely equal.

Turn 20:

- Sleep Talk calls Growth. Vaporeon now has a route to punish both passive
  waiting and Zapdos's attempted entry.

Turn 24:

- Cloyster poisons Vaporeon but faints to Surf. The poison matters, but
  Cloyster's removal also means melancholy0 loses the Spiker that created
  underlying's hazard problem.

Turn 28:

- Zapdos tries to use Vaporeon's sleep turn for Thunderbolt pressure and is
  removed by Sleep Talk Surf. After this, Vaporeon has done enough even if it
  cannot finish the battle.

Turn 34:

- Golem uses Rapid Spin instead of trying to win immediately with damage or
  Explosion. This protects underlying's remaining material from the hazard
  clock before the final trades begin.

Turns 36 and 42:

- Golem Explosion leaves Skarmory in finishing range, and Forretress Explosion
  removes Quagsire. These trades are good because the final Snorlax route has
  already been prepared.

Turn 43:

- Lovely Kiss catches Tyranitar while it uses Sandstorm. That sleep turn lets
  Snorlax move from backup piece to final converter.

## Practical Lessons

A converter can succeed without surviving:

- Vaporeon fainted, but it removed Cloyster and Zapdos and forced Snorlax
  through multiple recovery states. Evaluate the route state after the
  converter dies before calling the route failed.

Post-breakthrough turns are cleanup contracts:

- After the first converter opens the board, the next move should name the new
  blocker map. In this game, that meant Golem Rapid Spin, Golem Roar /
  Explosion, Exeggutor finishing Skarmory, and Forretress Explosion on
  Quagsire.

Rapid Spin can be final-material preservation:

- Turn 34 is not a passive detour. Removing Spikes keeps Golem, Forretress,
  Exeggutor, and Snorlax from paying unnecessary entry tax while they execute
  the handoff.

One-time trades need a prepared recipient:

- Golem and Forretress Explosion work because Snorlax can use the simplified
  board. If the final converter did not exist, the same booms would need a
  different proof.

Do not freeze the plan at the old win condition:

- Once Vaporeon died, the correct question was not "how do I keep sweeping
  with Vaporeon?" It was "which remaining blockers stop Snorlax from winning,
  and which resources can remove them?"

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab boss fights often have one piece that opens a route and a
  different piece that finishes it. A player-side plan should treat the first
  breaker, spinner, status user, or trade as a delivery system for the final
  route, not necessarily as the final route itself.
- If a boss ace has already removed key player answers but then faints, boss
  AI should re-score from public material instead of assuming the ace's route
  failed or secretly reading the unrevealed player bench.
- Rapid Spin, phazing, Explosion, Sleep Talk, Rest, poison, weather, and Spikes
  all require local source validation before exact Gym Leader Lab transfer.
- Boss AI must not use hidden player-team knowledge to decide whether the
  post-breakthrough handoff is winning. It can use public HP, status, hazards,
  revealed moves, observed switches/faints, and source-allowed memory.

## New Notebook Lesson

After a converter dies, rebuild the blocker map before abandoning the route.
If the converter removed key blockers, the next best move may be a utility or
trade that preserves final material and hands the opened board to a different
closer.
