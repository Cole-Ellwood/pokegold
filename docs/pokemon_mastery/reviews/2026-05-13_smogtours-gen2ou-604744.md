# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-604744

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-604744

Supporting source:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-smeargle.3696816/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Useful for support-chain
sequencing, Baton Pass timing, sacrifice cascades, and endgame route delivery.
This is not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 57 turns.

Players: choolio IGLESIAS defeated soulgazer.

## Teams Revealed

choolio IGLESIAS:

- Zapdos with Hidden Power, Thunderbolt, Roar, and Protect.
- Cloyster with Toxic, Spikes, and Surf.
- Snorlax with Curse, Double-Edge, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Golem with Earthquake, Rapid Spin, and Explosion.
- Smeargle with Agility, Baton Pass, and Spore.
- Marowak with Swords Dance and Rock Slide.

soulgazer:

- Snorlax with Curse, Double-Edge, and Rest.
- Golem with Roar.
- Raikou with Thunderbolt, Rest, Sleep Talk, and Crunch.
- Zapdos with Thunder and Hidden Power.
- Cloyster with Spikes and Surf.
- Umbreon with Charm, Toxic, Moonlight, and Pursuit.

## Phase Map

Opening hazard and anchor trade, turns 1-13:

- choolio moves Zapdos out of Snorlax and uses Cloyster to poison Snorlax, set
  Spikes, and force early Rest pressure.
- Cloyster faints on turn 4, but its job has already changed the game: the
  opposing side now pays for switches, and Snorlax has been pushed into a Rest
  cycle.
- choolio's Snorlax uses the Rest window to boost, while soulgazer's Golem is
  dragged into danger and nearly removed before Roaring.

Hazard pressure into blocker mapping, turns 14-33:

- Zapdos pressures Raikou and uses Roar after Raikou Rests, dragging Golem and
  Snorlax through Spikes.
- Golem later faints on entry, showing that the early layer became real route
  progress rather than decoration.
- soulgazer sets a layer on choolio's side with Cloyster, but choolio's Golem
  removes it with Rapid Spin on turn 26. Golem is then poisoned, so its future
  value becomes more trade-like than durable.
- Umbreon contains Snorlax with Charm and Toxic, so the game cannot be won by
  simply repeating Snorlax pressure.

Support delivery into Marowak, turns 34-38:

- choolio brings Smeargle into Umbreon as Umbreon uses Moonlight.
- On turn 35, Smeargle uses Agility while soulgazer switches to Zapdos.
- On turn 36, Smeargle uses Baton Pass to bring in Marowak as Zapdos uses
  Thunder, which does not affect the incoming receiver.
- Marowak uses Swords Dance on turn 37. When soulgazer switches Raikou into the
  boosted Marowak on turn 38, Rock Slide removes Raikou.

Trade cascade into Zapdos route, turns 39-47:

- After Raikou is gone, Marowak does not need to win immediately. choolio
  switches to Zapdos, uses Roar and Protect to keep the board playable, then
  brings in Golem.
- On turn 47, Golem Explodes into Zapdos. This spends a poisoned, limited
  support piece to remove another major obstacle to choolio's own Zapdos route.

Second Smeargle job and cleanup, turns 48-57:

- Smeargle returns on Cloyster and uses Spore. The first Smeargle sequence was
  boost delivery; this one is safe-entry delivery.
- Smeargle Baton Passes to Zapdos on turn 49. Cloyster wakes immediately and
  damages Zapdos, but the entry is still good enough for the route.
- Zapdos removes Umbreon and Cloyster, Roars Snorlax after it tries to Curse,
  then finishes the last Snorlax. A turn 52 critical hit accelerates the ending,
  so the exact KO pace should not be over-credited.

## Critical Turns

Turn 3:

- Cloyster sets Spikes after poisoning Snorlax. This is the seed of the later
  route: Golem, Raikou, Umbreon, Cloyster, and Snorlax all become more expensive
  to maneuver.

Turn 15:

- Zapdos uses Roar after Raikou Rests, dragging Golem through Spikes. This is
  not random shuffling; it converts the sleeping Raikou state and existing
  layer into progress against the broader team.

Turns 34-38:

- Smeargle's Agility and Baton Pass are only good because they deliver Marowak
  into a board where the pass matters. Marowak then removes Raikou, a blocker to
  Zapdos's later route.

Turn 47:

- Golem's Explosion removes Zapdos after Golem has already spun once and has
  been poisoned. The trade is justified by remaining roles, not by Golem being
  low-value in the abstract.

Turns 48-49:

- Smeargle changes jobs. Instead of trying to rebuild the earlier boost plan, it
  uses Spore plus Baton Pass as safe-entry support for Zapdos.

Turn 54:

- Zapdos uses Roar after Snorlax wakes and Curses. Even in cleanup, choolio
  denies the last possible setup route rather than trying to brute force every
  exchange with damage.

## Practical Lessons

Support is delivery, not progress by itself:

- Smeargle's first job is not "use Agility." Its job is "make Marowak fast
  enough to remove Raikou."
- Smeargle's second job is not "use sleep." Its job is "create a Zapdos entry
  after Raikou and Zapdos are gone."

The receiver decides whether the support turn is good:

- If Marowak could not enter safely, or if Raikou were not a meaningful blocker,
  Agility would be much less valuable.
- If Zapdos could not convert after the later Spore pass, the sleep turn would
  be only temporary control.

Do not freeze a support Pokemon into one role:

- The same Smeargle supports two different routes at two different times. A
  route map should update the support role after each blocker falls.

Trade cascades need a fresh route map after every spend:

- Cloyster sets the layer, Marowak removes Raikou, Golem removes Zapdos, and
  Smeargle delivers Zapdos. Each spend makes sense only because the previous
  spend changed what remained.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab support turns should be evaluated by receiver and route, not
  by the support move's reputation. This applies to screens, speed boosts,
  Swords Dance, Dragon Dance, Encore, sleep, weather, and forced-entry support.
- Against boss support chains, name the receiver before choosing the denial
  move. A low-damage support Pokemon can be urgent if the receiver becomes hard
  to answer next turn.
- When using the player's own support, do not set up because the move is
  available. Identify the exact boss blocker the receiver will remove, and what
  backup route exists if the pass, sleep, or entry branch fails.

## New Notebook Lesson

Support is a route-delivery tool. A support turn is progress only if it creates
the board state where a receiver removes a blocker, preserves the route, or
converts the endgame.
