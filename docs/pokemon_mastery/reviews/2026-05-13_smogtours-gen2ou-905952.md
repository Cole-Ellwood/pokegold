# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-905952

Replay: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-905952

Format: vanilla GSC OU.

Players:

- underlying
- A Real Jester

Result: A Real Jester won on turn 87.

## Team Shapes

underlying revealed:

- Cloyster: Spikes / Toxic / Explosion / Surf
- Zapdos: Hidden Power / Thunderbolt / Rest / Sleep Talk
- Snorlax: Double-Edge / Earthquake / Rest / Sleep Talk
- Golem: Rapid Spin
- Gengar: Thunderbolt / Ice Punch / Hidden Power
- Jolteon: Growth / Baton Pass

A Real Jester revealed:

- Exeggutor: Psychic / Thief / Sleep Powder / Explosion
- Snorlax: Double-Edge / Surf / Rest / Sleep Talk
- Starmie: Thunder Wave / Recover
- Cloyster: Spikes / Toxic / Surf / Explosion
- Zapdos
- Rhydon: Earthquake

## Phase Map

Opening, turns 1-18:

- underlying gets Spikes on turn 4.
- A Real Jester gets Spikes on turn 8.
- Exeggutor steals Zapdos's Leftovers early, and Starmie paralyzes Zapdos.
- underlying's Sleep Talk Snorlax removes Rhydon on turns 14-15.
- underlying's Cloyster Explodes on A Real Jester's Zapdos on turn 18.

Midgame, turns 19-44:

- A Real Jester leans on Surf RestTalk Snorlax as the long-game anchor.
- underlying uses Jolteon Growth into Baton Pass on turns 40-41.
- Growth-passed Gengar almost erases Starmie through Recover, then KOs it on
  turn 44, but Starmie paralyzes Gengar first.

Conversion attempt, turns 45-64:

- A Real Jester's Cloyster resets Spikes on turn 47 and later Explodes into
  Jolteon on turn 59.
- underlying freezes A Real Jester's Snorlax on turn 54, creating a huge
  apparent endgame opening.
- Exeggutor forces a severe preservation question. It KOs sleeping Zapdos on
  turn 63, then Explodes into underlying's Snorlax on turn 64.

Endgame, turns 65-87:

- underlying has only paralyzed Gengar left against A Real Jester's Surf
  RestTalk Snorlax.
- Freeze delays Snorlax until turn 75, but Rest converts the frozen state into
  a new sleep cycle.
- Paralysis on Gengar gives several lost turns, and Surf Snorlax eventually
  wins.

## Critical Turns

Turn 18:

- underlying's Cloyster Explosion on Zapdos is a clean route-conversion trade.
  Cloyster had already set Spikes and poisoned opposing Cloyster; Zapdos was a
  major pressure piece. This is a good example of spending the hazard setter
  after its main job is done.

Turns 40-44:

- Growth Baton Pass to Gengar is a deliberate route handoff from generic
  Zapdos/Snorlax pressure into special wallbreaking. The pass works because it
  converts immediately: Starmie is forced to Recover on turn 43 and then dies
  on turn 44.
- The cost is also real: Starmie's Thunder Wave means Gengar becomes a damaged,
  paralysis-exposed endgame piece instead of a clean closer.

Turn 54:

- Gengar freezes A Real Jester's Snorlax. This is a good outcome, but not a
  complete route by itself. The frozen Snorlax still has Rest if it gets a
  turn at low enough HP, and underlying still has to preserve enough material
  to punish the freeze before Rest resets the state.

Turn 64:

- Candidate earliest irreversible issue from underlying's side: Snorlax stays
  in and is removed by Exeggutor Explosion.
- Public state before the turn: A Real Jester has Exeggutor at low HP plus a
  frozen Snorlax. underlying has Snorlax and paralyzed Gengar. If Snorlax
  survives, underlying has a much stronger route against frozen Snorlax.
- The hard question is whether Gengar should absorb or threaten the Explosion
  branch. Switching Gengar risks Psychic or Sleep Powder branches, but staying
  in loses the healthier endgame piece to the most obvious one-time resource.
- This is not a certain blunder without damage and sleep-clause review, but it
  is the turn that best matches the cookbook's preservation tests: do not spend
  the piece that makes the apparent winning route real.

Turns 75-87:

- Rest breaks the freeze-only plan. Once A Real Jester's Snorlax Rests on turn
  75, underlying's remaining route is no longer "frozen Snorlax cannot move";
  it becomes "paralyzed Gengar must beat Surf RestTalk Snorlax through sleep
  cycles." That route is much worse.

## Recipes Reinforced

Explosion and sacrifice:

- Explosion is strongest when the lost role is already spent and the target is
  a live route blocker. Cloyster on Zapdos fits. Exeggutor on Snorlax also fits
  from A Real Jester's side because it removes the piece that could exploit the
  frozen Snorlax.

Primary-to-backup route handoff:

- Growth Pass Gengar was the correct kind of handoff because it immediately
  changed the Starmie route. After Gengar was paralyzed, the plan needed a
  second handoff back to preserving Snorlax as the more stable endgame piece.

Freeze/status outcome discipline:

- A freeze is not a plan by itself when the target has Rest and the attacker is
  vulnerable to paralysis. The route is only real if the player can prevent the
  frozen target from safely reaching Rest.

Preserve the piece that makes the route real:

- The frozen opposing Snorlax made underlying look favored, but the route still
  needed underlying's Snorlax as material. Once that Snorlax was traded for
  Exeggutor, paralyzed Gengar had to do too much alone.

## Romhack Transfer

- Boss fights can produce the same false signal: a status or lucky branch looks
  like the fight is solved, but the route still needs the correct material to
  convert. If a boss anchor is frozen, asleep, confused, locked, or recharging,
  ask what prevents it from using Rest, switching, stalling the clock, or
  forcing the converter into a bad state.
- Do not treat the status event as the win. Treat it as a temporary route
  discount that must be cashed out before the boss recovers the state.

## New Notebook Lesson

Temporary disable is not ownership. When status, freeze, sleep, confusion,
recharge, or lock-in creates an apparent opening, immediately identify the
piece that converts that opening and preserve it until the route is actually
closed.
