# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-891166

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-891166

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for general GSC
study, not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 27 turns. This is shorter than the target 30-plus-turn review standard,
so it is a warm-up review rather than a full mastery artifact.

## Teams Revealed

PDC:

- Snorlax with Lovely Kiss, Earthquake.
- Cloyster with Toxic, Surf, Spikes.
- Raikou with Thunder, Hidden Power.
- Vaporeon with Surf.
- Golem with Earthquake.
- Exeggutor with Giga Drain, Psychic.

Glfgno7:

- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Sleep Talk.
- Cloyster with Toxic, Spikes, Explosion.
- Zapdos with Thunder.
- Tyranitar with Earthquake, Rock Slide.
- Golem with Earthquake.
- Gengar with Hypnosis, Ice Punch.

## Route Summary

PDC's winning route became:

```text
Lovely Kiss opposing Snorlax
-> trade early Spikes
-> use Exeggutor to punish the sleeping Snorlax / Tyranitar / Golem cluster
-> keep Raikou healthy enough to close against Zapdos, low Snorlax, and Gengar
```

Glfgno7's plausible route became:

```text
Use Spikes and Zapdos pressure
-> force PDC's Raikou / Snorlax / Golem answers through hazard damage
-> use Cloyster Explosion to remove Snorlax
-> finish with Zapdos or Gengar if Raikou is weakened
```

The route failed because Tyranitar and Snorlax were heavily degraded before
Explosion happened, and Raikou stayed healthy enough to become the cleaner.

## Critical Turns

Turn 1: PDC's Snorlax used Lovely Kiss after taking Double-Edge. This converted
the opponent's Snorlax from central stabilizer into a long-term liability that
kept being forced through Spikes.

Turn 5: PDC set Spikes while Zapdos switched in. This was not "Spikes because
Spikes are good"; it created the clock that later made Snorlax, Tyranitar,
Cloyster, Golem, and Gengar punishable on every switch.

Turns 13-19: Exeggutor became the conversion piece. It came in on Golem's
Earthquake, punished the sleeping Snorlax switch, then punished Tyranitar's
attempt to cover it. The important lesson is that the route was built by prior
sleep and hazard state; Exeggutor was not just clicking favorable attacks in a
vacuum.

Turn 20: Gengar's Hypnosis missed into PDC's Snorlax. After the sleep attempt
failed, Glfgno7 changed route into Cloyster and Explosion instead of continuing
the sleep script. That is the right kind of re-score behavior even though the
overall position remained hard.

Turn 22: Cloyster's Explosion removed PDC's Snorlax. This had a named purpose:
remove a bulky stabilizer and try to open Zapdos/Gengar pressure. The issue is
that the route opened was probably too late because PDC's Raikou was still
healthy and Glfgno7's own Snorlax/Tyranitar/Cloyster resources were gone or
nearly gone.

Turn 24: Raikou crit Zapdos with Hidden Power. This accelerated the finish, but
the review should not blame the game only on that crit. Before the crit, PDC had
a healthy Raikou, hazards, Glfgno7's Snorlax in KO range after Spikes, no
Tyranitar, no Cloyster, and a Gengar that still had to fight Raikou.

## Practical Lessons

- Early sleep is strongest when it disables a central stabilizer and the rest of
  the game is organized around repeatedly making that Pokemon pay for re-entry.
- Spikes matter when they become a route clock. Here they turned ordinary
  switches into future KO ranges and helped convert the sleeping Snorlax into a
  liability.
- A Pokemon becomes the breaker because of the state around it. Exeggutor was
  dangerous because Snorlax was asleep, Tyranitar and Golem were exposed, and
  every grounded switch paid hazard damage.
- Explosion needs timing as well as target value. Removing Snorlax was coherent,
  but by the time Cloyster traded itself, the remaining route no longer looked
  strong enough without extra luck or a stronger Zapdos position.
- Do not erase route errors with outcome labels. The Raikou crit mattered, but
  the pre-crit board already showed PDC's long-term plan had mostly converted.

## Cookbook Update Candidate

Add to future hazard/sleep recipes:

```text
Sleep plus Spikes is not just "free turns." It is a re-entry tax on the slept
piece. The route is strongest when the slept target is a central stabilizer and
the opponent must keep reintroducing it through hazards before it wakes or can
Rest/Sleep Talk its way back into relevance.
```
