# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-451060

Replay: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-451060

Source team context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-sample-teams-breakdown-updated-july-2023.3688523/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for general GSC
study, not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 163 turns.

Players: Lavos defeated McMeghan.

## Why This Replay Matters

The Smogon sample-team note for Heracross + Explosions describes an
Explosion-heavy team that can still play slowly and methodically. This replay
is the proof pattern: Lavos has several one-time trade tools, but the win comes
from delaying them until the route contract is clear. Golem in particular spends
most of the game spinning, phazing, pivoting, and attacking instead of blowing
up early.

## Team Shapes

McMeghan revealed:

- Snorlax: Thunder, Double-Edge, Rest.
- Suicune: Sleep Talk, Ice Beam, Surf.
- Skarmory: Whirlwind, Rest, Drill Peck, Curse.
- Golem: Rapid Spin, Earthquake, Roar, Explosion.
- Cloyster: Spikes, Toxic.
- Raikou: Thunderbolt, Hidden Power, Roar, Rest.

Lavos revealed:

- Cloyster: Spikes, Surf, Toxic.
- Exeggutor: Sleep Powder, Psychic, Giga Drain.
- Raikou: Thunder, Hidden Power, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Golem: Roar, Earthquake, Rapid Spin.
- Snorlax: Curse, Body Slam, Self-Destruct.
- Heracross: Megahorn, Rest, Sleep Talk, Curse.

## Phase Map

Opening control, turns 1-18:

- Lavos sets Spikes immediately with Cloyster on turn 1.
- Exeggutor puts Suicune to sleep on turn 3, making McMeghan's bulky pivot less
  reliable and giving the Spikes layer more time to matter.
- Lavos uses Golem for switching and Roar pressure by turn 16. This is already
  a warning against "low-commitment boom" thinking: Golem has active jobs before
  it has a trade target.
- McMeghan spins the first layer away on turn 18, so Lavos has not won the
  hazard war yet.

Two-sided hazard control, turns 25-60:

- Both Cloyster use Spikes on turn 25.
- McMeghan's Golem spins Lavos's layer away on turn 28.
- Lavos's Golem removes McMeghan's layer on turn 54. This is one of the
  important route facts of the game: if Golem had been spent earlier, Lavos
  would have fewer ways to keep the long game from becoming a one-sided hazard
  tax.
- Raikou and Heracross absorb pressure through Rest and Sleep Talk rather than
  forcing a fast trade.

Long middle game, turns 61-119:

- The game becomes a repeated Rest, Sleep Talk, Spikes, phazing, and spin fight.
- Lavos repeatedly re-establishes Spikes with Cloyster, while both sides keep
  checking whether the hazard layer can survive long enough to convert.
- Lavos's Golem spins again on turn 108. Its value is still not its Explosion
  button; it is the ability to reset hazard pressure while preserving enough HP
  to remain a late-game attacker.

Delayed trade conversion, turns 120-133:

- McMeghan's Snorlax is forced into repeated Rest cycles while Spikes and
  switching keep narrowing its freedom.
- On turn 133, Lavos finally uses Self-Destruct with Snorlax, trading Snorlax
  for McMeghan's Snorlax after the opposing anchor has just woken and attacked.
- This is the delayed-contract moment: the trade removes a central stabilizer
  after Lavos has preserved Raikou, Heracross, Golem, and Exeggutor for the
  post-trade map.

Closing sequence, turns 144-163:

- McMeghan's Golem Explodes on Lavos's Cloyster on turn 144. That removes the
  setter, but it does not remove Lavos's remaining route pieces.
- McMeghan sets Spikes again on turn 147, but Lavos's low Golem still has a job:
  it uses Rapid Spin on turn 157.
- On turn 161, that same Golem attacks McMeghan's sleeping Raikou and removes
  it with help from a critical hit. The critical hit affects the result branch,
  but preserving Golem made that branch available.
- Lavos's Raikou then removes Suicune on turn 163, and McMeghan forfeits.

## Critical Turns

Turn 3:

- Sleep Powder does not start a fixed setup script. It weakens one of
  McMeghan's stabilizing pieces and gives the Spikes route room to develop.
  Lavos still has to keep re-scoring because McMeghan has Rapid Spin and
  RestTalk counterplay.

Turn 54:

- Lavos's Golem uses Rapid Spin instead of trading itself. This preserves the
  route. The Explosion-heavy team needs Golem's non-trade job more than it needs
  an immediate one-for-one.

Turn 108:

- Golem spins again. The repeated use proves the broader point: a one-time
  trade user can have a recurring role that is more valuable than a tempting
  sacrifice.

Turn 133:

- Snorlax's Self-Destruct is not a panic button. It is a route trade after
  McMeghan's Snorlax has been cycled, forced to Rest, and positioned as a
  stabilizer worth removing.

Turn 157:

- Low-health Golem still removes Lavos's side of Spikes. Low HP did not make it
  expendable because its remaining job was narrow but decisive.

## Extracted Lesson

Explosion and Self-Destruct should be treated as contracts, not instincts.

Before spending the user, write the contract:

```text
Target or route blocker to remove:
Route opened after the trade:
Recurring job lost by spending the user:
Why that job no longer matters:
Remaining pieces that convert after the trade:
Opponent countertrade or pivot that punishes the click:
```

If the user still has a live recurring job - spin, phaze, absorb, force Rest,
set hazards, scout a branch, or attack a key target - preserving it can be the
stronger route even on an Explosion-heavy team.

## Boss-Battle Transfer

Gym Leader Lab has many bosses with one-time or semi-one-time resources:
Explosion, Self-Destruct, Destiny Bond, Focus Band, priority revenge, phazing
PP, and low-health pivots. Do not treat those pieces as expendable because they
are damaged or because a trade move is available.

For boss advice, ask:

1. Does this piece still have a narrow live job after this turn?
2. Is the target the actual blocker to the win route, or only a valuable
   Pokemon?
3. If we spend the piece now, what answers the next boss route?
4. If we wait, what additional target or state would make the trade cleaner?
5. Does the boss have a pivot, status, setup, Protect-like move, or sacrifice
   branch that makes the trade fail?
