# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-917190

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-917190

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for GSC strategy
study, not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 63 turns.

Players: aminita$$ defeated Siatam.

## Teams Revealed

aminita$$:

- Zapdos with Thunder, Hidden Power, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Vaporeon with Surf, Sleep Talk.
- Forretress with Spikes, Explosion.
- Snorlax with Curse, Body Slam, Lovely Kiss, Earthquake.
- Steelix with Roar.
- Gengar with Ice Punch, Psychic, Explosion.

Siatam:

- Jynx with Lovely Kiss, Thief, Ice Beam, Psychic.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Earthquake, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Zapdos with Thunderbolt, Reflect, Whirlwind.
- Forretress with Spikes, Explosion.
- Gengar with Thunderbolt, Ice Punch.
- Starmie with Substitute, Confuse Ray, Surf, Nightmare.

## Route Summary

aminita$$'s route became:

```text
Absorb Jynx sleep with Vaporeon
-> get Spikes with Forretress despite paralysis
-> use Snorlax / Steelix / Zapdos to keep Siatam's Snorlax from becoming safe
-> trade Snorlax and Forretress resources through Explosion pressure
-> use Gengar Explosion to remove Zapdos
-> close with RestTalk Zapdos against low Jynx and Starmie
```

Siatam's route became:

```text
Use Jynx to disable Vaporeon and pressure aminita's defensive switches
-> establish own Spikes with Forretress
-> stop Snorlax setup with Forretress Explosion
-> block Forretress Explosion with Gengar
-> trade down until Nightmare / Substitute / Confuse Ray Starmie can endgame
```

The game became an endgame question: can RestTalk Zapdos remain healthy enough
to beat Starmie's Substitute cycle after Gengar removes opposing Zapdos?

## Critical Turns

Turn 3: Siatam's Jynx slept Vaporeon. aminita treated the sleeping Vaporeon as
a useful absorber rather than dead material; it later took Jynx attacks and
used Sleep Talk Surf to keep Jynx and Zapdos honest.

Turn 6: Forretress took Thunderbolt, became paralyzed, and still set Spikes.
This was a high-value resource trade because Spikes shaped nearly every grounded
switch after it: Jynx, Snorlax, Forretress, Gengar, and Starmie all paid the
entry tax.

Turn 15: Siatam answered with Forretress Spikes. From this point, both players'
switches had a visible cost, which made preservation and sacrifice sequencing
much sharper.

Turns 26-31: aminita's Snorlax paralyzed Siatam's Snorlax, Cursed as it Rested,
then caught the Forretress switch with Lovely Kiss. The sleep window was useful
but temporary: Forretress woke on turn 31 and Exploded, removing Snorlax before
it could convert the boost. This is the exact anti-script lesson: sleep created
a line, but the line had to respect wake and Explosion branches.

Turn 33: Siatam switched Gengar into aminita's low Forretress Explosion,
blanking the trade. This is a clean hidden-branch example. Explosion may be a
good route-conversion tool, but a revealed or plausible Ghost can turn it into
lost material.

Turns 37-39: aminita's Zapdos converted the earlier hazard work by forcing
Siatam's sleeping Snorlax through Spikes and then KOing it with Thunder. This
matches the GSC Spikes principle: Spikes matter because they make Rest loops
and defensive re-entries fail at the margin.

Turns 41-42: aminita's Gengar beat opposing Gengar with Psychic, then used
Explosion to remove Zapdos. This Explosion had a named endgame purpose: remove
the electric mirror / pressure piece so aminita's own Zapdos could fight the
remaining Jynx and Starmie.

Turns 44-63: Siatam's Starmie created a real endgame route with Substitute,
Confuse Ray, Surf, and Nightmare. aminita Rests with Zapdos on turn 48, gets
Nightmared on turn 49, then sacrifices Steelix to reset the bad active-state
condition and bring Zapdos back for the final cycle. Starmie repeatedly spends
HP on Substitute; because Zapdos keeps breaking the substitutes, the defensive
endgame becomes a self-taxing clock for Starmie despite Thunder misses and
confusion variance.

## Practical Lessons

- Sleep can create a setup window without making the setup safe. The Forretress
  wake into Explosion shows why every sleep line needs a catastrophe branch.
- Spikes convert offense by margins, not just by raw damage. Snorlax survived
  many individual attacks, but each re-entry through Spikes made the RestTalk
  loop less sustainable.
- A low-HP or statused Pokemon is not automatically expendable. Vaporeon
  remained useful as a sleep absorber and Jynx buffer; Steelix later became a
  useful sacrifice because its remaining role was smaller than the need to reset
  Zapdos's endgame state.
- Explosion has two separate questions: "what route does it open if it lands?"
  and "what branch punishes it if the opponent refuses the target?" The turn 33
  Gengar switch punished the second question.
- In endgames, a move like Substitute can be both protection and self-tax. If
  the opponent can consistently break it while staying healthy, the Substitute
  user may be spending its own win condition.

## Cookbook Update Candidate

Add a recipe for sacrifice as active-state reset:

```text
A sacrifice can be correct when it removes a bad active-state condition
Nightmare, confusion, trapping pressure, sleep-turn exposure, or forced
positioning, and brings back the actual win-condition piece into a cleaner
cycle. This is only legal if the sacrificed piece has no irreplaceable remaining
role.
```

## Still Unverified

- I did not run damage simulations for the exact Zapdos versus Starmie endgame.
- This is vanilla Showdown GSC; hazard, sleep, type, and move behavior must not
  be promoted to Gym Leader Lab without romhack verification.
- The turn 33 Forretress Explosion may have been acceptable if Forretress had no
  better route at 21%, but the review still tags the Ghost branch as the
  central lesson.
