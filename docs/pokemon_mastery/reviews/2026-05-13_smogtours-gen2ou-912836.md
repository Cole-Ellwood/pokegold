## Phase Map

Opening status and first layer, turns 1-5:

- Zapdos paralyzes zinc3's Snorlax on turn 1 while taking heavy Double-Edge
  damage.
- Forretress sets Spikes on turn 3 after entering Snorlax.
- zinc3 answers with Cloyster Spikes on turn 5, so both sides have a switch
  tax, but seanobiwan has already shown the first hazard tempo.

Starmie establishes spin value, turns 8-18:

- Starmie enters through Spikes on turn 8 and takes Cross Chop plus hazard
  damage, but it remains healthy enough to act.
- On turn 9, Starmie paralyzes Zapdos. The spinner is not only a remover; it
  also damages the opponent's speed route.
- On turn 18, Starmie uses Rapid Spin into Zapdos, clears seanobiwan's side of
  Spikes, and leaves at 44% after Leftovers. The spin preserves future switching
  while Starmie remains available for a second spin later.

Re-Spikes without retention, turns 19-24:

- Tyranitar removes Machamp on turn 20 after Machamp is forced through Spikes.
- Cloyster sets Spikes again on turn 21, but immediately faints to Tyranitar.
  The layer is real, but the setter is gone and Starmie is still alive.
- Steelix Roars Zapdos into Starmie on turn 23, so the new layer does create
  one concrete Starmie tax.
- On turn 24, Starmie uses Rapid Spin into 8% Alakazam, KOs it, and clears the
  layer again. This is the key teaching turn: Rapid Spin is also the progress
  move when it removes hazards and takes a fainted target.

Final conversion, turns 25-27:

- Zapdos misses Thunder into Forretress on turn 25.
- Forretress Explodes on Zapdos on turn 26, removing zinc3's healthiest
  remaining route piece.
- Zapdos then KOs the low paralyzed Snorlax through Spikes on turn 27, and
  zinc3 forfeits.

## Critical Turns

Turn 18:

- Starmie's first Rapid Spin is not just a housekeeping move. It preserves
  seanobiwan's switch loop while Starmie remains alive and able to repeat the
  job later.

Turn 21:

- Cloyster's second Spikes layer has a narrow route: create immediate phazing
  or switch-tax value before Starmie spins again. Because Cloyster faints that
  turn and Starmie survives, this is not a long hazard-retention plan.

Turn 24:

- Starmie spins into low Alakazam, clears the layer, and KOs the active. A
  spinner that can remove hazards while taking a KO has turned the support move
  into active progress.

Turn 26:

- Forretress has already set Spikes and watched Starmie control the other side
  of the hazard war, so Explosion into Zapdos is a route conversion rather than
  a premature loss of utility.

## Extracted Lesson

Do not evaluate Rapid Spin as only a defensive support move. In a hazard war,
ask whether the spin turn also does one of these:

```text
KOs or forces the active target.
Preserves a switch loop that was about to collapse.
Lets the spinner survive for another removal turn.
Buys entry for the next breaker or sacrifice.
Denies the opponent's freshly reset hazard before it converts.
```

If the spinner can remove hazards and make immediate progress, the anti-spin
plan must act before or on that turn. Re-setting hazards without a retention or
conversion plan can be only temporary pressure.

## Boss-Battle Transfer

Gym Leader Lab has three-layer Spikes and Rapid Spin clears all layers, so this
lesson becomes more important, not less. Against bosses with Corsola, Dewgong,
Tentacruel, Electrode, Starmie, or another spinner, do not assume Rapid Spin is
a passive turn. If the spinner can KO a low-health active, force a switch, or
survive comfortably, the spin turn may be the boss's progress move.

Before advising hazards into a boss spinner, ask:

1. Can the spinner enter safely?
2. Can Rapid Spin also KO, force out, or stabilize against the current active?
3. If the layer is removed immediately, what did the hazard turn actually gain?
4. Can the player punish the spin before it resolves, or only after the route
   already changed?
