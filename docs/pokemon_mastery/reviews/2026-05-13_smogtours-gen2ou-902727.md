# Battle Review: smogtours-gen2ou-902727

Source:

- Replay log: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-902727.log
- Tournament source: SPL XVII GSC discussion,
  https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/spl-xvii-gsc-discussion.3775984/

Players: M Dragon defeated A Real Jester.

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is expert-play study, not
Gym Leader Lab mechanics evidence.

Length: 80 turns.

## Revealed Structure

M Dragon:

- Jynx with Thief / Lovely Kiss / Ice Beam.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge.
- Cloyster with Spikes / Surf.
- Gengar with Ice Punch / Thief / Explosion.
- Zapdos with Thunderbolt / Hidden Power / Whirlwind.
- Sixth unrevealed in the reviewed log.

A Real Jester:

- Cloyster with Spikes / Rapid Spin / Protect.
- Raikou with Thunderbolt.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge / Toxic / Flamethrower / Rest.
- Misdreavus with Toxic / Rest / Sleep Talk / Psychic.
- Blissey with Heal Bell / Light Screen / Soft-Boiled.
- Skarmory with Toxic / Rest / Sleep Talk / Sand Attack.

## Phase Review

Opening theft, sleep, and hazard contract, turns 1-18:

- Jynx steals Raikou's Leftovers on turn 1, then sleeps Raikou on turn 4.
- Jynx also pressures Snorlax with Ice Beam and briefly freezes it, but the
  freeze cures immediately. The useful result is still damage plus tempo, not a
  permanent disabled-state route.
- Cloyster sets Spikes on turn 8, but the opposing Cloyster both sets Spikes
  and removes M Dragon's layer with Rapid Spin on turns 12-13.
- Jynx and Cloyster are poisoned, so the early pressure has an expiration date.

Re-establishing the layer before the reset hub takes over, turns 19-31:

- M Dragon sets Spikes again on turn 19 while A Real Jester's Cloyster is low.
- Blissey uses Heal Bell on turn 28, curing Raikou's sleep and the poison on
  A Real Jester's side.
- Blissey uses Light Screen on turn 31. M Dragon does not keep treating Zapdos
  as a pure damage attacker into the screen; Zapdos Whirlwinds and drags the
  low Cloyster through Spikes.

Zapdos converts Spikes through forced switches, turns 36-50:

- Blissey and Misdreavus can repeatedly reset damage with Soft-Boiled, Rest,
  Sleep Talk, and Light Screen support.
- Zapdos uses Whirlwind repeatedly into the Spikes layer, turning the reset hub
  into an entry-tax problem instead of a direct Thunderbolt damage race.
- Raikou faints to dragged Spikes on turn 41, and Misdreavus eventually faints
  to dragged Spikes on turn 50.
- This is not random shuffling. It is the point where the layer, the phazer,
  and prior chip finally become one route.

Removing the recovery hub directly, turns 53-55:

- Gengar enters into Blissey, steals Leftovers with Thief on turn 54, then
  Explodes on turn 55.
- The Explosion is not a generic low-HP trade. Blissey is the piece turning
  status, Light Screen, Soft-Boiled, and special bulk into reset turns.
- After Blissey falls, Zapdos can resume phazing and attacking without the same
  cleric/screen reset behind every exchange.

Endgame conversion, turns 56-80:

- Zapdos keeps using Whirlwind while Spikes punish Snorlax and Cloyster.
- Toxic eventually lands on Zapdos on turn 67, forcing a finite clock on M
  Dragon's phaze route.
- M Dragon switches from Zapdos to poisoned Cloyster on turn 71. Cloyster uses
  its remaining HP to damage Skarmory and force Rest/Sleep Talk branches.
- Zapdos returns to KO Skarmory on turn 77, and Snorlax finishes the sleeping
  opposing Snorlax on turns 79-80.

## Critical Turns

Turn 19:

- Setting Spikes again matters because the first layer was spun away. This is
  a good hazard-contract turn: M Dragon re-establishes the one external clock
  that can make Blissey / Misdreavus / Skarmory resets expensive.

Turn 28:

- Blissey's Heal Bell resets Raikou's sleep and the side's status problems.
  Any plan that counted only "Raikou is asleep" or "poison is ticking" must be
  revised here.

Turn 31:

- Blissey sets Light Screen. Zapdos answers with Whirlwind instead of treating
  reduced special damage as the whole board. The better question becomes
  "which forced switch pays Spikes and loses recovery tempo?"

Turns 36-42:

- Zapdos repeatedly forces entries. Raikou is dragged through Spikes and faints
  on turn 41 after earlier Jynx and hazard pressure.
- This is the cleanest point in the replay where forced switching becomes
  damage, not delay.

Turn 50:

- Misdreavus faints to dragged Spikes. Earlier Thunderbolt damage did not solve
  RestTalk by itself, but it made the later forced-entry route decisive.

Turns 54-55:

- Gengar uses Thief on Blissey, then Explosion. Removing the reset hub changes
  the value of every later Zapdos and Snorlax turn.

Turns 67-71:

- Zapdos becomes poisoned and can no longer be treated as an unlimited phaze
  engine. M Dragon switches to Cloyster and lets that degraded piece spend its
  remaining HP against Skarmory.

## Practical Lessons

Reset hubs must be broken as systems:

- Blissey was not only a special wall. It was a cleric, screen setter, recovery
  user, and soft reset for several earlier advantages.
- Against that kind of hub, direct damage may be useful only if it combines
  with hazards, forced switches, item removal, or one-time trades.

Forced switches become damage only when the entry ledger is live:

- Zapdos Whirlwind was strong because Spikes were up, the dragged targets had
  prior chip, and the reset hub could not erase every forced entry forever.
- Without Spikes or a favorable target map, the same Whirlwind pattern could
  have only rerolled into Blissey / Skarmory / Snorlax resets.

Revise status plans after Heal Bell:

- Raikou being asleep was valuable until Heal Bell changed the public state.
  The correct reaction is not frustration; it is re-scoring what still pressures
  the cured target.

One-time trades rise when they remove the reset function:

- Gengar Explosion on Blissey was a route trade because it removed the piece
  that kept invalidating special pressure, status, and screen turns.

Poison can force route handoff:

- Once Zapdos was poisoned, the repeated Whirlwind route gained a deadline.
  Switching to Cloyster on turn 71 showed the plan revision: spend a degraded
  support piece before returning to Zapdos for the forced KO.

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab bosses often have reset hubs in smaller forms: Recover,
  RestTalk, Heal Bell-like curing, Safeguard, screens, Rapid Spin, Protect,
  phazing, or weather reset.
- Do not attack a reset hub only by its visible HP. Ask which function keeps
  erasing progress, then choose the move that breaks that function: hazards,
  forced switches, item removal, status timing, direct KO pressure, or a
  controlled sacrifice.
- When transferring to the romhack, verify the local mechanics first: screen
  duration, cure behavior, Rapid Spin, Roar/Whirlwind timing, Explosion damage,
  item effects, and type/passive interactions are not guaranteed by vanilla GSC
  memory.

## New Notebook Lesson

When a recovery, cleric, screen, or spin hub keeps resetting the visible
exchange, stop measuring progress by immediate damage. Build a reset-hub
ledger: which function resets the route, what external clock taxes it, what
one-time trade can remove it, and when the control route must be handed off
because its user is now on a poison or HP clock.
