# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-912653

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-912653

Source thread context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-winter-seasonal-8-round-4.3778203/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Use this for hazard-retention and
spinblock timing lessons, not as romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 70 turns.

Players: Cakeconnesseur defeated JensenDale.

## Revealed Pieces

JensenDale:

- Cloyster with Spikes, Surf, Toxic.
- Gengar with Thunderbolt, Hypnosis, Ice Punch.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Earthquake, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Zapdos with Thunder, Thunder Wave, Whirlwind.
- Tyranitar with Screech, Rock Slide.
- Machamp with Cross Chop, Hidden Power.

Cakeconnesseur:

- Raikou with Thunder, Roar, Rest.
- Skarmory with Toxic, Whirlwind.
- Starmie with Rapid Spin, Psychic, Recover, Surf.
- Forretress with Rapid Spin, Spikes.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Earthquake, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Misdreavus with Hypnosis.

## Phase Map

Early layer and immediate removal, turns 5-11:

- JensenDale brings Cloyster into Skarmory and sets Spikes on turn 6.
- Cakeconnesseur reveals Starmie on turn 8, then Rapid Spin on turn 9,
  removing the layer while Cloyster's Toxic misses.
- Cloyster uses its final useful turn to set Spikes again on turn 10 before
  poison KOs it.
- Gengar enters after Cloyster faints. The re-set layer is only useful because
  the spinblocker can now threaten the next removal turn.

Midgame retention by actual positioning, turns 17-26:

- Starmie enters again on turn 17 and recovers on turn 18, but JensenDale
  brings in Gengar, forcing Starmie out on turn 19.
- Forretress later enters and clicks Rapid Spin on turn 22. JensenDale switches
  Gengar into the spin, and Rapid Spin fails into Ghost immunity.
- This is the cleanest lesson of the replay: hazard retention is not "we have a
  Ghost." Retention is "the Ghost can enter the spin turn and the punish branch
  is acceptable."

Counter-hazard and phaze pressure, turns 35-40:

- Forretress eventually sets Spikes on JensenDale's side on turn 36.
- Raikou's Roar on turn 38 drags Gengar through those Spikes, then Gengar lands
  Hypnosis on Snorlax on turn 39.
- The hazard map is now two-sided: JensenDale still has Spikes on the opponent,
  but Cakeconnesseur has started taxing the same Gengar/Snorlax/Zapdos routes.

Late spin and failed damage conversion, turns 41-48:

- Machamp forces out Snorlax and then KOs Misdreavus on turn 42 after Hypnosis
  misses and Hidden Power crits.
- Starmie comes in at 88%, heals to 94%, and uses Rapid Spin into Machamp on
  turn 43. The layer is removed.
- Machamp's Hidden Power chunks Starmie to 49%, but Starmie recovers repeatedly
  and eventually KOs Machamp on turn 48.
- The problem is not that JensenDale lacked a Ghost in the party. Gengar was
  alive. The problem is that the spin turn happened into Machamp, and the
  chosen punish did not cross the recovery or KO endpoint before Starmie reset.

Endgame after spin, turns 49-70:

- Without JensenDale's Spikes, Starmie, Skarmory, Snorlax, and Raikou can keep
  resetting positions more cheaply.
- Starmie and Skarmory force recovery, Toxic, and Whirlwind pressure while
  JensenDale's Tyranitar and Gengar are taxed by Cakeconnesseur's Spikes.
- Gengar finally falls to Snorlax Earthquake on turn 70, and JensenDale
  forfeits.

## Critical Turns

Turn 10:

- Cloyster re-sets Spikes even though poison will KO it.
- This is not generic layer autopilot. It is a final support job: create the
  layer, then hand off to Gengar immediately.

Turn 22:

- Gengar switches into Forretress's Rapid Spin and blocks it.
- This confirms the retention plan. The hazard route is live because the
  spinblocker is on the spin turn, not because it exists in the builder.

Turn 43:

- Starmie uses Rapid Spin into Machamp and clears the layer.
- Machamp damages Starmie, but not enough to deny Recover or remove Starmie.
  The layer is gone and the spinner remains a reset hub.

Turns 44-48:

- Starmie shows why "punish the spinner" needs an endpoint. It Recovers through
  repeated Hidden Power damage and then KOs Machamp.
- If the punish does not KO, force Rest/recovery into a worse branch, status,
  trap, or create a new converter, the spin turn still belongs to the spinner.

## Practical Lessons

A spinblocker is an entry condition, not a roster checkbox:

```text
Who blocks Spin:
Can it enter the actual spin turn:
What does it take on the wrong branch:
What does it do if the spinner attacks or recovers:
Does blocking Spin preserve a route before the next reset:
```

Re-setting a temporary layer can be correct when the handoff is immediate:

- Turn 10 is valuable because Cloyster's last support action creates a layer
  and Gengar enters right away.
- The same move would be weaker if Starmie could immediately spin again into a
  non-Ghost target or if no forced handoff existed.

Damaging the spinner after Spin is not automatically retention:

- Turn 43 removes the layer before Machamp's punish.
- The punish must then be judged as a new route: does it remove Starmie, deny
  recovery, create a forced KO, or open a cleaner?
- Here the answer is no; Starmie recovers and keeps its reset role.

Do not overfit to the miss/crit branch:

- Misdreavus missing Hypnosis and dying to a critical Hidden Power matters, but
  it is not the central hazard lesson.
- The central lesson is that Starmie's later Rapid Spin resolved into Machamp,
  not Gengar, and the damage branch did not convert.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab Rapid Spin clears all local Spikes layers, so the turn-43
  failure mode is even more severe at two or three layers.
- A local Ghost or spinblocker only counts as retention if it can enter the
  spinner safely under local type/passive/damage rules.
- If the player chooses to attack a boss spinner instead of blocking Spin, the
  attack must be checked against recovery, speed, priority, Focus Band, type
  passives, and whether the spinner survives to reset the hazard route anyway.
- Do not import vanilla one-layer damage into the romhack. Import the route
  question: did the layer survive until conversion, and if not, did the punish
  create an equal or better route?

## New Notebook Lesson

Hazard retention is a state, not a team label. A Ghost in the party, a spinner
on the roster, or a planned punish matters only if it can act on the removal
turn and the post-turn endpoint is favorable.
