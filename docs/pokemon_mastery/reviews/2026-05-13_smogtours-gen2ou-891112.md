## Phase Review

The opening creates clocks instead of immediate KOs, turns 1-33:

- Fear leads Forretress into Snorlax and gets early Toxic pressure while
  melancholy0's Snorlax uses Curse.
- Fear's Skarmory uses Whirlwind on turns 5 and 8, proving that the early
  answer is not damage race first. It is poison, hazards, and reset timing.
- melancholy0 gets Spikes up on Fear's side on turn 16, and Forretress later
  removes Fear's earlier layer with Rapid Spin on turn 31.
- Skarmory's Sand Attack on turn 33 creates a reliability tax, but it does not
  yet name the endpoint that wins the game.

Growth passing and accuracy drops test whether disruption converts, turns
37-108:

- Umbreon repeatedly uses Growth and Baton Pass, with Raikou, Skarmory, and
  Snorlax serving as possible receivers.
- Fear keeps returning to Whirlwind and poison pressure instead of treating
  each Growth or Sand Attack as proof that the route is lost.
- The important distinction is that disruption can be real without being
  decisive. Sand Attack changes hit odds, and Baton Pass changes receiver
  danger, but each still needs a payoff before Fear can reset, phaze, poison,
  switch, or spin.
- On turn 108, Umbreon passes to Skarmory, but Fear's Skarmory Whirlwinds and
  drags Forretress through Spikes. The pass created pressure, not a finished
  route.

The receiver route fails when the endpoint is attacked, turns 116-139:

- Umbreon Growths and passes to sleeping Raikou through Spikes.
- On turn 119, Fear's Tyranitar uses Earthquake to KO Raikou. The pass has
  resolved into the wrong endpoint for melancholy0: a sleeping receiver that
  can be removed before it converts.
- Tyranitar's Screech and Pursuit later pressure Umbreon and Skarmory, while
  Fear's Raikou removes Forretress on turn 139.
- This phase shows the endpoint rule from both sides. A pass is not progress
  because boosts moved; it is progress only if the receiver survives and acts.

The long middle turns support pressure into material, turns 140-188:

- Umbreon Rests on turn 140 and continues to create annoying boards with
  Growth and Pursuit, but Fear keeps enough hazard, phaze, and direct damage
  routes available.
- Raikou KOs Umbreon on turn 172.
- Gengar's Hypnosis / Ice Punch set is dangerous enough to respect, but Fear's
  Raikou eventually removes it with Hidden Power on turn 188.
- The position has not been clean, but the visible result is clear: the
  disruption pieces have not converted before Fear's clocks and attackers
  remove them.

The ending prices accuracy as reliability, not as the route, turns 213-221:

- On turn 213, melancholy0's Skarmory uses Sand Attack into Fear's Skarmory.
  Fear's Toxic fails into the Steel-type immunity.
- Fear switches Forretress into Snorlax on turn 214. This also avoids staying
  married to a debuffed Skarmory damage line.
- On turn 215, Forretress poisons Snorlax while Snorlax Curses.
- On turns 216-218, Fear uses Skarmory's resistance, Protect, and Whirlwind to
  convert poison into a clock and push Snorlax through Spikes later.
- Sand Attack appears again on turn 219, but Fear does not need repeated
  accuracy-dependent damage from Skarmory to win. Tyranitar enters on turn
  220, Snorlax takes Spikes plus poison, and Tyranitar's Rock Slide plus poison
  ends Snorlax on turn 221.

## Critical Turns

Turns 1-8:

- Early Toxic and Whirlwind establish that the route is clock plus reset, not
  a simple one-on-one damage race.

Turn 33:

- Skarmory reveals Sand Attack. This matters because it lowers hit reliability,
  but it still needs a receiver, clock, PP line, or damage endpoint.

Turns 73-108:

- Umbreon repeatedly tries Growth into Baton Pass. Fear's Whirlwind converts
  phazing from a defensive reset into hazard and receiver denial.

Turn 119:

- Tyranitar KOs the passed Raikou with Earthquake. This is the moment where the
  pass route is judged by receiver conversion, not by whether boosts were
  created.

Turns 172 and 188:

- Raikou removes Umbreon and Gengar. The disruption shell loses the pieces
  that could have turned annoyance into a win.

Turns 215-218:

- Forretress poisons Snorlax, then Skarmory uses resistance, Protect, and
  Whirlwind. This is the clean endpoint: poison and Spikes do the work while
  Skarmory manages the Snorlax action count.

Turn 221:

- Tyranitar's Rock Slide drops Snorlax to poison range, and poison finishes
  it. melancholy0 forfeits with Skarmory still alive.

## Practical Lessons

Accuracy disruption is a reliability tax:

- A public accuracy drop changes move value immediately. Re-score hit odds,
  whether the active should switch to clear stages, and whether a less
  accuracy-dependent endpoint is available.

It is not automatically an endpoint:

- Sand Attack is progress only if the misses buy something concrete: a setup
  turn, PP exhaustion, poison turns, a safe receiver, or a KO range. Repeating
  it while the opponent can switch, phaze, poison, or finish with hazards may
  only delay the game.

Baton Pass and accuracy pressure share the same proof burden:

- Both can create dangerous public states, but neither is self-proving. Name
  the receiver or clock that converts before declaring the route winning.

Switching can be route progress:

- Leaving a debuffed active can clear accuracy stages and return the plan to
  reliable tools. Do not keep clicking the old move only because it was the
  plan before Sand Attack.

Poison plus phazing is an endpoint when the clock is protected:

- In the final sequence, Toxic, Protect, Whirlwind, Spikes, and a late direct
  hit made Snorlax's Curse turns finite. That is different from merely
  "walling" Snorlax.

## Gym Leader Lab Transfer

Use the replay as route logic, not as romhack mechanics evidence.

Local examples:

- Falkner Pidgeotto Sand Attack positions: after one accuracy drop, re-score
  hit reliability and consider whether switching clears the problem without
  losing the route.
- Bugsy Ledian or Sabrina Espeon Baton Pass positions: phazing, Haze, Encore,
  status, or damage must name the receiver and the endpoint.
- Koga, Karen, or other attrition bosses: poison and phazing are real routes
  only when the answer has the HP, PP, timing, and target pool to protect the
  clock.

Boss AI boundary:

- Boss AI may use revealed accuracy drops, revealed moves, observed damage,
  public status, public hazards, and legal local memory.
- Boss AI must not use unrevealed player team, hidden moves, hidden items, or
  hidden bench answers to decide that an accuracy route or poison endpoint is
  safe.

## Policy Hook

Create or use this policy:

```text
After an accuracy change, re-score hit reliability and route endpoint. The
accuracy move is support, not the win condition, unless it feeds a named
receiver, clock, PP route, safe switch, or KO threshold before the opponent can
reset it.
```
