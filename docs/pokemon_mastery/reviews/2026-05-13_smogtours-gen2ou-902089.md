# Battle Review: smogtours-gen2ou-902089

Players: underlying defeated aminita.

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is expert-play study, not

Information model: no Team Preview. This review uses the full log for

Length: 79 turns.

## Revealed Structure

underlying:

- Snorlax with Curse / Double-Edge / Rest / Sleep Talk.
- Starmie with Rapid Spin / Thunder Wave.
- Cloyster with Spikes / Toxic / Explosion.
- Zapdos with Thunder.
- Tyranitar with Rock Slide.
- Steelix with Earthquake / Roar / Explosion.

aminita:

- Zapdos with Thunder / Hidden Power.
- Steelix with Roar / Curse / Earthquake.
- Snorlax with Body Slam / Curse / Rest.
- Cloyster with Spikes / Toxic / Ice Beam / Explosion.
- Starmie with Thunder Wave / Rapid Spin.
- Tyranitar with Rock Slide / Screech.

## Phase Review

Lead pressure and first route claim, turns 1-8:

- underlying leads Snorlax into Zapdos and immediately shows that the lead is
  not passive: Curse into Double-Edge forces Zapdos to 31% by turn 2.
- aminita pivots Steelix into Snorlax's Rest on turn 3 and Roars it out on
  turn 4, so the early Snorlax pressure does not become a free sweep.
- underlying's Cloyster sets Spikes on turn 6 and poisons Snorlax on turn 7.
  The opening route is pressure plus entry tax, not only lead damage.

Spikes, Rest, and phaze pressure, turns 9-23:

- Zapdos forces aminita's poisoned Snorlax to Rest on turn 11.
- Steelix repeatedly answers Snorlax with Roar and later Curse, while both
  sides use Spikes to make switching expensive.
- On turn 20, aminita's Cloyster sets Spikes on underlying's side. From here,
  the Snorlax route must account for its own entry costs, not only the damage
  it is dealing.

Starmie turns the hazard race into status control, turns 29-47:

- underlying's Starmie uses Rapid Spin on turn 29 and Thunder Wave on
  aminita's Snorlax on turn 30.
- Tyranitar paralyzes underlying's Tyranitar with Body Slam damage support on
  turn 31, then uses Screech to threaten Snorlax on turns 34-35.
- The Starmie mirror on turns 46-47 paralyzes both Starmie and then clears both
  sides of Spikes with Rapid Spin. This is a full route reset, not a generic
  "spinning is good" moment.

The board is reset, then rebuilt, turns 50-64:

- underlying's low Cloyster sets Spikes again on turn 50.
- aminita's Cloyster answers with Spikes on turn 51 and immediately KOs
  underlying's Zapdos with Ice Beam on turn 52.
- The Zapdos loss is serious, but underlying still has a Snorlax route, a low
  Cloyster Explosion, and Steelix as the final one-time trade.
- Steelix later uses Roar on turn 64, dragging Zapdos instead of letting
  aminita's Starmie stay as the clean answer to the Steelix board.

Snorlax forces the final trade map, turns 65-77:

- underlying's Snorlax absorbs Zapdos pressure while asleep, Sleep Talks Curse,
  and eventually beats Tyranitar on turn 69.
- It Rests again on turn 70, then Sleep Talks through Zapdos pressure and KOs
  Zapdos on turn 74.
- aminita's Cloyster uses Ice Beam into Snorlax, then Explodes on turn 77,
  KOing Snorlax with a critical hit.
- This removes underlying's main converter, but it also spends aminita's
  Cloyster and leaves the final material small enough to calculate.

Final trade order, turns 78-79:

- underlying has low Cloyster and Steelix against aminita's paralyzed Snorlax
  and paralyzed Starmie.
- Cloyster Explodes on turn 78 as aminita switches Starmie in. Whether treated
  as a read or a covered branch, the result is the important route fact:
  Starmie is gone, so Steelix no longer has to beat a Water-type.
- Steelix then Explodes on paralyzed Snorlax on turn 79. The final trade works
  because the remaining target map is exact: remove Starmie first, then trade
  Steelix for Snorlax.

## Critical Turns

Turn 6:

- Cloyster sets Spikes while Snorlax is active. This gives underlying's early
  Snorlax pressure a permanent route tax attached to future aminita pivots.

Turn 29:

- Starmie removes underlying's Spikes weakness. The spin matters because
  underlying's own Snorlax and support pieces are about to play a long switch
  and Rest cycle.

Turn 30:

- Starmie paralyzes aminita's Snorlax. That status matters in the final
  position because Steelix can trade into a slowed, simplified target instead
  of a fully flexible anchor.

Turn 42:

- Sleep Talk Double-Edge critically KOs Steelix. The crit is outcome variance,
  but the route context matters: Steelix had been one of aminita's main ways to
  Roar and resist Snorlax.

Turns 50-52:

- Both sides re-establish Spikes, then aminita's Cloyster KOs Zapdos. This is
  a re-score point: underlying cannot continue as if Zapdos is part of the
  endgame plan.

Turns 68-74:

- Snorlax removes Tyranitar and Zapdos across RestTalk turns. These turns are
  not only Snorlax species strength; they are the payoff from earlier Spikes,
  paralysis, and Steelix removal.

Turns 77-79:

- aminita's Cloyster trades with Snorlax, underlying's Cloyster trades with
  Starmie, and underlying's Steelix trades with Snorlax.
- This is the cleanest lesson in the replay: the last trade target is chosen by
  final material. Removing the target your remaining piece cannot beat can be
  stronger than hitting the currently visible anchor.

## Practical Lessons

Trade order is a material proof:

- Before spending a final Explosion, sack, Destiny Bond, Focus Band cash-out,
  or Perish trade, write the remaining pieces after each branch. If the final
  answer cannot beat one target, the trade should often remove that target even
  if another Pokemon is more visibly threatening.

Recurring jobs delay one-time trades:

- Cloyster sets Spikes, poisons Snorlax, and later saves Explosion for the
  simplified endgame. Steelix Roars before it Explodes. The one-time move is
  strongest after the user's recurring job is already spent or no longer needed.

Hazard control is not just layer count:

- Starmie spinning on turn 29 and the double-spin sequence on turn 47 changed
  which long route was safe. A side can remove hazards and still lose the
  later hazard race if the setter, status, and trade map are not re-scored.

Status becomes endgame material:

- Thunder Wave on Snorlax and Starmie does not win immediately, but the
  paralysis remains relevant when the final trades are calculated.

Do not keep the old route after a major loss:

- Losing Zapdos on turn 52 means the plan must be rebuilt around Snorlax,
  Cloyster, and Steelix. Strong advice explicitly names that handoff instead
  of acting like the old Electric pressure route still exists.

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab bosses can create similar final trade maps with Explosion,
  Destiny Bond, Perish Song, Focus Band, Quick Claw, priority, trapping, and
  forced sacks.
- The rule is not "boom when low." The rule is: after the trade, what is the
  exact final material, and which remaining boss route is still uncovered?
- When advising against a boss, verify local Explosion/Selfdestruct damage,
  Destiny Bond and Perish timing, Focus Band behavior, Quick Claw/priority,
  type/passive immunities, and AI switch incentives before calling a trade
  forced.
- Boss AI must not choose a final trade because it knows an unrevealed player
  bench. It can only use public active state, revealed moves, observed damage,
  status, hazards, and source-allowed priors.

## New Notebook Lesson

Endgame trade targets are chosen by final material, not by irritation. If one
trade user and one converter remain, remove the target the converter cannot
beat, then prove the last exchange before spending the next one-time resource.
