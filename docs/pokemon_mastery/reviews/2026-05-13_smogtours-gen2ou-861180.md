## Phase Review

Zapdos creates early disorder, turns 1-12:

- Zapdos opens with Thunder Wave into Snorlax and uses Whirlwind twice, dragging
  Starmie and Marowak.
- Raikou is paralyzed on turn 6.
- Cloyster poisons Starmie on turn 11, then Snorlax enters on Thunder.
- This is not a solved opening from preview. The first public facts are that
  underlying's Snorlax and Raikou are statused, Starmie is poison-vulnerable,
  and Zapdos has Whirlwind as a reset tool.

Both sides reveal reset hubs, turns 13-42:

- Skarmory Whirlwinds a Curse Snorlax on turn 14.
- melancholy0's Miltank uses Growl, Heal Bell, and Milk Drink to keep Snorlax
  and status pressure from compounding.
- Underlying's Miltank mirrors the same kind of team reset with Body Slam,
  Growl, Heal Bell, and Milk Drink.
- Marowak Rests on turn 39 and later benefits from Heal Bell support. The
  lesson is that the cleric is not a passive wall; it is enabling Rest users
  and keeping long routes alive.

The first breaker exchange is mixed, turns 57-88:

- Underlying's Marowak uses Swords Dance on turn 57 and KOs Heracross by turn
  59.
- Melancholy0's Marowak later uses Swords Dance into Raikou and KOs Raikou on
  turn 88, but is left at 13%.
- Both sides show the main leak branches against Miltank-style control:
  boosted physical breakers and strong special pressure. Any claim that a
  reset hub is winning must track those leaks.

Leak branches close one by one, turns 89-145:

- Melancholy0's low Marowak is preserved instead of thrown away, while Cloyster
  and Snorlax continue to tax Starmie, Skarmory, and Snorlax.
- Underlying's Marowak finally faints to Cloyster Surf on turn 103.
- Snorlax repeatedly threatens Curse, but Miltank's Growl / Milk Drink loop and
  Zapdos Whirlwind keep it from becoming a clean endpoint.
- By turn 145, underlying's Marowak and Raikou are gone. The remaining routes
  are increasingly about whether Snorlax, Starmie, Skarmory, or Miltank can
  create a new leak through the reset structure.

The late game becomes a leak audit, turns 146-222:

- Melancholy0 repeatedly uses Miltank to Growl opposing Snorlax, Milk Drink
  back into range, and Heal Bell when status matters.
- Zapdos enters to spread Thunder Wave, attack, and Whirlwind boosted Snorlax
  when Growl alone is not the right reset.
- Cloyster keeps trying to place Spikes, while Starmie repeatedly removes them
  with Rapid Spin.
- Underlying's Starmie, Skarmory, Miltank, and Snorlax all keep functions, but
  none of them creates a decisive leak after the main breakers are gone.

The final board proves the reset route, turns 216-223:

- Snorlax Curses on turn 216, then Double-Edges Zapdos on turn 217.
- Zapdos survives and Whirlwinds Snorlax out, dragging Skarmory.
- Snorlax re-enters, but Miltank comes in and uses Growl on turns 220 and 221.
- Underlying switches Skarmory into Growl on turn 222, then forfeits on turn
  223.
- The forfeit is not proof that every prior turn was forced. It does show that
  once the leak branches were gone, the low-damage Miltank / Zapdos reset
  structure had become a real endpoint rather than empty waiting.

## Critical Turns

Turn 6:

- Raikou is paralyzed. This matters much later because Raikou is one of the
  routes that could pressure Miltank and Zapdos instead of only trading into
  the physical reset loop.

Turns 57-59:

- Underlying's Marowak converts Swords Dance into the Heracross KO. This is
  the kind of leak that prevents a reset hub from being called safe too early.

Turn 88:

- Melancholy0's Marowak KOs Raikou. Removing Raikou reduces the special-pressure
  leak against Miltank / Zapdos structures.

Turn 103:

- Underlying's Marowak faints to Cloyster Surf. After this, the main physical
  breaker leak is gone.

Turns 179, 194, 195, 210, and 217:

- Whirlwind keeps stopping boosted or setup attempts from becoming immediate
  conversion. It is a partner to Growl, not a replacement for the whole route.

Turns 220-222:

- Miltank's Growl sequence is low-damage but route-winning because the leak
  audit has already happened. The remaining Snorlax branch is being reduced
  faster than it can convert.

## Practical Lessons

Reset hubs need a leak audit:

- Before calling Miltank, Skarmory, Starmie, or another reset piece winning,
  list what breaks the loop: mixed special pressure, Swords Dance / Curse,
  Explosion, phazing, status, hazards, PP, crit risk, or forced switch maps.

A cleric is a route hub, not just a cure button:

- Heal Bell changes how Rest users can be used. It lets Rest Marowak and other
  teammates return without spending the usual sleep turns, and it erases status
  clocks that would otherwise force risky progress.

Low-damage moves can be the endpoint after leaks are gone:

- Growl is not impressive on turn 1. In the final board, it is decisive because
  the remaining physical route is Snorlax trying to boost through a prepared
  debuff/recovery loop.

Do not overclaim stability while breakers remain:

- Earlier in the battle, Marowak and Raikou still made the reset hub vulnerable.
  Calling the loop winning before those pieces were handled would have been
  wishful.

Whirlwind and Growl are complementary:

- Whirlwind handles immediate boosted positions and target-map resets. Growl
  handles the active physical attacker when the player can stay in. Strong
  advice chooses between them by the current leak, not by move label.

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab does not automatically inherit vanilla Miltank's Heal Bell /
  Growl set. Exact transfer must check the local roster and move table.
- The route logic does transfer to Whitney Miltank's Milk Drink / Body Slam /
  Attract / Rollout loop: ask what breaks the recovery/disruption cycle before
  assuming chip is progress.
- Sabrina Hypno, Will Slowbro, Koga Muk, and other local recovery/reset pieces
  should be scored by leak audit: what damage, status, Haze/phaze, Encore,
  sacrifice, item, or PP route actually breaks the loop?
- Boss AI must not decide that a reset hub is winning because it secretly knows
  the player's unrevealed bench lacks a breaker. It can use public HP, status,
  hazards, revealed moves, observed damage, seen species, and source-allowed
  inference.

## New Notebook Lesson

A reset hub wins only after its leaks are accounted for. When the remaining
opponent routes cannot break the debuff/recovery/cure loop, low-damage moves
like Growl or Heal Bell can be endpoint moves; before that, they are only
stabilization.
