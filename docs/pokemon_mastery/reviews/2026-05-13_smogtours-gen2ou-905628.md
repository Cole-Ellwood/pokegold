## Phase Review

Fakes claims the first clock, turns 1-24:

- RUBYBLOOD leads Snorlax into Zapdos and starts Curse immediately, but Fakes
  pivots Forretress in on turn 1.
- Forretress poisons Snorlax on turn 2, sets Spikes on turn 3, and Golem Roars
  Snorlax after Rest on turn 5.
- RUBYBLOOD removes the first Spikes layer with Forretress on turn 23, but the
  opening has already forced Rest, entry damage, and role reveals.
- The lesson is not that the lead was solved from Team Preview. The lesson is
  that the first public contacts quickly expose the route map: Toxic, Spikes,
  Roar, and Rest determine who owns the early clock.

Fakes spends material to keep the long route alive, turns 140-186:

- Vaporeon pressures through Skarmory and KOs it on turn 150 after Growth.
- RUBYBLOOD removes Golem on turn 157, then Fakes uses Forretress Explosion on
  turn 159 to leave Snorlax at 12%.
- Fakes's Zapdos KOs RUBYBLOOD's Forretress on turn 161.
- Starmie finally clears Spikes on turn 186, but Zapdos KOs Starmie on the
  same turn. The spin matters, but the spinner is gone and RUBYBLOOD has lost
  both dedicated hazard-control pieces.

Raikou removes Vaporeon, then Gengar removes Raikou, turns 228-263:

- Raikou forces progress by hitting Vaporeon with Thunderbolt on turn 228.
- After Rest, Raikou Sleep Talks Thunderbolt twice and KOs Vaporeon on turn
  235.
- Fakes then uses Gengar to pressure the sleeping Raikou with Ice Punch and
  Thunderbolt. Gengar Explodes on turn 263, removing Raikou.
- This is the decisive handoff. RUBYBLOOD has traded the Vaporeon answer into
  a route where the remaining material is Umbreon plus a very low Snorlax
  against Zapdos plus Snorlax.

Umbreon contains but does not convert, turns 264-333:

- Umbreon repeatedly uses Charm, Pursuit, Rest, and Sleep Talk against
  Fakes's Zapdos / Snorlax pair.
- The loop is not fake. Charm suppresses Snorlax's damage, Rest keeps Umbreon
  alive, and Pursuit catches several switches.
- It is also not a proven winning loop. Fakes can alternate Zapdos and Snorlax,
  collect Leftovers, Rest Zapdos if needed, and avoid letting Pursuit chip
  become a forced KO.
- RUBYBLOOD's only remaining converter is Snorlax, but it is low, asleep, and
  exposed to recoil. Umbreon is holding the board together more than it is
  ending the board.

The endpoint fails, turns 334-336:

- RUBYBLOOD brings in sleeping Snorlax at 38% against Zapdos.
- Zapdos Thunders Snorlax to 7%; Sleep Talk calls Double-Edge and hits Zapdos
  to 46%, but Snorlax faints to recoil on turn 334.
- Zapdos Rests to full on turn 335 while Umbreon remains asleep, and
  RUBYBLOOD forfeits on turn 336.
- The final board proves the loop problem: if the only damage endpoint dies to
  recoil and the opponent can still reset with Rest, containment did not equal
  conversion.

## Critical Turns

Turn 5:

- Golem Roars after Snorlax Rests. This prevents early Curse Snorlax from
  turning one favorable rest cycle into an automatic sweep.

Turn 150:

- Vaporeon KOs Skarmory. RUBYBLOOD loses the main Whirlwind / Toxic physical
  control piece, which makes later containment depend more heavily on Umbreon.

Turn 186:

- Starmie removes Spikes but is KOed by Zapdos. This is a route reset with a
  cost, not a clean defensive victory.

Turns 235 and 263:

- Raikou KOs Vaporeon, then Gengar Explodes on Raikou. After this exchange,
  the endgame is not "Umbreon walls enough things." It is "Umbreon must
  somehow make Zapdos / Snorlax finite while preserving low Snorlax."

Turns 281-303:

- Umbreon catches multiple switches with Pursuit and keeps using Charm / Rest,
  but Fakes's Snorlax repeatedly climbs back near full while Zapdos remains
  outside forced KO range. The loop contains threats without proving an
  endpoint.

Turns 334-335:

- Snorlax's Sleep Talk Double-Edge creates damage but dies to recoil, then
  Zapdos Rests. The last possible converter is spent before the loop can cash
  out.

## Practical Lessons

Containment is not conversion:

- A loop can be tactically correct and strategically insufficient. Before
  calling it winning, name the endpoint: KO range, forced Rest, PP exhaustion,
  hazard damage, poison, trapping, switch denial, or a preserved converter.

Pursuit chip needs a recovery-denial story:

- Pursuit on switching Zapdos / Snorlax looks like progress, but Leftovers,
  Rest, and alternating targets can erase the route unless the chip crosses a
  forced action threshold.

Preserve the converter that actually ends the game:

- Umbreon could keep fighting, but the remaining Snorlax was the only piece
  that could still deal large damage. Its HP, sleep turns, and recoil risk had
  to be treated as the endpoint budget.

Re-score after the Electric trade:

- Removing Vaporeon with Raikou was valuable. Losing Raikou to Gengar on turn
  263 changed the whole route, so advice after that point must stop acting as
  if Electric pressure is still available.

Do not import Team Preview thinking:

- The review can use the full team sheet after the fact. A live advisor in
  GSC, and a Gym Leader Lab boss AI, must build the route from lead contact,
  revealed moves, observed damage, status, hazards, and public switches.

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab can create similar loops with Charm, Growl, Screech, Toxic,
  Protect, RestTalk, Pursuit, trapping, screens, phazing, Haze, weather,
  recoil, and multiple Spikes layers.
- Do not mark a boss or player loop as winning merely because the current
  active cannot break through. Ask what finite resource is shrinking and which
  piece converts when it shrinks enough.
- Boss AI must not use unrevealed player-team knowledge to decide that a
  containment loop is winning or losing. It can use public active state,
  revealed moves, observed damage, status, hazards, seen species, PP evidence,
  and source-allowed priors.
- Local advice must verify romhack Rest / Sleep Talk behavior, Pursuit timing,
  stat-stage persistence, switch incentives, PP, items, hazards, recoil, and
  AI public-memory rules before transferring exact GSC claims.

## New Notebook Lesson

A contained loop still needs an endpoint. If the opponent can reset the chip
with switching, Leftovers, Rest, or PP while your only converter is being
spent, the line is a hold, not a win condition.
