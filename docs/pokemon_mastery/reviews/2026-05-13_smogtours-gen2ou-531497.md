# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-531497

Replay: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-531497

Source team context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-sample-teams-breakdown-updated-july-2023.3688523/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for general GSC
study, not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 52 turns.

Players: the pirate99 defeated Pohjis.

## Why This Replay Matters

The Smogon sample-team note for Paralysis & Marowak describes a team that
spreads paralysis, uses Spikes and shared physical pressure, then gives Marowak
the chance to convert after its checks have been chipped or slowed. This replay
is a compact turn-1-to-endgame example: the Marowak route is visible early, gets
partly denied, and still wins because the team keeps improving the route state
instead of demanding an immediate sweep.

## Phase Map

Opening disruption, turns 1-10:

- Turn 1 is messy: a critical hit forces the pirate99's Snorlax into immediate
  Rest on turn 2. The route does not collapse; the team shifts into utility.
- Pohjis sets Spikes on turn 3. the pirate99 answers with Cloyster Spikes on
  turn 6, but Pohjis's Starmie removes that layer on turn 7.
- On turn 8, both Starmie use Thunder Wave. This matters more than the visible
  Starmie mirror: speed control is route material for Marowak later.
- On turn 10, the pirate99's Starmie spins away Pohjis's early Spikes, keeping
  the Marowak team from starting the route under a permanent entry tax.

Building the route ladder, turns 17-24:

- the pirate99 re-sets Spikes on turn 17.
- Zapdos removes Pohjis's Cloyster on turn 19, then paralyzes opposing Zapdos
  on turn 20. The Marowak route now has fewer hazard-control and speed-control
  problems.
- Tyranitar enters into Zapdos on turn 21 and uses Screech on turn 22. The
  source team note says Tyranitar's job is to force in shared Marowak checks
  and chip them; turn 23 shows that job clearly when Starmie enters through
  Spikes and takes Rock Slide.
- Pohjis's Starmie spins the layer away on turn 24, so the route is still not
  secure. The correct conclusion is not "Spikes failed"; it is "the route needs
  one more conversion step."

First Marowak conversion attempt, turns 28-32:

- Cloyster sets Spikes again on turn 28 while Pohjis's Snorlax uses Rest.
- Cloyster Explodes on turn 29, leaving Snorlax very low and giving Marowak a
  clean entry.
- Marowak uses Swords Dance on turn 30. Sleep Talk Rest fully resets Snorlax,
  so the first setup window is not a clean sweep.
- Marowak still removes Snorlax by turn 32. This is not failure; it is
  conversion of one blocker.

Counter-route containment, turns 33-43:

- Pohjis sends Marowak after Snorlax falls. the pirate99 pivots to Zapdos on
  turn 33 and uses Hidden Power on turn 34 to cut it down.
- Pohjis's Zapdos uses Rest on turn 36, letting the pirate99 re-enter Marowak
  on turn 37 and pressure the sleeping Zapdos.
- On turn 39, Pohjis's Marowak re-enters through Spikes and is left at 40 HP
  after the exchange.
- Turn 43 is the route-locking turn: the pirate99's Zapdos uses Whirlwind,
  drags in the low Marowak, and Spikes finishes it. The support package, not a
  raw sweep, removed the opposing converter.

Final cleanup, turns 44-52:

- Tyranitar removes Zapdos on turn 49 after Zapdos misses Thunder.
- Rhydon removes Tyranitar, but is left low enough for the pirate99's damaged
  Marowak to clean with Earthquake on turn 52.
- The same Marowak that was not able to sweep from turn 30 still becomes the
  final converter because earlier support removed Snorlax, Starmie, opposing
  Marowak, and Zapdos.

## Critical Turns

Turn 8:

- Thunder Wave is not generic status. It is part of the route ladder: every
  slowed or constrained target makes Marowak's later entries less fragile.

Turn 23:

- Tyranitar's Rock Slide into Starmie is support for Marowak, not isolated
  damage. It punishes the shared check that entered because of Tyranitar's
  pressure.

Turn 29:

- Cloyster's Explosion does not win immediately, especially because Sleep Talk
  Rest resets Snorlax on turn 30. It still creates the first real Marowak entry
  and helps remove Snorlax by turn 32.

Turn 43:

- Whirlwind converts earlier Spikes and chip into the removal of opposing
  Marowak. The support move wins because the target was already put into the
  right state.

Turn 52:

- Marowak at low HP is still a converter. The route was not "Marowak sweeps
  from full." The route was "remove blockers until Marowak's one remaining
  attack is enough."

## Extracted Lesson

Treat a win condition as a support ladder, not a promised sweep.

For a planned converter, track:

```text
Converter:
Required entry state:
Current blockers:
Support jobs already completed:
Support jobs still needed:
Backup pieces that can finish the same route:
What proves the route is dead:
```

The converter does not have to win the first time it enters. A good first entry
can remove one blocker, force Rest, reveal a counter-route, or create the final
damage map. The route stays live if the team can still preserve the converter
or transfer the same route to a backup piece.

## Boss-Battle Transfer

Gym Leader Lab boss fights often have obvious player converters: a setup
sweeper, a type-advantaged attacker, a bulky Rest user, a weather abuser, a
priority cleaner, or a one-time trade user. Do not require that piece to solve
the fight immediately.

For boss advice, ask:

1. Which support jobs make the converter real: status, hazards, chip, spin,
   phazing, Haze, Encore, sacrifice, or safe entry?
2. If the converter enters now and only removes one blocker, is that enough?
3. Which boss counter-route appears after the first blocker falls?
4. Can another piece finish the same route if the primary converter is low?
5. What exact event proves the route is dead rather than paused?
