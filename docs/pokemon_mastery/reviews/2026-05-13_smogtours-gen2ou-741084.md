## Phase Review

Fear gets early disruption and material, turns 1-29:

- Fear opens Zapdos into Snorlax, sets Reflect, and paralyzes Snorlax on turn
  2.
- Tyranitar Roars Snorlax on turn 4, then removes Cloyster with Pursuit on
  turn 6 after Cloyster has placed Spikes.
- Starmie later uses Rapid Spin on turn 19, removing the entry tax from
  Fear's side.
- Tyranitar chases Exeggutor with Flamethrower and Pursuit, and Exeggutor
  faints on turn 29.
- The opening looks favorable for Fear, but the remaining route map still has
  BlazingDark's Raikou, Rhydon, and Snorlax as separate leak branches.

Miltank stabilizes Snorlax, but Quagsire does not close the map, turns 31-64:

- Fear's Miltank reveals Heal Bell, Growl, Milk Drink, and Seismic Toss.
  Against Curse Snorlax, this is a real reset hub: Growl can answer Attack
  boosts while Milk Drink preserves the hub.
- Quagsire uses Belly Drum on turn 40 and again on turn 55. It removes Zapdos
  on turn 59, but it does not remove Snorlax, Rhydon, or Raikou.
- Quagsire uses Belly Drum again on turn 63 and faints to Snorlax's Body Slam
  on turn 64 after Earthquake damage into Snorlax.
- A converter taking one target is not the same thing as leak closure. The
  reset hub still needs a route through the remaining attackers.

Rhydon and Raikou keep forcing different answers, turns 65-135:

- BlazingDark's Raikou repeatedly uses Rest and Sleep Talk while Fear tries to
  make progress with Tyranitar, Snorlax, Zapdos, and Miltank.
- Rhydon uses Roar on turns 93, 97, and 99, preventing Fear's Snorlax setup
  line from becoming a clean endpoint.
- Fear's Snorlax eventually faints to Rhydon Earthquake on turn 127.
- Zapdos falls to Raikou Thunder on turn 135.
- This phase is the main leak-audit evidence: Miltank can answer some physical
  damage, but it is not the whole team. Rhydon changes the active route, and
  Raikou pressures through the special side.

Miltank becomes the last hub before the leaks are gone, turns 136-171:

- Miltank heals and uses Growl repeatedly into Raikou. That does not meaningfully
  reduce Raikou's Thunder or Crunch pressure.
- Tyranitar Roars Raikou on turn 154 but later faints to Crunch on turn 160.
- By turn 171, Fear is largely down to Miltank and Starmie against Snorlax,
  Rhydon, and Raikou.
- The hub is still alive, but the board is not contained. The remaining leaks
  demand different answers.

The endgame proves the failed leak audit, turns 172-236:

- Miltank stabilizes a low Snorlax sequence with Growl and Milk Drink on turns
  176-181.
- When Raikou enters on turn 182, Miltank's Growl no longer answers the main
  damage class.
- Later, Snorlax Curses again on turn 228. Miltank Growls on turns 229 and
  230, but BlazingDark switches to Rhydon on turn 231.
- Miltank Growls Rhydon on turns 231 and 232, but Rhydon still KOs it with
  Earthquake on turn 232.
- Starmie damages Raikou on turns 233-235, but Raikou Rests and then Sleep
  Talks Thunder to KO Starmie on turn 236.

## Critical Turns

Turn 31:

- Miltank uses Heal Bell. The cleric hub is real: it can erase status and keep
  long routes alive.

Turns 40, 55, 59, and 64:

- Quagsire's Belly Drum route removes Zapdos but not the remaining leak map,
  then Quagsire faints. The hub still has to account for Snorlax, Rhydon, and
  Raikou.

Turns 93, 97, and 99:

- Rhydon repeatedly Roars Fear's attempts to build Snorlax pressure. It is not
  just a physical attacker; it also denies setup conversion.

Turn 127:

- Rhydon KOs Fear's Snorlax. After this, Miltank's reset loop loses a major
  partner and the leak map gets sharper.

Turn 160:

- Raikou KOs Tyranitar. The special-pressure leak remains alive while Fear's
  remaining material gets narrower.

Turns 176-181:

- Miltank handles the immediate Snorlax line with Growl and Milk Drink. This
  is stabilization, not proof that the whole endgame is won.

Turns 231-232:

- Rhydon enters, absorbs Growl twice, and still KOs Miltank with Earthquake.
  The reset hub continued the old move after a different leak entered.

## Practical Lessons

Reset hubs can be correct locally and losing globally:

- Growl was a coherent answer to Curse Snorlax in several positions. It was
  not a coherent answer to the whole remaining Snorlax / Rhydon / Raikou map.

Every new entry re-opens the leak audit:

- When Rhydon or Raikou enters, stop asking whether Miltank can handle
  Snorlax. Ask whether the hub handles the active leak now, and whether the
  answer to that leak can still enter.

Debuffs need a target and an endpoint:

- A Growl into one target does not become permanent progress against a team
  that can rotate. If the opponent can change targets, heal, phaze, or attack
  through the debuff, the loop still needs damage, PP, hazard, status, or a
  preserved converter.

A converter KO is not leak closure:

- Quagsire removing Zapdos mattered, but it did not make Miltank safe against
  Rhydon, Raikou, and Snorlax. Judge the route by the leaks that remain, not by
  whether one target fell.

## Gym Leader Lab Transfer

Use this replay as route logic, not as romhack mechanics evidence.

Local transfer:

- When a player relies on Growl, Charm, Reflect, recovery, cleric support, or
  a phazer to contain a boss route, list every boss piece that still breaks the
  loop.
- If a different boss attacker enters, re-score from that active's damage,
  typing, status, speed, item, and local AI incentives. Do not keep using the
  reset move that handled the previous target.
- A boss-side reset or debuff loop can be used only from public player state.
  Boss AI must not conclude "no leaks remain" from hidden player moves, hidden
  items, or unrevealed bench Pokemon.

Local evidence needed for exact advice:

- Move effects and PP for debuffs, recovery, cleric moves, phazing, and Haze.
- Whether local switching, forced switching, Haze, Baton Pass, or items reset
  or preserve stat stages.
- Damage evidence for the active leak after debuffs, screens, weather, type
  passives, and items.

## Policy Hook

Use this as the losing-side contrast to the reset-hub rule:

```text
A reset hub is winning only after the leak audit closes. If a new attacker or
function enters, the old reset move becomes suspect until it is proved against
that new leak.
```
