# Battle Review: smogtours-gen2ou-902619

Source:

- Replay log: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-902619.log
- Tournament source: SPL XVII GSC discussion,
  https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/spl-xvii-gsc-discussion.3775984/

Players: beware of coco defeated Zokuru.

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is expert-play study, not
Gym Leader Lab mechanics evidence.

Information model: there is no Team Preview. This review uses the full replay
for postgame study, but individual turn lessons should be scored from the
public state available at that turn.

Length: 97 turns.

- Raikou with Thunderbolt / Hidden Power / Rest / Sleep Talk.
- Skarmory with Toxic / Curse / Sleep Talk.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge / Earthquake / Rest / Sleep Talk.
- Forretress with Toxic / Spikes / Hidden Power / Rapid Spin.
- Misdreavus with Perish Song / Toxic.
- Vaporeon with Surf.

beware of coco:

- Cloyster with Spikes.
- Raikou with Thunder / Hidden Power / Rest / Sleep Talk.
- Snorlax with Body Slam / Curse / Self-Destruct.
- Steelix with Curse / Earthquake / Roar / Explosion.
- Machamp with Rock Slide / Fire Blast / Cross Chop.
- Heracross with Megahorn / Rest / Sleep Talk / Curse.

## Phase Review

Opening contact and first hazard exchange, turns 1-14:

- Zokuru opens Raikou into Cloyster, while beware of coco immediately pivots to
  Raikou and starts the Electric mirror.
- Skarmory lands Toxic on Raikou on turn 8, forcing the opposing Raikou toward
  a Rest cycle by turn 11.
- Cloyster sets Spikes on Zokuru's side on turn 13, and Forretress answers by
  setting Spikes on turn 14. The game quickly becomes a two-sided entry-tax
  contest instead of a clean special exchange.

Steelix enters as the route reset, turns 20-40:

- Steelix enters on Zokuru's Resting Raikou on turn 20 and starts combining
  Curse, Earthquake pressure, and Roar.
- Zokuru gets meaningful damage on Steelix with Snorlax Earthquake, but Steelix
  keeps finding Leftovers turns and forced switches.
- Forretress removes Zokuru's Spikes weakness on turn 36, but Cloyster resets
  Spikes on turn 40 before fainting later. One successful Rapid Spin did not
  permanently solve the hazard contract.

The phaze loop owns the clock, turns 41-60:

- Misdreavus starts a Perish Song line on turn 42, but Steelix is able to leave
  before the count converts.
- Skarmory repeatedly tries to turn the Steelix matchup into a Curse setup
  route. Steelix answers with Roar on turns 48, 51, 53, 55, 56, 59, and 60.
- Each reset erases the boosted Skarmory position while Zokuru's dragged
  Pokemon pay Spikes again. Steelix rises from the low-mid HP range back to
  full through Leftovers during the sequence.
- Toxic does not solve the Steelix route. The log shows Steelix immune to
  Skarmory's Toxic on turns 27, 58, 66, and 72.

Support collapse and one-time trades, turns 63-80:

- Cloyster finally faints on turn 63, but the damage from the second Spikes
  layer sequence has already shaped Zokuru's side.
- Machamp pressures the weakened structure: Fire Blast cuts Skarmory to 46% on
  turn 64, and a critical Cross Chop KOs Vaporeon on turn 65.
- Misdreavus uses Perish Song again on turn 67, but Steelix KOs it with
  Earthquake before the route can force a trade.
- Forretress spins away Zokuru's Spikes on turn 68, but the spin arrives after
  Vaporeon and Misdreavus have already been lost and Skarmory is degraded.
- Steelix Explodes on Skarmory on turn 74. Snorlax then Self-Destructs into
  Zokuru's Snorlax on turn 80, clearing the way for Heracross.

Heracross inherits the endgame, turns 80-97:

- Heracross enters against a sleeping Raikou after both Snorlax have fallen.
- Raikou paralyzes Heracross with Sleep Talk Thunderbolt on turn 81, but
  Heracross uses Rest on turn 82 and repeatedly resets damage with RestTalk.
- The final sequence has variance, including Sleep Talk Rest branches and a
  critical Megahorn, but the route is not only luck: the earlier phaze and trade
  sequence removed or spent the pieces that could keep Heracross from inheriting
  the board.

## Critical Turns

Turn 20:

- Steelix enters as Raikou uses Rest. This is the first clear sign that the
  opposing plan can attack sleep turns with a Ground phazer instead of playing
  a passive Electric mirror.

Turns 27 and 58:

- Skarmory's Toxic attempts show Steelix immunity. Any route that expected
  poison to put Steelix on a clock must be revised from public evidence.

Turns 36 and 40:

- Forretress successfully uses Rapid Spin on turn 36, but Cloyster sets Spikes
  again on turn 40. This is the hazard-contract lesson: removing a layer is not
  the same as controlling the setter, spinblocker, or forced-switch engine.

Turns 48-60:

- Skarmory tries repeated Curse entries into Steelix. Steelix repeatedly Roars
  while Spikes are up.
- This is the replay's central lesson. After the first reset, the question is
  not "can Skarmory eventually boost enough?" It is "what changes before the
  next Roar plus Spikes cycle?" If the answer is nothing, re-entering the setup
  loop is opponent progress.

Turns 63-68:

- Cloyster dies, Vaporeon and Misdreavus fall, and Forretress removes Spikes.
  The late spin is useful in isolation, but the route map has already changed:
  two support pieces are gone and Skarmory is low enough for Steelix Explosion
  to finish the job.

Turn 74:

- Steelix Explodes on Skarmory. This is a route trade, not a random damage
  click: Steelix has already consumed setup attempts, phazed through Spikes,
  and removed the physical wall that would matter for the remaining endgame.

Turn 80:

- Snorlax uses Self-Destruct on Zokuru's Snorlax. After this, Heracross is no
  longer trying to break a full answer map; it is inheriting a simplified board.

## Practical Lessons

Repeated phaze resets turn setup into opponent progress:

- A boost is not progress if the boost is erased and the backline pays Spikes
  every time. Once the same phazer has reset the same setup route, the next
  attempt needs a new fact: hazard removal, phazer damage, a trap, a forced
  Rest, PP pressure, Explosion pressure, or a different converter.

Hazard removal has a timing requirement:

- Rapid Spin on turn 36 mattered, but Cloyster re-established Spikes on turn
  40. Rapid Spin on turn 68 also mattered, but it arrived after key losses.
  Hazard control is strongest before the phaze loop converts, not after the
  loop has already spent the team.

Do not count status routes that public mechanics have rejected:

- The log explicitly shows Steelix immune to Toxic. After that information is
  public, the Steelix answer map cannot keep listing Toxic as the clock.

One-time trades are strongest after the route job is done:

- Steelix did not need to preserve itself forever. After Roar plus Spikes had
  degraded Zokuru's side, Explosion on Skarmory removed the remaining physical
  wall and handed the game to Snorlax plus Heracross.

Endgame converters inherit from earlier board simplification:

- Heracross won a volatile RestTalk endgame, but it arrived after Vaporeon,
  Misdreavus, Skarmory, and Snorlax had been removed or spent. The late cleaner
  was enabled by the midgame route map, not discovered on turn 80.

## Boss Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab has no symmetrical Team Preview, so player-side preparation
  may use known boss source data, while boss AI must not plan from an unrevealed
  player bench.
- The phaze-loop lesson transfers cleanly: if a boss can combine Roar,
  Whirlwind, Haze, Encore, Perish timing, Spider Web pivots, or similar control
  with hazards or passive damage, repeated setup attempts may feed the boss
  route instead of creating progress.
- Romhack Spikes can stack to three layers, so the cost of re-entering a
  failed setup loop can be higher than in vanilla GSC.
- Before giving exact advice, verify local Roar/Whirlwind/Haze/Encore timing,
  Spikes fractions, Rapid Spin clearing, type/passive immunities, Explosion
  damage, RestTalk behavior, and boss AI incentives from source or trace.

## New Notebook Lesson

When setup is repeatedly reset by phazing or another control move, stop asking
whether the setup Pokemon walls the current attack. Ask what changes before the
next reset. If no resource, mechanic, or route blocker changes, exit the loop:
remove hazards, pressure the control user, trade, trap, pivot, or hand off to a
different converter.
