# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-604804

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-604804

Supporting source:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/gsc-ou-gengar.3696769/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Useful for trap-lure planning,
blocker removal, sacrifice sequencing, and special-route conversion. This is
not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 55 turns.

Players: daddy you are home defeated Garay oak.

## Teams Revealed

Garay oak:

- Zapdos.
- Skarmory with Drill Peck and Curse.
- Blissey with Flamethrower, Heal Bell, and Light Screen.
- Starmie with Thunder Wave, Rapid Spin, Surf, and Recover.
- Snorlax with Belly Drum and Body Slam.
- Rhydon with Earthquake.

daddy you are home:

- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Lovely Kiss, and Self-Destruct.
- Zapdos with Thunder.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Toxic, Surf, and Explosion.
- Raikou with Thunder.
- Gengar with Destiny Bond, Mean Look, Perish Song, and Ice Punch.
- Espeon with Hidden Power and Baton Pass.

## Phase Map

Opening special-wall map and hazards, turns 1-20:

- Garay oak shows a stable defensive structure: Skarmory covers Snorlax,
  Blissey covers Zapdos and Raikou, and Starmie spins Cloyster's early Spikes.
- daddy repeatedly resets Cloyster and Zapdos around those answers, but the
  route does not break open while Blissey and Starmie both keep doing their
  jobs.
- Lovely Kiss lands on Zapdos on turn 20, weakening Garay oak's ability to
  control the Electric route later.

Hazard and status pressure into setup bait, turns 21-29:

- Cloyster keeps forcing Starmie to spend turns on Rapid Spin while poison
  pressure accumulates.
- Garay oak uses Belly Drum Snorlax on turn 27 into Cloyster's Spikes turn, but
  Gengar enters on Body Slam on turn 28 and stops the immediate route.
- Destiny Bond on turn 29 forces Garay oak to avoid the obvious KO line and
  switch.

Trap removes the route blocker, turns 30-34:

- Garay oak brings in Blissey on Gengar. Gengar uses Mean Look, then Perish
  Song.
- Blissey burns Gengar and uses Heal Bell, but it cannot leave before the
  Perish count expires. It faints on turn 34 after setting Light Screen.
- The important route fact is not that Gengar got a KO. The route fact is that
  the special wall and cleric are gone, making Espeon and Raikou harder to
  cover later.

Trade cascade, turns 35-49:

- Rhydon pressures Snorlax, so daddy uses Self-Destruct into Zapdos on turn 36.
- Garay oak's Starmie spins repeatedly, but poison and repeated entries make it
  less able to preserve the whole defensive map. Starmie eventually faints to
  Cloyster on turn 47.
- Garay oak's Belly Drum Snorlax returns, and Cloyster Explodes into it on turn
  49. That removes a dangerous converter before Espeon enters.

Special-route endgame, turns 50-55:

- Espeon removes Rhydon, then Baton Passes to Raikou as Skarmory uses Curse.
- Raikou needs Thunder to connect through the final turns. The three misses on
  turns 52-54 are major outcome branches, so the ending should not be treated
  as a clean forced line.
- The strategic setup for the ending was still coherent: Blissey and Zapdos had
  been removed, Snorlax was gone, and Raikou was the remaining special-route
  converter.

## Critical Turns

Turns 7-11:

- Starmie removes Spikes twice and gets poisoned. This is the first sign that
  Garay oak's route depends on Starmie and Blissey continuing to cover several
  jobs at once: removal, status spreading, special defense, and cleric support.

Turns 27-29:

- Belly Drum Snorlax tries to convert while Cloyster sets Spikes, but Gengar
  enters and uses Destiny Bond. The setup route is not dead, but it is paused
  and must be rebuilt around Gengar's trade threat.

Turns 30-34:

- Gengar traps Blissey and trades its own future for the exact blocker that was
  keeping Zapdos, Raikou, and Espeon manageable.
- Blissey setting Light Screen before fainting matters, but it is temporary
  mitigation, not replacement for a living wall and cleric.

Turn 36:

- Snorlax uses Self-Destruct into Zapdos. This is part of the same route
  cascade: remove the pieces that still stop the special endgame after Blissey
  is gone.

Turn 49:

- Cloyster Explodes into Belly Drum Snorlax. The trap opened a special route,
  but Garay oak still had a physical converter. Daddy spends Cloyster to stop
  that route before sending in Espeon.

Turns 52-55:

- Raikou's cleanup includes repeated Thunder misses before the final hit. The
  misses are outcome luck. The route lesson is earlier: the blocker-removal
  sequence created a position where Raikou was the remaining converter at all.

## Practical Lessons

Trap the blocker, not the nearest target:

- Gengar trapping Blissey matters because Blissey was the wall and cleric that
  kept the special route contained.
- A trap that removes a replaceable target would not deserve the same label.

Name the beneficiary before trapping:

- The beneficiaries here are Zapdos, Raikou, and Espeon. Gengar is not trying
  to be the final route by itself.
- If the beneficiary cannot use the missing blocker, the trap is only a trade.

Protect the newly opened route:

- After Blissey falls, daddy still has to remove Zapdos and Belly Drum Snorlax.
  The trap did not end the game; it changed which blockers mattered next.

Do not over-credit the cleanup:

- The final Thunder sequence contains major miss branches. Review the plan by
  the pre-turn route map, not by the exact final dice.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab has several trap, Perish, Destiny Bond, Pursuit, lock, and
  lure-adjacent tools. The transferable question is always: which route blocker
  is being removed, and who benefits?
- Against boss teams, do not spend a trap or Destiny Bond line just because the
  target is strong. Spend it when the target is the piece that stops the
  player's actual route, or when it prevents the boss's immediate route.
- After any trap succeeds, rebuild the route map. The next blocker may be a
  priority user, phazer, spinner, recovery piece, or alternate boss converter.

## New Notebook Lesson

A trap or lure is a blocker-removal contract: target, beneficiary, route opened,
role lost, and next blocker must all be named before the move earns a strong
label.
