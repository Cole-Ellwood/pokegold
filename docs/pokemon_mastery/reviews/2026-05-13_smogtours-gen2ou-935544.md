# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-935544

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-935544

Supporting source: https://www.smogon.com/gs/articles/move_priority

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Useful for control-move timing,
phazing, and sleep-state conversion. This is not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 55 turns.

Players: aminita defeated RUBYBLOOD.

## Teams Revealed

aminita:

- Jynx with Thief and Ice Beam.
- Forretress with Spikes, Toxic, and Rapid Spin.
- Zapdos with Reflect, Thunderbolt, and Whirlwind.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Earthquake, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Starmie with Nightmare and Surf.
- Gengar with Ice Punch.

RUBYBLOOD:

- Snorlax with Earthquake, Double-Edge, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Cloyster with Surf, Spikes, and Explosion.
- Zapdos with Reflect, Thunderbolt, and Whirlwind.
- Steelix with Toxic and Roar.
- Gengar with Hypnosis.
- Jolteon with Growth and Thunder.

## Phase Map

Opening hazard tempo, turns 1-8:

- Jynx steals Leftovers from Snorlax and then gives way to Forretress.
- Forretress sets Spikes on turn 3, poisons Cloyster on turn 4, and removes
  aminita's side of Spikes on turn 5.
- RUBYBLOOD sets Spikes again on turn 6, so aminita has a route but not a clean
  hazard lock.

Phazing pressure against sleeping Snorlax, turns 13-23:

- aminita's Snorlax Rests on turn 13 and is forced out by Zapdos Whirlwind.
- RUBYBLOOD uses Whirlwind again on turns 20, 22, and 23, turning Snorlax's
  sleep turns and aminita's re-entries into a continuing tax.
- This is strong route pressure, not random shuffling: the sleeping anchor is
  denied stable turns while Spikes are active.

Missed sleep into route loss, turns 28-35:

- RUBYBLOOD's Gengar misses Hypnosis twice into Zapdos on turns 28 and 29, then
  faints to Thunderbolt.
- Zapdos then forces Snorlax to Rest, keeps pressure through Sleep Talk turns,
  and Forretress removes aminita's Spikes on turn 35.
- This sequence is a reminder to re-score after a miss: the intended sleep
  route vanished, and the position became about preserving Zapdos and removing
  the hazard tax.

Control move as active route denial, turns 37-38:

- RUBYBLOOD switches sleeping Snorlax into Zapdos on turn 37. aminita uses
  Whirlwind, pulling Jolteon in through Spikes.
- Jolteon uses Growth on turn 38. aminita does not try to win the damage race;
  Zapdos uses Whirlwind again, denying the setup route before it becomes the
  center of the game.
- The key fact is timing: the control move is used while Zapdos is healthy
  enough and the route is live, not after Jolteon has already converted.

Sleep-state cash-out, turns 43-47:

- Snorlax is forced back in asleep and takes Spikes plus Jynx Ice Beam.
- Starmie enters on Sleep Talk Rest, then uses Nightmare and Surf while Snorlax
  is asleep.
- The final Surf on turn 47 crits, so the KO is partly an outcome branch. The
  decision structure is still teachable: aminita identified that the current
  sleep state could be cashed out immediately instead of waiting.

Cleanup, turns 48-55:

- Cloyster trades with Starmie after setting Spikes.
- Jynx removes Zapdos, then Snorlax covers Jolteon. Jolteon's final Thunder
  miss on turn 55 affects the exact ending, but Jolteon had already been forced
  to spend turns trying to rebuild after earlier Whirlwind denial.

## Critical Turns

Turn 5:

- Forretress spins away Spikes immediately after Cloyster sets them. This keeps
  aminita from entering the middle game with a forced switch tax already
  attached to every pivot.

Turns 20-23:

- RUBYBLOOD's Zapdos repeatedly uses Whirlwind into sleeping Snorlax. The move
  is valuable because Snorlax's intended RestTalk stabilization is denied while
  Spikes create a cost on the dragged Pokemon.

Turns 28-29:

- Gengar misses Hypnosis twice and is removed. This is a clean re-score point:
  after the first miss, the sleep route is no longer a script; after the second,
  Gengar is gone and the whole control map changes.

Turn 35:

- Forretress spins again, this time with Cloyster coming in poisoned and
  already paying Spikes. This preserves aminita's switch loop before the final
  control sequence begins.

Turns 37-38:

- Zapdos uses Whirlwind first into sleeping Snorlax, then immediately into
  Growth Jolteon. These are the central turns of the review.
- The move is not "defense because danger exists." It denies two concrete
  routes: sleeping Snorlax gaining stable Sleep Talk turns, and Jolteon using
  Growth to become the next converter.

Turns 45-47:

- Nightmare plus Surf pressures sleeping Snorlax. The turn 47 crit matters, so
  the review should not label this as a guaranteed forced KO.
- The better lesson is that sleep-state opportunities must be cashed out while
  they are live. Waiting can let the target wake, switch, or reset with Rest.

## Practical Lessons

Use phazing when the opponent's route is forming, not only after it is already
winning:

- A control move is best when it denies the next step of the route. Zapdos did
  not need to wait for multiple Growth boosts before using Whirlwind.

Phazing still needs a next-state plan:

- Forced switches are progress when they combine with Spikes, sleep turns,
  poisoned anchors, unfavorable entries, or a preserved answer map.
- Forced switches are much weaker if the new target enters for free and starts
  a stronger route.

After sleep misses, abandon the script:

- Gengar's missed Hypnosis attempts show the same principle as the local sleep
  drills: once the sleep route fails, the next move must be chosen from the new
  state, not from the old plan.

Separate decision quality from outcome quality:

- Starmie's Nightmare line used the sleeping Snorlax state well, but the final
  KO involved a critical hit. The route pressure was real; the exact KO timing
  was not guaranteed from the log alone.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab has many forced-control tools: Roar, Whirlwind, Haze, Encore,
  trapping, sleep, and lock-in moves. The transferable principle is timing:
  use control while it denies a live route and the user still survives the
  punish.
- Do not assume vanilla GSC Roar or Whirlwind timing in the romhack without
  checking local source or fixtures.
- Against boss setup Pokemon, the question is not "can I damage it?" first. It
  is "does damage stop the route, or do I need a control move before the boost,
  lock, sleep state, or support chain converts?"

## New Notebook Lesson

Control moves are route-denial moves. They are strongest when they interrupt
the opponent's next conversion step and leave a playable follow-up state.
