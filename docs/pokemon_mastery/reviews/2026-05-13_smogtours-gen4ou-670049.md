# Review: smogtours-gen4ou-670049

Replay: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen4ou-670049

Source context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/dpp-ou-rate-my-team.3716857/

Format: DPP OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for transferable planning
study, not GSC or romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 22 turns.

Players: Sakito defeated zf.

## Why This Replay Matters

The source context points to this as a high-level example of fast DPP offense
keeping tempo. The portable lesson is not the exact sets or DPP mechanics. It
is the sequence: early hazard, first converter, forced control response,
support trade, second converter, and endgame cleanup.

Stopping the first setup attempt did not end Sakito's route. It only changed
which converter inherited the board.

## Phase Map

Opening hazard and first trade, turns 1-3:

- Sakito's Azelf sets Stealth Rock on turn 1 while zf moves from Azelf to
  Tyranitar.
- Turn 2 denies Tyranitar support with Taunt while Tyranitar misses Fire Blast.
- Turn 3 Explosion leaves Tyranitar badly damaged. Sakito has spent the lead,
  but the hazard remains and zf's Tyranitar no longer works as a long-game
  stabilizer.

First converter checked, turns 4-6:

- Sakito brings in Gyarados and uses Dragon Dance as zf switches to Gyarados.
- zf answers with Thunder Wave on turn 5. This is the correct kind of control:
  it stops the immediate Gyarados route instead of trying to trade generic
  damage.
- zf then moves Jirachi into the locked Outrage on turn 6. The first converter
  has been contained, but it forced a specific answer and revealed zf's control
  structure.

Pressure handoff, turns 7-13:

- Sakito pivots to Metagross while Jirachi uses Substitute.
- Jirachi removes Metagross by turn 10, but it has been cut to low HP in the
  process. Metagross did not sweep; it priced the answer.
- Gengar enters, pressures Tyranitar, and Sakito's own Tyranitar finishes the
  weakened Tyranitar on turn 13. The early Explosion plus hazard damage made
  this endgame material, not just opening noise.

Second trade and cleanup, turns 14-22:

- Machamp lands Dynamic Punch into the returning Gyarados on turn 14, but
  Sakito switches to Gengar as zf moves to Azelf.
- Gengar removes Azelf on turn 16, then Explodes on Dragonite on turn 17. This
  is the decisive handoff: a support attacker becomes a trade piece once it can
  remove the next converter or revenge path.
- Sakito's Tyranitar removes Machamp, Jirachi removes Tyranitar, and Sakito's
  Dragonite removes the low Jirachi.
- zf's Gyarados cannot re-enter through Stealth Rock at the end. The turn-1
  hazard is still part of the final route.

## Critical Turns

Turn 5:

- Thunder Wave on Gyarados is good control because it directly stops the first
  setup route. The mistake would be thinking this also solves the whole
  offensive chain.

Turn 10:

- Removing Metagross is real progress for zf, but Jirachi is now low. Sakito's
  route is not "Metagross wins"; it is "Metagross damages the answer enough
  that the next attacker inherits a better board."

Turn 17:

- Gengar's Explosion removes Dragonite, not because Gengar is low, but because
  the trade deletes a major revenge/converter path and leaves Sakito's remaining
  pieces better positioned.

## Extracted Heuristic

A sequential converter team does not need the first converter to win. The first
converter can force status, reveal the answer, price the answer, or create the
trade map for the next converter.

When advising a boss fight, after stopping the visible setup route, immediately
ask:

```text
Which boss piece inherits this board next?
Which player answer was spent to stop the first converter?
Did the first route leave hazards, status, chip, or a lost pivot behind?
What move denies the next converter without pretending the first one was the
entire fight?
```

## Boss-Battle Transfer

This applies to bosses with staged pressure rather than one ace:

- Bugsy can move from Ariados/Ledian support into Scyther conversion.
- Lance can move from Dragonite or support pressure into the next Dragon route.
- Blue can move from adaptive opener pressure into Gyarados, Tauros, Arcanine,
  or Rhydon.
- Red can move from Pikachu lead pressure into Espeon, Snorlax, sun, or
  Blastoise.

The lesson is not "play offense fast." The lesson is to ask whether the current
answer also survives the next converter.
