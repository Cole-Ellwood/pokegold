# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-804450

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-804450

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for GSC strategy
study, not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 55 turns.

Players: OmBrArch defeated Alice Kazumi.

## Teams Revealed

OmBrArch:

- Snorlax with Curse, Sleep Talk, Rest, Double-Edge.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Toxic, Surf.
- Gengar with Ice Punch, Thunderbolt.
- Zapdos with Thunder, Hidden Power.
- Tyranitar with Rock Slide.
- Alakazam with Psychic, Toxic.

Alice Kazumi:

- Snorlax with Lovely Kiss, Curse, Double-Edge, Earthquake.
- Steelix with Curse, Earthquake, Roar, Explosion.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Toxic, Ice Beam.
- Zapdos with Thunder, Rest, Sleep Talk.
- Starmie with Rapid Spin, Thunder Wave, Surf.
- Marowak with Swords Dance, Earthquake.

## Route Summary

OmBrArch's route became:

```text
Accept Snorlax sleep because Sleep Talk remains functional
-> establish Spikes and poison opposing Cloyster
-> use Gengar to block Rapid Spin and remove Starmie
-> poison opposing Snorlax even at the cost of Alakazam
-> preserve enough Snorlax bulk/boosts to answer Marowak and win the final
   Sleep Talk endgame
```

Alice Kazumi's route became:

```text
Use Lovely Kiss and Steelix Curse pressure to slow opposing Snorlax
-> trade Spikes
-> use Starmie to remove hazards if Gengar can be pressured
-> use Steelix Explosion to remove Zapdos
-> try to convert with Swords Dance Marowak and poisoned Snorlax / sleeping Zapdos
```

Alice's conversion failed because the Starmie spin route was denied, Snorlax was
poisoned before it could stabilize, and Steelix Explosion removed Zapdos but did
not remove OmBrArch's remaining Marowak answer.

## Critical Turns

Turn 1-5: Alice used Lovely Kiss into OmBrArch's SleepTalk CurseLax. The miss on
turn 1 let OmBrArch Curse; the hit on turn 2 still did not fully disable
Snorlax because Sleep Talk called Curse on turn 3 and could still call Rest.
This is a key warning against treating sleep as ownership of the position. A
sleeping SleepTalk anchor can still improve its state.

Turn 7-8: both sides set Spikes, and OmBrArch Toxic'd Alice's Cloyster. This
made Alice's Cloyster a short-lived hazard piece rather than a durable spinner
or long-game wall. The poison later killed it on turn 39.

Turns 23-27: Alice brought Starmie into Tyranitar and took Rock Slide plus
Spikes. OmBrArch then switched Gengar into Rapid Spin, blocking the removal of
Spikes, and used Thunderbolt to remove Starmie despite taking Thunder Wave,
Surf damage, and a full paralysis turn. This was a high-value role trade:
Gengar's health was spent to keep the hazard clock and eliminate the spinner.

Turn 33: OmBrArch's Alakazam used Toxic on Alice's Snorlax and immediately died
to Double-Edge. This looks bad as a one-turn exchange, but it created the timer
that decided the endgame. The poisoned Snorlax later lost too much HP to
Spikes, poison, and recoil to beat SleepTalk Snorlax.

Turns 41-44: OmBrArch tried to restart Snorlax pressure while Alice used Steelix
to Roar and then Explode on Zapdos. The Explosion had a named route: remove the
Ground-immune Electric so Marowak could convert. The problem was incomplete
blocker accounting. Snorlax and Cloyster were still live enough that Marowak did
not get a clean endgame.

Turns 47-49: Marowak entered against Snorlax, used Swords Dance, and still died
to boosted Double-Edge after Earthquake failed to KO. OmBrArch's earlier Curse
state mattered. The correct review is not just "Snorlax is bulky"; it is that
the prior sleep/Sleep Talk/Curse sequence preserved an answer to the Marowak
route after Zapdos was gone.

Turns 50-55: Alice's poisoned Snorlax landed Lovely Kiss on OmBrArch's Snorlax,
but Sleep Talk called Rest on turns 51 and 53. Alice's Snorlax died to poison
and recoil, then Zapdos died to Sleep Talk Double-Edge. This endgame is the
cleanest lesson from the log: RestTalk can make sleep a reset button rather than
a liability when the opponent is on a poison/recoil clock.

## Practical Lessons

- Sleep is target-dependent. Sleeping a non-SleepTalk wall can create a route;
  sleeping a SleepTalk anchor may still leave it able to boost, heal, or attack.
- Spinblocking can be worth major HP and status if the hazard clock is central
  and the spinner is close enough to remove. Gengar did not need to stay healthy
  forever; it needed to deny Starmie's one important job.
- Sacrificing a frail attacker for Toxic is legal only when the poisoned target
  is an anchor and the resulting timer is likely to decide the game. Alakazam's
  Toxic was not generic chip; it changed Snorlax from stabilizer into expiring
  resource.
- Explosion must account for every blocker to the route it opens. Steelix
  removing Zapdos helped Marowak, but Snorlax remained healthy and boosted
  enough to stop the conversion.
- Endgames are often clock contests, not damage races. Poison, recoil, Spikes,
  Sleep Talk Rest calls, and remaining HP determined the final Snorlax mirror
  more than one-turn type advantage.

## Still Unverified

- I did not run exact damage simulations for Steelix Explosion, Marowak
  Earthquake into boosted Snorlax, or the final Sleep Talk branches.
- This replay is vanilla Showdown GSC; Spikes, type, item, and sleep behavior
  must not be promoted to Gym Leader Lab without local verification.
- Alice's Steelix Explosion may have been the best available comeback route, but
  the review still tags the blocker-accounting failure as the strategic lesson.
