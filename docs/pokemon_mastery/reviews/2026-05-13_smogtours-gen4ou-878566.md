## Why This Replay Matters

This is a long hazard-control game, not a clean setup sweep. The portable
lesson is that a hazard plan needs three pieces: setting, retaining, and
removing. If the spinblocker or spinner is spent before the route converts,
the hazard layer can disappear at the exact moment the team needs it most.

For Gym Leader Lab, this matters against bosses such as Pryce, Jasmine, Will,
Janine, Koga, and Bruno. A three-layer Spikes plan is only real if the team can
keep the layers, punish Rapid Spin, or convert the layers before they are
removed.

## Phase Map

Opening hazard race, turns 1-10:

- Rotom chunks Empoleon on turn 1, but Hydro Pump leaves Rotom at 7%. This
  matters later because Rotom is the natural spinblocker and never becomes a
  durable blocker again.
- Tentacruel sets Toxic Spikes on turn 3.
- Bronzong sets Stealth Rock on turn 8.
- Abomasnow changes sand to hail and uses Leech Seed to finish Empoleon on
  turn 10. gritane has hazards plus weather, but the retention piece is already
  heavily damaged.

Spin pressure and trade, turns 11-18:

- Tentacruel spins away gritane's Stealth Rock on turn 13, but is left at 10%.
- Rotom tries to repair with Pain Split against Bronzong but stays very low.
- Click X My Man's Bronzong Explodes on turn 17. gritane's Bronzong survives,
  but Starmie enters into Toxic Spikes and Stealth Rock.
- On turn 18, Starmie uses Rapid Spin into Abomasnow and removes both hazards.
  This is the pivot turn: gritane had hazard pressure, but not enough
  spinblocker health to retain it.

Weather and anchor loop, turns 19-31:

- Abomasnow misses Leech Seed into Jirachi on turn 19.
- Tyranitar repeatedly returns to sand and pressures Gliscor, Bronzong,
  Abomasnow, and Clefable with Crunch.
- Clefable can Wish and Protect, but the game is now about whether the remaining
  material can survive Tyranitar plus Starmie after hazards are gone.
- Bronzong faints on turn 29 and Abomasnow faints on turn 31 after landing
  Leech Seed. The seed pressures Tyranitar but does not restore the lost hazard
  route.

Second hazard attempt and endgame, turns 32-42:

- Tentacruel sets Toxic Spikes again on turn 32.
- Starmie Thunderbolts Rotom on turn 33; hail finishes Rotom, removing the
  spinblock route entirely.
- Starmie spins the Toxic Spikes away on turn 34 while Clefable Knocks Off.
- Tyranitar eventually reveals Superpower to remove Clefable on turn 38.
- Gliscor beats Tyranitar, but Starmie cleans the last two Pokemon with Ice
  Beam and Thunderbolt.

## Critical Turns

Turn 1:

- Rotom's early damage is not just HP loss. It changes the hazard-war map by
  making the spinblocker too fragile to repeatedly cover Starmie. A later
  hazard plan should treat Rotom as a narrow emergency piece, not a durable
  blocker.

Turn 18:

- Starmie's Rapid Spin removes both Toxic Spikes and Stealth Rock. Abomasnow
  does not punish the spin enough to preserve the hazard route. This does not
  prove the switch was wrong by itself, because Rotom was very low, but it
  shows the consequence: the hazard plan had no healthy retention layer.

Turn 32-34:

- Re-setting Toxic Spikes is only useful if the next Starmie entry is punished
  or if the layer immediately converts. Starmie removes the layer two turns
  later, so the turn functions more as a temporary tax than a durable plan.

Turn 38:

- Tyranitar reveals Superpower and removes Clefable. This changes the endgame
  from a Wish/Protect stabilizing route into a forced Gliscor-plus-Tentacruel
  finish. After Gliscor removes Tyranitar, Starmie has the exact coverage and
  HP needed to clean.

## Extracted Heuristic

Hazards are not one resource. Track them as a three-part contract:

```text
set:
  who creates the layer and what it costs

retain:
  who blocks, punishes, statuses, or removes the spinner

convert:
  what route the layer enables before it disappears
```

If the retain part collapses, either convert immediately or stop treating the
hazard layer as the main plan.

## Boss-Battle Transfer

Use this in Gym Leader Lab when a boss has Rapid Spin, hazards, or repeated
forced-switch pressure:

- Pryce: Cloyster and Dewgong make the hazard plan two-sided. Setting Spikes is
  not enough if Dewgong spins without losing route value.
- Will: Forretress and Starmie can split setter/spinner pressure, so the player
  must decide whether to deny hazards, preserve a spinner, or pressure the
  spinner's entry.
- Jasmine and Janine: Explosion can remove a route piece, but only counts as
  hazard control if it denies the current spin/setter route or converts the
  layers already on the field.
- Bruno: Onix's Spikes/Roar pressure matters most when it taxes the real
  Fighting answers before they can do their jobs.

The question is not "are hazards good?" The question is "who wins the next
spin turn, and what route becomes better if the layer survives only briefly?"
