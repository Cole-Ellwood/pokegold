# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-908690

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-908690

Raw log:
https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-908690.log

Forum source:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/smogon-premier-league-xvii-week-5.3777540/

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Useful for route planning study,
not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 47 turns.

Players: M Dragon defeated Fakes.

## Teams Revealed

M Dragon:

- Snorlax with Curse, Body Slam, Thunder, and Rest.
- Zapdos with Thunder, Hidden Power, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Exeggutor with Sleep Powder, Psychic, and Explosion.
- Cloyster with Spikes.
- Golem with Earthquake.
- Gengar with Thief, Thunderbolt, Hidden Power, and Explosion.

Fakes:

- Exeggutor with Sleep Powder and Psychic.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Earthquake, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Forretress with Spikes.
- Golem with Roar.
- Gengar with Hidden Power.
- Zapdos with Thunderbolt and Whirlwind.

## Route Summary

M Dragon's winning route became:

```text
Let Zapdos absorb the opening sleep
-> use Snorlax to pressure Fakes's early Snorlax / Forretress / Golem map
-> set Spikes with Cloyster after Exeggutor's Sleep Powder attempts fail
-> downgrade low-HP Exeggutor from sleep pressure into Explosion material
-> remove RestTalk Snorlax with Exeggutor Explosion
-> remove Exeggutor with Gengar Explosion
-> reduce the game to preserved Zapdos versus Zapdos + weakened Forretress
```

Fakes's plausible route became:

```text
Sleep Zapdos immediately
-> set Spikes with Forretress
-> use Golem and Zapdos phazing to tax M Dragon's switches
-> lean on RestTalk Snorlax as the central stabilizer
-> keep Zapdos healthy enough to win after Spikes and trades
```

That route failed because Golem, Gengar, Snorlax, and Exeggutor were all
removed before Zapdos could convert. M Dragon's own Zapdos looked inactive for
much of the game, but the rest of the route kept removing the pieces that made
it inactive.

## Critical Turns

Turn 1:

- M Dragon switches Zapdos into Exeggutor's Sleep Powder. This is not just
  "something had to sleep." Zapdos has Rest and Sleep Talk, so it is a credible
  sleep absorber and future converter.
- The opening choice protects Snorlax and Exeggutor from losing their early
  jobs while accepting that Zapdos may need a long support plan before it can
  matter again.

Turns 6-9:

- Fakes sets Spikes with Forretress, then M Dragon keeps Snorlax in and attacks
  into Golem. Golem is paralyzed, Roars Snorlax out once, and then falls to
  Gengar on turn 9.
- The critical point is route damage, not the immediate scoreline. Once Golem
  is gone, Fakes's phazing and Zapdos-control structure is thinner.

Turns 11-15:

- Exeggutor misses Sleep Powder into Snorlax and Exeggutor multiple times, then
  takes a large Double-Edge on turn 14.
- The useful lesson is not "keep clicking sleep until it works." The role of
  Exeggutor changes after the misses and damage. It is no longer a comfortable
  repeated sleep threat; it becomes a low-HP piece that still has Psychic and
  Explosion as one-time route tools.

Turn 16:

- Cloyster sets Spikes while taking another Snorlax Double-Edge. This looks
  expensive because Cloyster drops to 36 after Leftovers, but the layer gives
  every later forced grounded switch a cost.
- The layer matters immediately: Gengar, Snorlax, Forretress, and Exeggutor all
  later enter through it before the final Zapdos endgame.

Turns 20-25:

- M Dragon uses Rest with Snorlax, wakes into Curse on turn 23, then lands Body
  Slam paralysis on Fakes's Snorlax before both Snorlax use Rest on turn 25.
- This sequence keeps M Dragon's Snorlax useful without pretending it must be
  the only win condition. It buys time and damages the opposing anchor enough
  for later trade routes to matter.

Turns 32-35:

- Zapdos Sleep Talks Thunder into sleeping Snorlax on turn 32, but Fakes's
  Snorlax Sleep Talks Rest on turn 33 and resets to full HP.
- M Dragon does not keep treating chip damage as the route after that reset.
  Exeggutor uses Psychic once, survives Earthquake, then Explodes on turn 35
  to remove Snorlax.
- This is the cleanest lesson in the game: when the anchor resets, ask whether
  a one-time converter is now more valuable than continuing the same pressure.

Turns 36-37:

- Gengar immediately Explodes on Exeggutor. The second Explosion is not a
  random low-HP button; it removes another piece that could block, punish, or
  complicate the preserved Zapdos route.
- The two Explosions form a trade cascade with a named purpose: remove the
  stabilizer, then remove the next support piece.

Turns 42-46:

- M Dragon's Zapdos Sleep Talks Rest twice, then uses Hidden Power and Thunder
  to win the Zapdos duel at very low HP.
- There is outcome variance in the Sleep Talk rolls and damage, but the route
  existed because Zapdos had been preserved since turn 1 and the opposing
  support map had been removed around it.

## Practical Lessons

Early sleep absorption can be a route commitment:

- A sleeping Pokemon is not automatically spent. If it has Rest, Sleep Talk,
  strong matchup value, or unique endgame utility, the rest of the battle can
  be played to keep that route alive.

Downgrade a damaged role instead of discarding the piece:

- Exeggutor's repeated Sleep Powder misses changed its job. It stopped being a
  comfortable sleep-pressure piece, but it still had a high-value Explosion
  target. This is the right mental model for bad luck branches: update the
  piece's remaining jobs instead of mourning the original one.

Trade cascades need a named destination:

- Exeggutor Explosion into Snorlax and Gengar Explosion into Exeggutor were
  correct-looking because they pointed to the same endgame. If those trades had
  removed impressive but replaceable targets while leaving the actual Zapdos
  blockers intact, they would have been much weaker.

Spikes can be worth an ugly setup turn:

- Cloyster paid a lot of HP to set Spikes, but the layer taxed enough later
  switch-ins to support the route conversion. The test is not whether the
  setter looks healthy afterward; it is whether the layer changes future
  switches, rests, trades, or endgame thresholds.

## Romhack Transfer

- Against Gym Leader Lab bosses, a Pokemon that gets slept or heavily damaged
  may still have a narrower job: one setup turn, one revenge hit, one pivot,
  one status move, one item turn, or one sacrifice entry.
- Do not import the exact GSC Snorlax or Spikes math into the romhack without
  checking local mechanics. Transfer the planning idea: when a piece loses its
  original role, re-label its remaining jobs before deciding it is expendable.
- Boss route sheets should include a "degraded job" field for key pieces:
  what can this Pokemon still do if it misses, gets slept, is chipped, or loses
  its first intended target?

## New Notebook Lesson

Bad luck does not only change the board; it changes job labels. After a miss,
crit, wake, phaze, or unexpected damage, downgrade each affected Pokemon into
its remaining real jobs before choosing the next move. A damaged sleep user may
become an Explosion converter, a sleeping attacker may become the preserved
endgame, and a weakened wall may still be the one-turn stop needed to win.
