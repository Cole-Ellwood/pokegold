# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-935507

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-935507

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Useful for long-game planning,
hazard-war review, and sacrifice timing. This is not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 155 turns.

Players: mrsoup defeated Seanobiwan.

## Teams Revealed

Seanobiwan:

- Cloyster with Spikes.
- Raikou with Thunder, Hidden Power, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Skarmory with Toxic, Curse, Drill Peck, and Rest.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Flamethrower, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Golem with Earthquake, Rapid Spin, and Explosion.
- Jynx with Ice Beam.

mrsoup:

- Misdreavus with Thunder, Hypnosis, Pain Split, and Psychic.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Ice Beam, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Surf, and Toxic.
- Jolteon with Thunderbolt, Growth, and Substitute.
- Donphan with Earthquake, Rapid Spin, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Skarmory with Drill Peck.

## Phase Map

Opening sleep and first hazard edge, turns 1-13:

- Seanobiwan switches Cloyster out of Misdreavus into Raikou on turn 1.
- Misdreavus lands Hypnosis on Raikou on turn 2, forcing Seanobiwan to spend
  early turns through Sleep Talk and switches.
- mrsoup brings in Cloyster and sets Spikes on turn 5. From that point,
  Seanobiwan's Raikou, Snorlax, Cloyster, Golem, and Jynx all pay repeated entry
  costs.

Jolteon pressure and counter-hazards, turns 14-44:

- Jolteon uses Growth and Substitute to force Snorlax into Rest cycles.
- Seanobiwan's Cloyster sets Spikes on turn 26, but Donphan removes that layer
  with Rapid Spin on turn 37.
- Cloyster sets Spikes again on turn 40, so the game becomes a two-sided hazard
  war rather than a clean one-sided clock.

Spinner trade, turns 45-65:

- Golem pressures mrsoup's Snorlax and removes Seanobiwan's own Spikes with
  Rapid Spin on turn 55.
- On turn 63, Donphan's Sleep Talk calls Rapid Spin and removes Seanobiwan's
  Spikes before Golem uses Explosion to KO Donphan.
- That Explosion removes future spin access, but it does not preserve the
  current layer. After the trade, Seanobiwan has lost Golem and mrsoup still has
  Cloyster, which immediately sets Spikes again on turn 65.

Hazard reset into anchor fight, turns 66-112:

- Seanobiwan's low Cloyster sets Spikes one more time on turn 73, then faints
  to Sleep Talk Double-Edge.
- Both sides have hazards, but the remaining shape favors mrsoup's Snorlax and
  route pressure because Seanobiwan's spinner is gone and Skarmory is being
  forced into repeated Rest and damage sequences.
- Turn 103 is a major luck branch: Snorlax freezes Skarmory with Ice Beam.
  The freeze matters, but the prior hazard and Rest pressure had already made
  Skarmory's role expensive.

Endgame conversion, turns 113-155:

- Seanobiwan tries to hold the game with Raikou and RestTalk Snorlax.
- Skarmory eventually faints on turn 124, Snorlax faints on turn 136, and
  Raikou is left trying to outlast mrsoup's Snorlax alone.
- Raikou forces several Snorlax Rest cycles, but cannot convert before Snorlax
  gets enough Double-Edge turns to win on turn 155.

## Critical Turns

Turn 5:

- Cloyster sets the first Spikes after Raikou has already been put to sleep.
  This is strong sequencing: sleep did not win by itself, but it created the
  tempo for the first long-game tax.

Turns 26 and 37:

- Seanobiwan gets Spikes up with Cloyster, then Donphan removes them. This is
  the first warning that "I set hazards" is incomplete. The relevant question
  is whether the layer survives long enough to change a route.

Turn 55:

- Golem spins away mrsoup's Spikes while Snorlax is asleep. This keeps
  Seanobiwan's defensive loop affordable for a while. A spinner does not need
  to live forever if it performs the job at the decisive time.

Turn 63:

- Donphan's Sleep Talk calls Rapid Spin and removes Seanobiwan's Spikes before
  Golem's Explosion KOs Donphan.
- The KO is real, but the timing changes the trade. Golem did not stop this
  spin; it stopped future spins. Whether that is enough depends on the
  post-trade hazard map.

Turn 65:

- mrsoup's Cloyster sets Spikes again immediately after Golem is gone. This is
  the conversion check after the sacrifice: removing the spinner did not solve
  the hazard war because the opposing setter still had a clean reset turn.

Turn 73:

- Seanobiwan's Cloyster sets Spikes again at low HP and faints to Sleep Talk
  Double-Edge. The layer matters, but the setter is now gone, so the game is
  committed to the current hazard state.

Turns 98-103:

- Skarmory Rests, tries to build with Curse and Drill Peck, then gets frozen by
  Snorlax Ice Beam. The freeze is a high-impact outcome branch, but the review
  should not collapse the whole game into "freeze decided it." The earlier
  hazard and Rest cycles had already made Skarmory's job narrow.

Turns 112-155:

- Seanobiwan's Raikou and Snorlax force repeated Rest cycles from mrsoup's
  Snorlax, but they do not have enough route pressure left. The endgame is not
  about raw HP alone; it is about whether Raikou's damage changes Snorlax's
  Rest timing before Double-Edge closes the game.

## Practical Lessons

Do not count a sacrifice as hazard control unless it changes the post-trade
hazard map:

- Explosion on a spinner after the spinner has already removed your layer is
  different from Explosion before the spin happens.
- The first case removes future spin access. The second preserves the current
  layer. Those are not the same route.

After any sacrifice in a hazard war, ask the conversion check:

```text
Who can set next?
Who can spin next?
Can either side block or punish the next spin?
Which remaining Pokemon are taxed by the layer?
Did the sacrifice remove the current problem, or only a future copy of it?
```

Sleep Talk branches must be priced before committing a one-time resource:

- Turn 63 is a useful branch example because Donphan's Sleep Talk selected
  Rapid Spin before Golem's Explosion. The correct review question is not "did
  Explosion KO?" It is "what had already happened before Explosion executed?"

Do not over-credit the final luck branch:

- The Skarmory freeze was important, but the earlier hazard map, Rest cycles,
  spinner trade, and route pressure explain why Skarmory was in a narrow role
  in the first place.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab has three-layer Spikes, so the exact layer math is different.
  The transfer is the sacrifice audit, not the vanilla damage value.
- Against boss teams with Rapid Spin, Haze, phazing, or hazard setters, a
  sacrifice that removes one support piece must still be followed by a roster
  check: can another boss Pokemon immediately recreate the same problem?
- If a player Pokemon Explodes, faints to recoil, or is spent as a sack after a
  boss support move already resolved, review the turn by state transition, not
  by the KO message.

## New Notebook Lesson

In a hazard war, "remove the spinner" is not specific enough. The policy must
say whether the move denies the current spin, denies future spins, removes the
setter, preserves the user's own removal route, or opens a separate win route.
