## Phase Review

Opening hazard contest, turns 1-15:

- Both players start Jynx and immediately pivot to Snorlax, then to Cloyster.
- THE PDC SHOW gets the first Spikes layer on turn 3; Glfgno7 answers with
  Spikes on turn 4.
- Golem removes THE PDC SHOW's side of Spikes on turns 7 and 14, but Glfgno7's
  Cloyster re-sets the layer on turns 8 and 15.
- This creates a recurring route question: can Golem's spin turns buy enough
  freedom, or will Cloyster keep re-imposing the switch tax?

Item removal into sleep pressure, turns 16-21:

- THE PDC SHOW's Jynx uses Thief on Snorlax on turn 16 and takes Leftovers.
- Jynx then lands Lovely Kiss on turn 17, attacks on turn 18, and sleeps Snorlax
  again on turn 19 after an early wake.
- This is not just a damage sequence. Removing Leftovers means every future hit,
  Spikes entry, and sleep turn is less likely to be erased passively.
- The line remains branchy: Sleep Talk can still call Earthquake or Rest, and it
  does both later. The item removal improves the route; it does not disable the
  anchor.

Counter-sleep and phaze/trade turn, turns 22-27:

- Glfgno7's Jynx steals Jynx's stolen Leftovers on turn 22 and later puts THE
  PDC SHOW's Snorlax to sleep on turn 24.
- Glfgno7 switches Steelix into sleeping Snorlax on turn 25, then Roars on turn
  26 and Explodes on Zapdos on turn 27.
- Steelix's trade removes Zapdos, but it also removes Glfgno7's phaze/trade
  resource. The next question becomes whether Gengar can finish the remaining
  Snorlax route quickly enough.

Dynamic Punch endgame attempt, turns 28-35:

- Glfgno7's Gengar attacks sleeping Snorlax with Dynamic Punch repeatedly.
- The line has a high payoff because confusion plus damage can stop Snorlax from
  stabilizing.
- The line is also fragile: several misses occur, and Sleep Talk repeatedly
  calls Rest or Earthquake.
- On turn 35, Sleep Talk Earthquake removes Gengar. THE PDC SHOW's Snorlax
  survives the endgame pressure.

## Decision Quality Notes

Thief on Snorlax was route-improving:

- Snorlax was an anchor expected to switch into Jynx and keep answering pressure
  over many turns.
- Removing Leftovers made Ice Beam, Spikes, and later sleep turns matter more.
- The follow-up was coherent: Lovely Kiss plus damage, not item removal as a
  standalone trick.

Steelix Explosion was route-converting but expensive:

- Removing Zapdos made sense if Zapdos was the key remaining pressure route.
- The cost was losing Roar and Steelix's ability to interrupt later Snorlax or
  Gengar-adjacent routes.
- After that trade, Glfgno7 needed Gengar's Dynamic Punch route to work.

Dynamic Punch was a forced-looking route, not a clean route:

- Gengar needed to break Snorlax before Sleep Talk Rest or Earthquake stabilized
  it.
- Misses and Sleep Talk rolls were not review errors by themselves; they were
  part of the priced branch.
- The lesson is to label this as high-variance conversion after other routes
  were spent, not as a stable endgame.

## Practical Lessons

Item removal must name the clock it changes:

- Thief mattered because Snorlax wanted repeated passive recovery while taking
  Spikes, sleep pressure, and direct damage.
- Removing a recovery item is strongest when the target must keep entering or
  absorbing pressure later.

Sleep after item removal is still not a script:

- Snorlax continued acting through Sleep Talk and early wake branches.
- The correct review is not "Jynx slept Snorlax, therefore the route is won."
  It is "Jynx removed passive recovery, then used sleep and damage to make each
  Snorlax turn more expensive."

One-time trades demand a next-route audit:

- Steelix's Explosion spent a phazing resource. After a trade like that, the
  next question is not whether the KO was valuable in isolation; it is which
  remaining route now has to win.

## Boss Transfer

- Against Gym Leader Lab anchors with Leftovers, Mint Berry, Focus Band, Choice
  Band, Life Orb, or type-boost items, treat the item as part of the route map.
- Removing or exploiting an item is correct only if the follow-up is named:
  force recovery, deny setup, open a damage threshold, remove a one-time safety
  branch, or create clean entry.
- Local Thief exists and can steal non-mail held items when the user has no
  item, but Knock Off and Trick are not local move-table options.
- Boss AI may not preserve pieces like a human, and standard local AI scoring
  discourages Thief unless it is the only move available. Item-removal plans
  still need current roster, AI, and damage evidence.
