# Battle Review: smogtours-gen2ou-902742

Source:

- Replay log: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-902742.log
- Tournament source: SPL XVII GSC discussion,
  https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/spl-xvii-gsc-discussion.3775984/
- Supporting principle: GSC Thief discussion,
  https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/thief.3543261/
- Supporting principle: GSC Explosion guide,
  https://www.smogon.com/gs/articles/guide_to_explosion

Players: elodin defeated RUBYBLOOD.

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is expert-play study, not
romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 89 turns.

## Revealed Structure

elodin:

- Exeggutor with Thief, Leech Seed, Psychic, and Explosion.
- Raikou with Thunder, Hidden Power, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Cloyster with Spikes, Toxic, Surf, and Explosion.
- Snorlax with Lovely Kiss and Earthquake revealed.
- Golem with Rapid Spin, Roar, and Earthquake.
- Vaporeon with Growth and Surf.

RUBYBLOOD:

- Snorlax with Curse, Double-Edge, and Rest.
- Misdreavus with Toxic, Thunderbolt, and Sleep Talk.
- Forretress with Spikes and Rapid Spin.
- Raikou with Thunderbolt, Hidden Power, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Skarmory with Drill Peck and Whirlwind.
- Suicune with Toxic and Surf.

## Phase Review

Opening item and sleep contract, turns 1-18:

- Exeggutor uses Thief as Snorlax pivots to Misdreavus, stealing Misdreavus's
  Leftovers and gaining Leftovers itself.
- RUBYBLOOD immediately gets value back by poisoning elodin's Raikou with
  Misdreavus, so the Thief turn is not a free win.
- elodin's Snorlax then sleeps Misdreavus with Lovely Kiss on turn 7. This
  matters later because Misdreavus is the Ghost that can interfere with
  Explosion endgames.
- Golem removes the early Spikes tax on turn 17, then Roars Snorlax on turn 18
  and drags in a sleeping Raikou through elodin's Spikes.

Midgame anchor control, turns 19-43:

- RUBYBLOOD uses Skarmory and Forretress to deny clean setup and remove Spikes.
- elodin poisons Snorlax with Cloyster on turn 28, then uses Golem's Roar on
  turn 30 to stop Snorlax from simply converting Curse plus Double-Edge.
- Golem is not expendable just because it is chipped. It still has the spin and
  phaze jobs that keep the route map playable.
- RUBYBLOOD sets Spikes again on turn 43, forcing elodin to play the rest of
  the game with entry costs attached to every pivot.

Vaporeon pressure into phaze resistance, turns 44-67:

- Vaporeon uses Growth on turn 45, but Raikou enters to stop the clean route.
- Later, Vaporeon boosts again into Skarmory on turn 65. Skarmory Whirlwinds
  it on turn 66, but only after taking Surf to 29%.
- The setup did not sweep immediately, but it still changed the board. Skarmory
  was moved into range for Raikou later, while Golem used its last HP to absorb
  the next Skarmory turn and give Vaporeon clean re-entry.

Converter window, turns 68-80:

- Vaporeon boosts against sleeping Raikou on turn 68 and pressures through
  Sleep Talk branches. It survives Thunderbolt on turn 71 and KOs Raikou.
- elodin then uses Raikou to remove Suicune on turns 74-76. This is a route
  handoff: Vaporeon breaks the electric answer, then Raikou takes over against
  the bulky Water.
- Forretress spins elodin's Spikes away on turn 80, so the endgame cannot be
  credited to hazard pressure alone.

One-time trade endgame, turns 82-89:

- Exeggutor removes Misdreavus on turn 84. This is the key blocker cleanup
  before the final Explosion sequence.
- Raikou removes Skarmory on turn 86.
- Cloyster Explodes on Forretress on turn 88 after Cloyster is low and
  Forretress is the remaining support blocker.
- Exeggutor Explodes on sleeping Snorlax on turn 89. The final trade works
  because the Ghost is gone and the earlier support jobs have already been
  cashed out.

## Critical Turns

Turn 1:

- Thief does two jobs: it removes Misdreavus's passive recovery and gives
  Exeggutor a long-game item.
- The second job matters. Exeggutor later survives entry damage, poison, and
  repeated turns well enough to remove Misdreavus and still trade with Snorlax.

Turn 7:

- Lovely Kiss on Misdreavus is not just "sleep something." It disables the
  Ghost that can punish Explosion timing and makes later one-time trades easier
  to plan.

Turns 17-18:

- Golem spins and then phazes. This is a support piece doing two separate
  route jobs, not a generic physical wall entering because it has good typing.

Turns 28-30:

- Toxic on Snorlax plus Roar from Golem prevents the Curse route from becoming
  a stable midgame plan. Golem spends HP, but it spends it on a real job.

Turns 65-71:

- Vaporeon's Growth line is not invalidated by Whirlwind just because the
  immediate sweep ends. Surf damage puts Skarmory in range, and sleeping Raikou
  becomes a real converter target afterward.

Turns 83-89:

- elodin waits until Misdreavus and Skarmory are gone before spending the final
  Explosions. The endgame is not "low HP means boom." It is blocker map first,
  trade second.

## Practical Lessons

Item removal can change the user as much as the target:

- Thief is often described as removing Leftovers from a wall, but this replay
  also shows the stolen item keeping Exeggutor useful for 80 more turns.
- In boss advice, ask whether the item-control move creates a durable future
  job for the user, not only whether the target loses an item.

Sleep can prepare a later trade lane:

- Sleeping Misdreavus made the Ghost less able to interfere with elodin's long
  Explosion plan. The sleep was not immediately converted into a sweep, but it
  changed which endgames were safe to pursue.

Low-HP support is still support if the job remains live:

- Golem was repeatedly chipped, but its Rapid Spin and Roar turns were route
  material. It became expendable only after those jobs had mostly been spent.

Setup pressure can be partial progress:

- Vaporeon did not need to sweep on its first boosted turn to justify the line.
  If setup damages the phazer, reveals the answer, or creates a later cleaner
  route, the move can still be productive.

Explosion must wait for the blocker map:

- The final double Explosion line works because Misdreavus is gone and the
  remaining targets are Forretress and Snorlax. Earlier booms would have carried
  more Ghost, phaze, and support-loss risk.

## Boss Transfer

- Against Gym Leader Lab bosses, identify the pieces that block one-time
  trades: Ghosts, Protect, Substitute, Endure, Focus Band, passive type effects,
  speed/priority, and any local Explosion behavior.
- A low-HP spinner, phazer, sleep user, or exploder should not be labeled spent
  until its remaining job is named and checked against the boss route.
- If the user's final route depends on Explosion, Destiny Bond, Perish Song, or
  a controlled sack, build the blocker map first. The move rises only after the
  immunity/protection/preservation problem is solved.
- Do not import the exact GSC interaction blindly. Romhack advice needs local
  source or fixture checks for type chart, passive type abilities, item effects,
  Protection states, and move execution order.
