## Revealed Structure

underlying:

- Zapdos with Hidden Power / Rest / Sleep Talk / Thunder.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge / Lovely Kiss / Self-Destruct / Thunder.
- Cloyster with Explosion / Spikes.
- Machamp with Cross Chop / Curse / Light Screen / Rock Slide.
- Steelix with Curse / Earthquake / Roar / Rock Slide.

MrSoup:

- Cloyster with Rapid Spin / Spikes.
- Snorlax with Defense Curl / Rest / Rollout / Toxic.
- Skarmory with Curse / Rest / Toxic / Whirlwind.

## Phase Review

The opening is not solved by species labels, turns 1-24:

- Zapdos leads into Cloyster, then underlying pivots to Snorlax as MrSoup also
  brings in Snorlax.
- Lovely Kiss puts Skarmory to sleep on turn 2, but MrSoup immediately starts
  revealing a nonstandard support shell: Dragonite Reflect, Blissey Present /
  Light Screen, and Umbreon Mean Look.
- Blissey's Present drops Cloyster from full HP to 10% on turn 6. From public
  information, Blissey is no longer just a passive special wall in this game.
- Underlying spends Snorlax's Self-Destruct on turn 24 to remove Umbreon. The
  trap route is gone, but the remaining screen / Haze / Present / phaze shell
  still has routes.

The support shell survives the first trade, turns 25-89:

- Blissey heals the team with Heal Bell on turn 25.
- Dragonite repeatedly uses Reflect and Haze to deny Steelix setup. Haze is a
  route reset, not just a defensive flourish.
- Cloyster gets Rapid Spin value on turn 89, removing Spikes from MrSoup's
  side before fainting. The spinner still did its job even though it died.

Vaporeon tests whether Blissey is setup bait, turns 96-127:

- Vaporeon starts a Growth route against Blissey on turn 96.
- Blissey has already shown Present, Light Screen, Heal Bell, and Soft-Boiled,
  so the question is not "Can Blissey be set up on by type?" It is whether the
  revealed damage, screen, and recovery loop lets Vaporeon convert before
  being forced to Rest.
- Present drops Vaporeon from 82% to 6% on turn 107. Vaporeon Rests on turn
  108 and keeps trying, but it eventually faints to Present on turn 127.
- The important lesson is not that Present is generally reliable. It is that a
  revealed damage function must replace the old "passive wall" label.

Skarmory turns Spikes and Whirlwind into a resource drain, turns 128-209:

- Cloyster Explodes into Skarmory on turn 129 but does not remove it.
- Skarmory recovers with Rest and later uses Whirlwind repeatedly while Spikes
  are on underlying's side.
- Machamp is forced through Spikes enough times that it reaches low HP, then
  is poisoned by Toxic on turn 207 and faints to poison after attacking on
  turn 209.
- This is a public route conversion: Whirlwind is progress because Spikes and
  poison make the forced entries worse each time.

The delayed Rollout route appears late, turns 286-310:

- Snorlax shows Toxic into Zapdos on turn 287, then Defense Curl on turns 288,
  289, 295, and 296.
- Before Rollout is revealed, Defense Curl is not proof of the fourth move, but
  it is a strong reason to preserve phazing and avoid treating Snorlax as
  harmless setup.
- Rollout starts on turn 297. Steelix eventually Roars Snorlax out on turn
  300, but the route returns later.
- On turns 306-307, Snorlax survives Thunder damage, is paralyzed, and still
  uses Rollout to KO Zapdos. Steelix then removes Snorlax with Earthquake on
  turns 309-310.

The final board is Haze plus immunity plus PP, turns 311-337:

- After Snorlax falls, Steelix is the last underlying Pokemon.
- Dragonite repeatedly uses Haze or switches around Steelix's Ground attacks,
  while Skarmory can absorb Earthquake entirely.
- Steelix eventually reaches Struggle on turn 329. Underlying forfeits on turn
  337.
- The ending is not about a single flashy surprise. It is the accumulated result
  of revealed functions: screens, Haze, phazing, Present damage, Spikes,
  Rollout pressure, Flying immunity, and PP.

## Critical Turns

Turn 6:

- Present chunks Cloyster from full HP to 10%. This publicly invalidates the
  assumption that Blissey has no immediate damage function.

Turn 24:

- Snorlax Self-Destruct removes Umbreon. The trap route is denied, but the
  rest of MrSoup's support shell remains intact.

Turn 89:

- Cloyster removes Spikes with Rapid Spin before fainting. A support Pokemon
  can be successful if it completes its job before dying.

Turns 107 and 127:

- Present first forces Vaporeon into emergency recovery, then later KOs it.
  The setup route failed because the "wall" had revealed damage, screen, and
  recovery functions.

Turns 190-209:

- Skarmory's Whirlwind and Toxic convert Spikes into a Machamp HP clock. The
  route is slow, but each forced entry is real progress.

Turns 297-310:

- Rollout reveals the payoff for earlier Defense Curl. Steelix Roar is the
  right class of answer once the lock route appears, but the Rollout route
  returns later and removes Zapdos.

Turns 329-337:

- Steelix is reduced to Struggle against Dragonite / Skarmory immunity and
  Haze control. PP became the final route map.

## Practical Lessons

Reclassify by revealed function:

- Once a Pokemon reveals damage, trapping, Haze, phazing, spin, screens, or a
  lock route, stop using the old species label as the plan. The relevant label
  is the function that now changes the route.

A surprise is not only a one-turn lure:

- Present Blissey did not just win one exchange. It changed whether Vaporeon
  could treat Blissey as setup bait for dozens of turns.

Do not overinfer hidden companion moves:

- Defense Curl suggests a possible Rollout route, but before Rollout is shown
  the correct public conclusion is "preserve the anti-lock answer and watch for
  the companion move," not "the full set is known."

Support jobs can succeed before death:

- Cloyster fainted after Rapid Spin, but it had already changed the hazard map.
  Judge the support piece by whether its job resolves, not by whether it lives.

PP is a route, not a tiebreaker:

- The final Steelix position was decided by move immunity, Haze, and spent PP.
  Long-game advice must track whether the last answer still has the PP to be
  an answer.

## Boss Transfer

- Do not import Pokemon Showdown's Present damage into Gym Leader Lab advice.
  Local Present must be checked through `engine/battle/move_effects/present.asm`,
  `data/moves/present_power.asm`, and damage evidence; the local move table
  lists `PRESENT` as fixed-effect data.
- Rollout transfer is more direct, but still local: check
  `engine/battle/move_effects/rollout.asm`, local BP/accuracy/PP, Defense Curl
  state, miss behavior, lock state, Haze/phazing legality, and boss rosters.
- Whitney Miltank already has a Rollout commitment drill. This replay reinforces
  that Rollout is a conversion route after the board supports it, not a move to
  recommend because the species is famous for it.
- Clair, Koga, Janine, Pryce, and Red-style fights can all create similar
  reclassification moments through Haze, phazing, spin, traps, screens, or
  recovery. Use local source and observed public state before importing the
  expert-game route.
- Boss AI may react to revealed player moves, observed damage, status, hazards,
  screens, traps, and public move effects. It must not use unrevealed player
  team, hidden moves, hidden items, or future knowledge to decide that a
  surprise route exists or is absent.

## New Notebook Lesson

After a nonstandard move is publicly revealed, reclassify the Pokemon by the
function it now performs. A wall with demonstrated damage is not free setup
bait; a passive-looking setup move can signal a delayed lock route; a support
piece that completes its job before fainting may already have converted value.
