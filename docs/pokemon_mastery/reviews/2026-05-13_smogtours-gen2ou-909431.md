# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-909431

Source: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-909431

Format: vanilla GSC OU on Pokemon Showdown. Useful for GSC long-game planning,
not romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 139 turns.

Players: A Real Jester defeated choolio IGLESIAS.

## Teams Revealed

A Real Jester:

- Articuno with Toxic and Ice Beam.
- Tyranitar with Rock Slide and Roar.
- Forretress with Spikes, Toxic, Rest, and Defense Curl.
- Starmie with Substitute, Surf, and Rapid Spin.
- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Toxic, Flamethrower, and Rest.
- Raikou with Thunderbolt, Crunch, Rest, and Sleep Talk.

choolio IGLESIAS:

- Snorlax with Double-Edge, Earthquake, Rest, and Sleep Talk.
- Gengar with Thunderbolt, Ice Punch, and Explosion.
- Cloyster with Surf, Spikes, and Toxic.
- Zapdos with Thunder and Whirlwind.
- Steelix with Curse, Earthquake, and Explosion.
- Jolteon with Substitute, Growth, and Thunderbolt.

## Route Summary

A Real Jester's winning route became:

```text
Toxic Snorlax immediately
-> set Spikes first
-> remove own Spikes with Starmie even at the cost of Starmie later
-> poison Zapdos with Forretress
-> let Steelix Explosion remove Articuno but preserve Raikou + Forretress
-> cycle Raikou into Zapdos/Jolteon and Forretress into Snorlax until Spikes,
   poison, Rest timing, and low Jolteon HP make choolio's remaining routes fail
```

choolio's plausible route became:

```text
Use Cloyster Spikes plus Zapdos Whirlwind to tax switches
-> remove Starmie with Gengar Explosion so Spikes can stick
-> use Jolteon Growth/Substitute to overload Snorlax/Raikou
-> use Steelix Explosion to trade out Articuno
-> win with Zapdos/Jolteon pressure plus RestTalk Snorlax
```

The route failed because A Real Jester spun the hazards off before Starmie died,
poisoned Zapdos, kept Forretress healthy enough to wall Snorlax repeatedly, and
kept Raikou as the Electric answer even after paralysis and Rest cycles.

## Critical Turns

Turn 1: Articuno used Toxic on Snorlax. This did not beat Snorlax by itself,
but it immediately made Snorlax's early offense expire into Rest timing and
created a long-game habit: choolio's anchors would be forced to spend turns
resetting rather than only attacking.

Turn 8: Forretress set the first Spikes. From then on, choolio's Snorlax,
Cloyster, Gengar, Steelix, and Jolteon all paid a visible switching tax. This
became decisive only because A Real Jester later removed hazards from his own
side.

Turn 12: Starmie used Rapid Spin while behind Substitute. This preserved A Real
Jester's switch loop. Without this spin, the later Raikou/Forretress cycling
would have been much more expensive.

Turns 16-18: Snorlax poisoned Cloyster and then removed it. This cut off
choolio's hazard setter early. The important point is not the KO alone; it is
that A Real Jester could keep one-sided Spikes as the long-game clock.

Turn 21: Gengar Exploded into Starmie. This removed the spinner after it had
already performed its most important job. The trade helped choolio by removing
future spin access, but it did not undo the earlier hazard removal.

Turns 23-29: Jolteon used Substitute and Growth to break through Snorlax. This
was choolio's best conversion window. A Real Jester lost Snorlax but left
Jolteon low enough that Spikes and Raikou could contain it later.

Turn 36: Forretress took Zapdos Thunder and landed Toxic. This was the pivot of
the long endgame. Once Zapdos was poisoned, every later Zapdos entry had a
finite clock, and A Real Jester could stop trying to "win quickly."

Turn 38: Starmie spun again and died to Thunder. This looks ugly by immediate
material count, but it made the final Raikou/Forretress loop possible because
A Real Jester's side no longer had to pay Spikes on every pivot.

Turn 44: Steelix Exploded on Articuno. That trade removed a visible annoyance,
but it did not remove the actual endgame structure: Raikou answered the
Electrics, Forretress answered Snorlax, and choolio's grounded pivots still
paid Spikes.

Turns 48-139: Forretress and Raikou played a sealed loop. Forretress often used
Defense Curl or even failed Spikes attempts while choolio's Snorlax failed to
make progress. These moves were not route-starting moves; they were waiting
moves inside a position where the opponent's Zapdos was poisoned, Jolteon was
near dead on Spikes, and Snorlax could not break Forretress fast enough.

Turn 134: Zapdos finally fainted to poison after repeated Raikou pivots. This
was the delayed payoff from turn 36, not a random late-game event.

## Practical Lessons

- A no-progress move can be correct only inside a contained winning loop. Failed
  Spikes clicks are normally state errors, but here Forretress was using low
  value turns to preserve the loop while the opponent's poisoned Zapdos and
  Spikes-hit Jolteon expired.
- The difference between "no progress" and "waiting" is opponent route access.
  If the opponent can set up, heal for free, spin, or remove an answer, waiting
  is bad. If the opponent is already on a clock and all punish branches are
  covered, waiting can be the route.
- Spinning before the spinner dies can be enough. Starmie did not need to
  survive forever; it needed to remove hazards at the right time so the final
  defensive loop remained affordable.
- Poisoning the right phazer or Electric can matter more than attacking it.
  Forretress's Toxic on Zapdos made repeated Whirlwind/Thunder pressure
  self-limiting.
- Endgame loops should be reviewed by route ownership, not by whether each move
  looks active. Forretress's passive-looking turns were acceptable because the
  game state was already winning if the loop stayed intact.
