# Review: smogtours-gen3ou-805152

Replay: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen3ou-805152

Source context:
https://www.smogon.com/forums/threads/adv-swiss-i-top-8.3754448/

Format: ADV OU on Pokemon Showdown. This is useful for transferable planning
study, not GSC or romhack mechanics proof.

Length: 30 turns.

Players: Pak defeated UltiNooba.

## Why This Replay Matters

The portable lesson is late support-job preservation. UltiNooba's opening
Zapdos Agility into Celebi plan fails quickly, but the game does not become a
single failed-script story. Pak preserves Vaporeon after it has already taken
damage and later uses it for one precise endgame job: Salac Berry activation
into Baton Pass for Metagross. A low-HP support piece can still be the route
piece if its remaining job is narrow and decisive.

For Gym Leader Lab, this matters when a boss or player support Pokemon looks
"almost spent" but can still pass Speed, reset weather, block spin, absorb a
status, force a final entry, or create the one setup turn that decides the
fight.

## Phase Map

Opening pass route fails, turns 1-5:

- Zapdos uses Agility and passes to Celebi while Vaporeon uses Ice Beam twice.
- Celebi adds Calm Mind, but Salamence enters and removes it with Hidden Power.
- The first route did not fail because support is bad. It failed because the
  receiver could not survive the opposing answer map.

Midgame material and pressure trades, turns 6-16:

- Swampert uses Curse as Heracross enters, then survives long enough to remove
  Heracross with Hydro Pump.
- Tyranitar starts sand and pressures Swampert out. UltiNooba's Heracross later
  enters and begins acting as the next converter.
- Metagross removes Tyranitar on turn 13. Vaporeon then pressures Swampert and
  helps remove it by turn 16.

Heracross conversion and miss branch, turns 17-28:

- UltiNooba's Heracross removes Salamence on turn 17 and later uses Swords
  Dance against Swampert.
- Megahorn misses on turn 25, but Heracross still removes Swampert on turn 26
  and Tyranitar on turn 27.
- Vaporeon is now low enough to trigger Salac Berry after sand while still
  alive. It removes Heracross with Ice Beam on turn 28.

Late support job converts, turns 29-30:

- Vaporeon uses Baton Pass on turn 29 and passes the Speed boost to Metagross.
- Metagross outspeeds and KOs Jirachi with Earthquake on turn 30.
- Vaporeon's value was no longer "bulky Water." Its endgame job was one clean
  pass to the final converter.

## Critical Turns

Turn 5:

- Celebi faints after the opening pass route. The important review question is
  not "was Baton Pass bad?" It is whether the receiver, answer map, and backup
  route were understood before committing.

Turn 24:

- Heracross uses Swords Dance into Swampert. This creates a real conversion
  route, but it also makes the remaining priority, weather, and revenge map
  urgent. The route is strong only if the last answers are removed or outpaced.

Turn 28:

- Vaporeon's low HP becomes a resource because Salac Berry activates while it
  still has the turn structure to move next. Low HP did not make Vaporeon
  expendable; it made the Baton Pass job available.

Turn 29:

- Baton Pass is the route conversion, not a generic support move. The pass only
  matters because Metagross can immediately use the inherited Speed to remove
  the last opposing Pokemon.

## Extracted Heuristic

A support piece is spent only when it has no remaining decisive job.

Before discarding a low-HP support Pokemon, ask:

```text
remaining support job:
receiver or beneficiary:
required trigger:
can it still act before dying:
what route opens if it acts:
what route closes if it is sacrificed now:
```

If the remaining job creates a forced or strongly favored endgame, preserve the
piece even if it can no longer perform its original broad role.

## Boss-Battle Transfer

Use this against bosses with support pieces that change roles over time:

- Bugsy: Ledian may stop being a general support piece but still have one
  Reflect, Encore, or Baton Pass job for Scyther.
- Sabrina: Mr. Mime, Espeon, or Hypno may stop being bulky but still create one
  screen, Encore, Baton Pass, or status window.
- Misty: a low-HP rain setter may still reset weather for Lapras or Lanturn.
- Red: a low-HP sun setter may still make Charizard or Venusaur the next route.
- Any player team: a damaged pivot may still be the one clean entry, status
  absorber, or speed-control move needed for the final converter.

The local question is not "is this Pokemon healthy?" It is "what exact job does
it still have, and does that job decide the route?"
