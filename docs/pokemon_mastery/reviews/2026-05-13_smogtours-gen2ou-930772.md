# Review: smogtours-gen2ou-930772

Replay: https://replay.pokemonshowdown.com/smogtours-gen2ou-930772

Format: vanilla GSC OU.

Players:

- JoshSketch
- CloudyEJ

Result: CloudyEJ won after JoshSketch forfeited on turn 33.

## Team Shapes

JoshSketch revealed:

- Cloyster: Spikes / Toxic / Surf / Explosion
- Snorlax: Earthquake
- Steelix: Curse / Roar / Explosion
- Zapdos: Thunder / Hidden Power
- Gengar: Hypnosis / Ice Punch / Thunderbolt
- Tyranitar: Dynamic Punch

CloudyEJ revealed:

- Snorlax
- Cloyster: Spikes / Toxic / Surf
- Zapdos: Thunderbolt / Sleep Talk / Rest
- Tyranitar: Screech / Earthquake / Protect
- Steelix: Explosion
- one unrevealed Pokemon

## Phase Map

Opening, turns 1-6:

- Both players reach the standard Cloyster hazard position.
- CloudyEJ sets Spikes first on turn 2; JoshSketch answers with Spikes on turn
  3 after Toxic misses.
- Both Cloysters become poisoned, so the hazard setters start turning into
  finite resources rather than long-term pivots.

First route pressure, turns 7-15:

- CloudyEJ's Zapdos paralyzes JoshSketch's Snorlax on turn 7.
- CloudyEJ's Tyranitar then starts using Screech plus Earthquake pressure to
  tax JoshSketch's Steelix, Zapdos, and Cloyster pivots.
- JoshSketch cashes out Cloyster with Explosion on CloudyEJ's Cloyster on turn
  15, removing the opposing hazard setter after both sides already have Spikes.

Trade cascade, turns 16-31:

- JoshSketch sleeps Zapdos with Gengar on turn 16.
- Steelix uses Curse and Roar to force Tyranitar through Spikes on turn 21,
  then Explodes on Zapdos on turn 22.
- Gengar sleeps Tyranitar on turn 24, but Tyranitar wakes quickly and
  eventually removes Gengar with Earthquake on turn 28.
- CloudyEJ answers JoshSketch's Zapdos route by switching Steelix into Thunder
  on turn 30 and Exploding on Zapdos on turn 31.

End state, turns 32-33:

- JoshSketch has only a paralyzed Snorlax left after a critical-hit Earthquake
  removes CloudyEJ's Tyranitar.
- CloudyEJ still has Snorlax and at least one unrevealed Pokemon, so JoshSketch
  forfeits.

## Critical Turns

Turn 15:

- JoshSketch's Cloyster Explosion on opposing Cloyster is a reasonable resource
  conversion. Cloyster had set Spikes, was poisoned, and opposing Cloyster was
  still a hazard and Toxic piece. The trade simplifies the hazard war.

Turn 19:

- JoshSketch sends Tyranitar into opposing Tyranitar and clicks Dynamic Punch.
  The miss is bad luck, but the branch is also expensive: Earthquake KOs
  JoshSketch's Tyranitar immediately. This is a good reminder that inaccurate
  route-saving moves need a miss branch, especially when the user dies if the
  move fails.

Turn 22:

- Steelix Explosion on sleeping Zapdos is the first major route-conversion
  attempt. It removes a top offensive pressure piece and spends Steelix after
  Steelix has already phazed Tyranitar through Spikes.
- The important follow-up question is not only "did Explosion get value?" It is
  "which route now wins after Steelix is gone?" JoshSketch's likely answer is
  Gengar plus Zapdos pressure against Tyranitar and the remaining team.

Turns 24-28:

- Gengar sleeping Tyranitar is temporary control, not ownership. Tyranitar
  wakes quickly on both sleep cycles and Earthquake eventually removes Gengar.
- From the public state, the Gengar line needed either a faster cash-out,
  stronger damage threshold, or a preservation handoff once the wake branch
  became live.

Turns 30-31:

- CloudyEJ's Steelix switch into Thunder and immediate Explosion is the cleaner
  trade-cascade answer. JoshSketch's Zapdos looked like the main remaining
  route after Gengar fell, so removing it leaves only a paralyzed Snorlax.
- This is the same resource logic as turn 22, but with a clearer target: remove
  the opponent's last credible converter, not just a strong Pokemon.

## Recipes Reinforced

Explosion and sacrifice:

- Explosion is strongest when it removes the piece that actually converts the
  opponent's route. CloudyEJ's Steelix on Zapdos is the cleanest example in the
  game.

Temporary control must cash out:

- Sleep on Zapdos and Tyranitar changed the game, but neither status event was
  a route by itself. The sleeping target still had switch, wake, Sleep Talk, and
  countertrade branches.

Trade cascade ledger:

- After each Explosion or forced sack, immediately rewrite the answer map. The
  value of the next trade depends on the new remaining routes, not on the value
  of the previous trade.

## Romhack Transfer

- Gym Leader Lab bosses will often have fewer perfect competitive pivots, but
  the trade-cascade lesson transfers directly. If the user spends Explosion,
  Destiny Bond, Perish Song, a Focus Band proc, the only phazer, or the only
  status absorber, the next question is which boss route becomes uncovered.
- Against boss rosters, this matters most when a turn looks like a clean
  knockout. Removing the current Pokemon can still be wrong if it gives the
  ace, weather user, setup sweeper, spinner, or recovery anchor the board it
  wanted.

## New Notebook Lesson

One good trade does not make the next trade good. After every one-time resource
is spent, rebuild the live route map before spending the next one.

## Unverified

- I am not labeling turn 19 or turn 22 as blunders. The review point is the
