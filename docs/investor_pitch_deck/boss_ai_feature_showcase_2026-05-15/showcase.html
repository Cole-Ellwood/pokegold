The Fun Promise

Scary, stylish, still fair.

Ordinary boss AI still plays by the rules: public information, revealed moves, visible HP/status/boosts, boss-owned party state, and observed switch tempo. The one exception is Haki, and it is boxed up like a rare candy in a locked case.

Better battle memory

After four real public moves, the boss stops inventing a fifth hidden coverage move.

Less silly button mashing

Trainer flavor

Before / After 01

Four moves means four moves.

Before

The boss keeps worrying about a mystery fifth move.

If a player species can learn scary coverage, the old plausible-threat model could keep pricing that threat even after the active Pokemon had already shown a complete four-move set.

Surf Rest Roar Toxic Imaginary fifth slot?
After

The boss respects the revealed set and stops shadowboxing.

Public STAB and revealed moves still count. Speculative learnset and pre-evolution coverage stops only when the active species has revealed four non-tainted real moves.

Safety twist: Transform, Mimic, Metronome, Mirror Move, Sleep Talk, Sketch, and Struggle taint the reveal history, so copied or temporary moves cannot trick the boss.

Before / After 02

Hazards are now a mini-game, not a script.

Before

Qwilfish could keep stacking into a public spinner.

In a Janine-style Spikes position, adding another layer can be great. But if the active target has already shown Rapid Spin, blindly clicking more Spikes can turn into a free reset button for the player.

Spikes Rapid Spin revealed Two layers up
After

The boss asks: can this layer actually stick?

Layer-two and layer-three Spikes now discourage extra stacking when the active player Pokemon has publicly revealed Rapid Spin.

Real guardrails kept: no Rapid Spin revealed still favors the third layer; spinner-capable species without revealed Spin does not count; a fourth Spikes click remains bad.

Before / After 03

The boss learned when not to get fancy.

1

Dead setup

Before: boost because setup is cool. After: boost only if it still has value and the turn is affordable.

2

Slow status

Before: Toxic or sleep could crowd out the hard answer. After: a public KO line gets priority.

3

Early recovery

Before: heal because damaged. After: recovery waits for real route preservation windows.

4

Big boom

Before: Explosion is generic damage. After: healthy no-KO booms are bad, low-HP cash-outs are tasty.

5

Wincon pivot

Before: a key mon might stay in under public danger. After: it gets a switch-confidence boost if it lacks a KO.

Example: Bugsy's Scyther

Swords Dance is exciting, but not into obvious public fire pressure unless the boost changes the route.

Example: status into Alakazam

If the boss can remove the threat now, it should not spend the turn trying to be clever first.

Boss Personality

Leaders now get more of their own battle flavor.

This is not a bag of scripts. Personality is a close-score nudge, so a boss can feel like itself without resurrecting bad moves or overriding hard public fail gates.

Janine / Koga

Poison, Toxic, Spikes, Baton Pass, and confuse pressure get style weight when legal.

Morty

Ghost pressure, sleep, Mean Look, Curse, Destiny Bond. Spooky, but gated.

Pryce

Ice/Ground pressure plus healing and force-switch identity.

Lance

Dragon/Flying pressure, weather/safeguard flavor, and non-KO Hyper Beam restraint.

Blue / Red

Mixed endgame identity, with ace-pressure and close-score style.

Everyone

Hard gates still win. Personality makes a good choice cooler; it does not make a bad choice legal.

The Spooky Prototype

Morty's Gengar got the first Haki moment.

Normal AI

Public-only. No current-turn mind reading.

Boss move scoring still happens before player input. That means the ordinary AI cannot peek at the player's selected move, hidden party, hidden items, or hidden PP.

Rulebook mode: strong because it reads the board well.

Haki branch

One locked, audited, dramatic exception.

On Gengar's ace-first active turn, if Morty has not spent Haki, Gengar moves first, and the player locked a strong super-effective attack, the prototype may spend Haki to choose Destiny Bond.

It is quarantined in `BossAI_TryMortyHakiOracle`, refreshes move data, sets trace risk bit 3, and is explicitly allowlisted by the no-cheat audit.

Player Feel

This should feel like a better Pokemon battle.

The player is not supposed to see the code. They are supposed to feel the gym leader adapt: remembering what was shown, refusing obvious bait, protecting important pieces, and sometimes making one terrifying anime read because this is still a Pokemon game.

More "oh no" moments

The boss cashes out, pivots, or denies a greedy line when the public board says it should.

Fewer goofy turns

Setup, recovery, status, and Explosion are less likely to happen at the wrong time.

More leader identity

Morty feels spooky, Janine feels toxic, Lance feels dangerous, and the endgame gets sharper.

Bottom line: the hack gets tougher without turning into a hidden-info machine. It plays more like a prepared rival sitting across from you, not like a debugger with a battle menu.
