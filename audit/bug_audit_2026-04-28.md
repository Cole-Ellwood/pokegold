## Root cause (single event)

64 of the modified working-tree files share one of two damage signatures:

- **NUL-padded tails** (24 files): file size is unchanged or larger; content is intact through the original byte length, then a run of `0x00` bytes appears at the tail.
- **Strict-prefix truncations** (40 files): file is exactly the first *N* bytes of HEAD, with the trailing *K* bytes simply missing.

Both signatures are characteristic of a **failed file-system flush** — typically a crashed sync (OneDrive / Dropbox / iCloud), a power-loss event, or an editor that dies mid-write. They are not the result of normal edits. No commit in `git log` shows this damage; HEAD is clean.

I verified this by reading every damaged file's bytes and comparing to `git show HEAD:<file>`. For 62/64 the working tree is *byte-identical to a prefix of HEAD* (modulo NUL padding). For only 2 (`docs/manifest.md`, `docs/agent_navigation/doc_roles.md`, etc., plus a handful of py scripts) is the WT content materially edited but *also* truncated.
