Champion Lance trace/audit excerpt

Trainer data:
- `data/trainers/ai_tiers.asm`: CHAMPION/LANCE is `AI_TIER_LATE`.
- `data/trainers/parties.asm`: Gligar leads with Spikes, Steelix has Roar, and
  Gyarados has Hyper Beam.

First-turn Spikes:
- Gligar's lead Spikes uses `.ApplySpikesLayerBias`.
- At layer 1, `wBossAITurnsElapsed < 2` gives high first-scoring-turn bias
  unless `.EnemyUnderPressure` detects immediate public pressure.

Lance avoids non-KO Hyper Beam:
- Lance reaches the `.champion` role branch.
- When the current move has `EFFECT_HYPER_BEAM`, `.HasKOLine` is checked.
- If Hyper Beam does not have a KO line, the move is discouraged by the late
  tier role weight.
- Result: Gyarados' Hyper Beam is not favored as a neutral non-KO option.

Spikes plus phazing:
- Steelix's Roar has `EFFECT_FORCE_SWITCH`.
- With Spikes active, Roar receives +8 when public +2 setup or repeated-switch
  pressure is present and Lance lacks an immediate KO.

+2 setup punish:
- Public +2 Attack, Special Attack, Speed, or Evasion feeds both
  `.ApplySetupPunishBias` and the Spikes+phazing branch.
- Steelix Roar is the Lance denial move for this priority check.

Immunity pivot preference:
- Public primary-threat type comes from `BossAI_GetPrimaryThreatType`.
- Switch candidate risk now adds +3 to non-immune candidates in
  `.ApplyPrimaryThreatImmunityTieBreak`.
- Because `BossAI_RefineSwitchCandidateForPlausibleRisk` rejects replacement at
  only an equal two-point margin, +3 is the minimum narrow tuning that lets an
  immune candidate beat a neutral candidate.
- Example public case: an Electric primary threat makes Ground-type Lance
  candidates eligible as immunity pivots over merely neutral candidates.

Result:
- Lance passes first-turn Spikes, non-KO Hyper Beam avoidance, Spikes+Roar
  response, +2 setup punishment, and immunity-pivot preference by source-path
  trace.
