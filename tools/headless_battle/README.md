# Headless Battle Simulator

`tools.headless_battle` is the first text/JSON no-GUI battle-state path. It is intentionally narrow: selected move-vs-move turns, selected BP=0 single-stat stage moves, selected Dragon Dance/Calm Mind/Quiver Dance setup moves, selected Recover/Softboiled/Milk Drink self-heal moves, selected Rain Dance/Sunny Day weather setup and countdown, selected Spikes setup and Spikes entry damage, selected PoisonPowder/Poison Gas/Toxic poison-status moves, selected Thunder Wave/Stun Spore/Glare paralysis-status moves, selected EFFECT_BURN_HIT/EFFECT_POISON_HIT/EFFECT_PARALYZE_HIT damaging status secondaries, selected Absorb/Mega Drain/Leech Life/Giga Drain drain moves, selected EFFECT_SLEEP moves plus Rest and sleep action denial/wake handling, selected held PSNCUREBERRY/PRZCUREBERRY/ICE_BERRY/MINT_BERRY/MIRACLEBERRY status cures after supported status applications, selected caller-supplied Safeguard/Substitute status blockers, selected Substitute move creation and Substitute HP routing for selected damaging hits, paralyzed speed/full-paralysis checks including Fighting/Electric type-passive paralysis modifiers, repeated action maps with a max-turn cap, `auto_replace_or` for simple KO/send-out loops, ROM-mirrored wild random move choice, selected switch actions, caller-supplied replacement after KO, explicit enemy `auto_replace` choice after KO, scenario-supplied Boss AI switch-roll branching, explicit active Potion/Super Potion/Hyper Potion/Max Potion/Full Restore actions, explicit stats/species/stat-stage state, PP decrement for selected moves, supported after-hit item HP effects, modified-speed turn order and equal-speed tie RNG, basic critical-hit checks, basic move accuracy hit/miss checks, damage variation with fixed/sample/exhaustive RNG, selected damaging status secondary chance RNG, initial poison/burn/toxic residual damage after selected moves and supported item actions, selected-action `turns[]` progression with HP/RNG carryover, pre-variation HP damage through the existing damage oracle, post-score Boss AI selector replay, and executable post-score Boss AI selector turns when the scenario supplies final score bytes.

Run:

```powershell
python -m tools.headless_battle --template
python -m tools.headless_battle --scenario .local\tmp\headless_turn.json
python -m tools.headless_battle --scenario .local\tmp\headless_turn.json --json
python -m tools.headless_battle.rom_differential
python tools\audit\check_headless_battle_simulator.py
```

Default text output names source-mirrored rows as pending differential and
prints the out-of-scope boundary. Use `--json` or `--json-out` for the full
machine-readable proof surface.

Proof boundary:

- `damage_core_pre_variation` is byte-proven through the existing `tools.damage_debugger.oracle.predict_damage` and `tools.damage_debugger.clobber_smoke` gate.
- `normal_hit_fixed_rng_differential` is a named ROM-backed golden: enemy Pidgey Tackle into player Cyndaquil with fixed link RNG bytes `[255, 255]` must match the headless event for PP decrement, no-critical result, damage variation, final damage, and active HP after the ROM writes damage. This proves that selected NormalHit case only; broader move effects remain labeled pending differential until they get their own goldens.
- `basic_critical_hit_rng` mirrors `BattleCommand_Critical` for the NormalHit command order: critical check first, damage variation second, accuracy third.
- `damage_variation_rng_branching` mirrors `BattleCommand_DamageVariation`: 0/1 damage skips variation; otherwise accepted multipliers are 217..255 after the ROM's rotate-right rejection loop.
- `basic_move_accuracy_rng` mirrors the basic `BattleCommand_CheckHit` raw-byte threshold for move accuracy and always-hit moves. Thunder uses the selected Thunder path for rain bypass and sun's `50 percent + 1` threshold.
- `basic_status_residual` mirrors `ResidualDamage` for initial poison, burn, and toxic residual after a selected move when both active Pokemon remain alive.
- `basic_pp_decrement` mirrors the supported `BattleCommand_DoTurn` PP decrement: selected and executable-selector moves lose 1 PP before their supported effect path, and zero-PP selected moves are rejected until Struggle is implemented.
- `supported_after_hit_item_effects` mirrors Rocky Helmet contact recoil, Shell Bell healing, and Life Orb recoil after successful damaging hits. The isolated handler effects are byte-proven by `tools.damage_debugger.clobber_smoke`; the headless simulator applies the same HP fractions and item order for this subset.
- `explicit_active_hp_restore_items` mirrors the source HP amounts, max-HP caps, Full Restore's supported status/toxic cure, turn consumption, and actor residual after an explicit active Potion/Super Potion/Hyper Potion/Max Potion/Full Restore action. This is a sandbox scenario action: ordinary trainer-battle Pack availability, automatic trainer item dispatch, bag inventory accounting, bench targeting, revives, PP restore, X items, and item text/scripts remain out of scope.
- `selected_weather_setup_moves` supports Rain Dance and Sunny Day. These moves consume PP, set active weather, set `weather_count` to 5, decrement once at each completed turn end, and clear when the count reaches 0. Damage and Thunder accuracy already read active weather. Sandstorm damage, weather text timing beyond event records, weather healing moves, SolarBeam charge skipping, and automatic Boss AI weather-choice generation remain out of scope.
- `selected_spikes_entry_damage` supports selected Spikes moves and Spikes damage on selected switch/replacement/auto_replace entry. Spikes layers cap at three; entry damage is max HP / 8, / 6, or / 4 with a minimum of 1, and Flying-type entrants are immune. If Spikes KO the entrant while a living bench Pokemon remains, the outcome exposes `replacement_pending` for that side. The headless audit also runs `tools.damage_debugger.hazard_smoke`, which ROM-checks Spikes/Rapid Spin WRAM layer state, Spikes damage fractions, and Flying immunity; full headless turn differential remains pending. Pursuit-on-switch, Ditto Imposter activation, Rapid Spin removal in headless state, groundedness overlays outside Flying typing, and other switch-in effects remain out of scope.
- `selected_turn_order_priority_speed` is source-mirrored for priority, unequal modified speed, and non-link equal-speed ties.
- `explicit_stat_stage_state` supports caller-supplied active stat stages for attack/defense/speed/sp_attack/sp_defense in the user-facing -6..+6 range. Damage stats and turn-order speed use `data/battle/stat_multipliers.asm`; critical-hit stat handling follows `CheckDamageStatsCritical`; switch/replacement resets stages. Accuracy/evasion stages, stage-changing moves outside the selected single-stat BP=0 subset, Baton Pass/Psych Up, badge boosts, status speed, and passive stat/speed modifiers remain out of scope.
- `selected_stat_stage_only_moves` supports selected BP=0 single-stat attack/defense/speed/sp_attack/sp_defense stage moves. Self-raising moves mutate the actor; opponent-lowering moves run the basic accuracy check first; caps/floors report no effect. Accuracy/evasion stage moves, damaging secondary stat effects outside selected burn/poison/paralysis status secondaries, multi-stat chains outside the selected setup subset, Substitute/Mist blockers, and text/animation side effects remain out of scope.
- `selected_multi_stat_setup_moves` supports Dragon Dance, Calm Mind, and Quiver Dance. These moves consume PP, mutate their chained supported stat stages, report cap/no-effect through the shared stat-stage machinery, and influence later damage and turn order. Dragon Dance mirrors `bestattackup` using the simulator-supported current attack/sp_attack values; burn/passive attack modifiers, Curse, Psych Up, Baton Pass, Substitute/Mist blockers, and text/animation side effects remain out of scope.
- `selected_damaging_status_secondaries` supports selected EFFECT_BURN_HIT, EFFECT_POISON_HIT, and EFFECT_PARALYZE_HIT damaging moves. These consume secondary effect-chance RNG after the successful hit path unless the target is behind a Substitute, apply burn/poison/paralysis after damage when source no-effect checks pass, and preserve later residual/speed effects. Safeguard blocks the secondary after a successful effect-chance check. Thunder has a separate selected path because its command order differs. Flame Wheel, Sacred Fire, poison multi-hit, freeze/confusion/stat-down secondaries, and text/animation side effects remain out of scope.
- `selected_thunder_weather_order` supports Thunder's source command order for the modeled subset: critical and damage stats/calculation, Thunder weather accuracy, checkhit, secondary effect chance, STAB/pre-variation damage, then damage variation. Rain bypasses accuracy RNG; sun changes the hit threshold to `50 percent + 1`. Fly/Dig interactions, Protect, BrightPowder, accuracy/evasion stages, Lock-On, and text/animation side effects remain out of scope.
- `selected_drain_moves` supports Absorb, Mega Drain, Leech Life, and Giga Drain. These heal the user after successful active HP damage by half actual damage with a minimum of 1 and a max-HP cap, and healed HP carries into later turns. Into active Substitute, they follow `CheckHit`'s drain-substitute miss path after damage variation and before accuracy RNG. Dream Eater, Leech Seed, Big Root-style modifiers, and exact combined ordering claims with after-hit held items remain out of scope.
- `selected_sleep_status_moves` supports selected EFFECT_SLEEP moves. These consume PP, run the basic accuracy check, branch fixed/sample/exhaustive duration RNG over stored sleep counters 3..5, deny sleeping actions while decrementing the counter, and let the waking action continue. Safeguard and Substitute blockers are modeled for caller-supplied active state. Sleep Clause state, held sleep prevent items, Snore/Sleep Talk execution, Nightmare, Dream Eater, tree-mon initial sleep, and text/animation side effects remain out of scope.
- `selected_rest_move` supports Rest as a selected EFFECT_HEAL move. It fails at full HP; otherwise it restores full HP, clears supported toxic counter state, sets sleep with stored counter `REST_SLEEP_TURNS + 1`, and then uses the same later sleep action denial/wake handling. Stat recalculation side effects, Sleep Clause, held cures, text/animation side effects, and Snore/Sleep Talk execution remain out of scope.
- `selected_held_status_cures` supports immediate active held status cures after selected poison/paralysis/sleep status moves and selected damaging burn/poison/paralysis secondaries. PSNCUREBERRY cures poison/toxic, PRZCUREBERRY cures paralysis, ICE_BERRY cures burn, MINT_BERRY cures sleep, and MIRACLEBERRY cures those modeled statuses; the simulator clears status plus toxic/sleep counters and consumes the held item. Held prevent items, freeze/confusion cures, Sleep Clause clearing from held sleep cures, text/animations, stat recalculation side effects, and party item writes beyond active state remain out of scope.
- `selected_safeguard_substitute_blockers` supports caller-supplied active Safeguard and Substitute state for selected status blockers. Safeguard blocks selected poison/paralysis/sleep status moves after hit checks and before sleep-duration RNG, and blocks selected damaging burn/poison/paralysis secondaries after successful effect-chance checks. Substitute blocks selected BP=0 poison/paralysis/sleep status moves and blocks selected damaging secondary effect-chance before secondary RNG; Safeguard wins when both are active for BP=0 status moves.
- `selected_substitute_move` supports selected Substitute moves. They consume PP, fail when the user already has Substitute or would be too weak after paying `floor(max_hp / 4)`, otherwise subtract that HP cost, set active Substitute, and store `substitute_hp` for later damage routing. Trapping clear, animations/text, and volatile side effects outside active Substitute HP remain out of scope.
- `selected_substitute_hp_routing` supports caller-supplied or move-created active Substitute HP for selected non-drain damaging hits. Damage is routed into `substitute_hp`, active HP stays unchanged, the Substitute clears when damage meets or exceeds its remaining HP, and after-hit item effects are skipped because the source `DoSubstituteDamage` path resets `wCurDamage`. Selected drain moves into Substitute are treated as `CheckHit` misses before accuracy RNG. Baton Pass, multi-hit continuation details, Focus Punch/contact side effects, and text/animations remain out of scope.
- `selected_self_heal_moves` supports Recover, Softboiled, and Milk Drink. These moves consume PP, restore half max HP with a max-HP cap, report full-HP no-effect cases, and then run supported post-action residual.
- `selected_poison_status_moves` supports PoisonPowder, Poison Gas, and Toxic. These moves consume PP, run the basic accuracy check, apply poison/toxic to healthy non-Poison non-immune targets, initialize `toxic_count` at 0, and preserve later residual ticks. Safeguard, Substitute, Poison-type targets, type immunity, and already-statused targets report no effect. Held prevent items and damaging poison secondary effects outside the selected secondary subset remain out of scope.
- `selected_paralysis_status_moves` supports Thunder Wave, Stun Spore, and Glare. These moves consume PP only after the full-paralysis gate lets the actor move, run the basic accuracy check, apply paralysis to healthy non-immune targets, reduce future turn-order speed through `ApplyPrzEffectOnSpeed` including the Fighting/Electric type-passive paralysis modifiers, and branch full-paralysis action denial with `TypePassive_GetUserParalysisFailThreshold_Far` thresholds. Safeguard, Substitute, type immunity, and already-statused targets report no effect. Held prevent items, non-paralyzed Electric speed passives, and damaging paralysis secondary effects outside the selected secondary subset remain out of scope.
- `multi_turn_selected_action_progression` carries active HP/RNG state across a caller-supplied `turns[]` list and stops early on KO.
- `repeat_plan_auto_replace_or` lets a scenario use `repeat: {"max_turns": N, "actions": ...}` or top-level `max_turns` to reuse one action map until battle-over or the cap. `auto_replace_or` uses a normal fallback action while the side is alive and explicit `auto_replace` when fainted.
- `selected_switch_and_replacement` supports caller-selected `switch` actions from `bench[]` and caller-supplied `replace` actions after a KO. Switch actions run the supported residual tick for the incoming active; both entry paths reset stat stages, Focus Energy, active Substitute, and toxic counter state. Selected Spikes entry damage is handled separately.
- `auto_replacement_choice_basic_type_chart` supports explicit enemy `auto_replace` actions after KO. It mirrors the source-shaped trainer replacement preference using `data/types/type_matchups.asm`: prefer a living bench Pokemon with a super-effective move, otherwise avoid a living bench Pokemon hit super-effectively by the player's public types, otherwise use low-three-bit random rejection. Matchup overlays from Dragon's Majesty, Air Balloon, Foresight/identified state, and switch-in effects remain out of scope.
- `boss_ai_selector_from_post_score_bytes` reuses `select_from_score_bytes` for already-known final score bytes.
- `boss_ai_selector_move_execution` branches fixed/sample/exhaustive selector RNG over already-known final score bytes, records the chosen slot, validates the selected move id when available, and then executes the selected move through the same turn-order and damage path.
- `boss_ai_switch_roll` supports scenario-supplied Boss AI switch confidence, threshold, candidate bench index, and fallback stay action. It mirrors the final `BossAI_SwitchOrTryItem` confidence gate and margin-based roll: margin >=20 uses 230/256, margin >=10 uses 192/256, otherwise 141/256. Fixed/sample modes consume one switch-roll byte when confidence reaches threshold; exhaustive mode branches switch and fallback stay. Live switch candidate/confidence computation, item usage, Haki/perish/KO guard computation, loop/sack/wincon threshold bias computation, and ROM materialization remain out of scope.
- `wild_random_move_choice` mirrors the four-slot wild enemy random move loop for non-empty, nonzero-PP moves. Fixed/sample modes consume bytes until a legal slot appears; exhaustive mode branches one outcome per legal slot.
